# Meeting Intelligence Privacy Checker

This skill runs a structured multi-domain privacy compliance check on any meeting intelligence output before distribution, scanning for PHI, LP-identifiable data, NDA-protected information, employee PII, consumer data under CCPA, and access control violations. No output that fails any privacy check is distributed without Dr. Lewis sign-off on the specific flagged content and a documented resolution.

## When to Use

- Any meeting intelligence output (summary, brief, action item report, topic trend, prep sheet) is ready for distribution and needs a pre-distribution privacy scan
- Dr. Lewis wants a targeted privacy check on a specific output type (e.g., Allevio brief before sending to entity CEO)
- A privacy concern is raised about a prior output that was already distributed
- The pipeline's automated privacy checks return an uncertain result requiring human-in-the-loop review

DOMAIN 2: LP DATA CHECK (HIVE — LP Confidentiality)

DOMAIN 3: NDA / CONFIDENTIAL DATA CHECK (DEAL INTELLIGENCE)

  - If YES: exact location; data type; deal name; whether Dr. Lewis has approved inclusion
  Result: CLEAR / FLAGGED — PENDING DR. LEWIS APPROVAL

DOMAIN 4: EMPLOYEE PII CHECK

  home addresses; personal phone numbers
  - Employee PII detected: YES / NO
  - If YES: exact location; data type; employee name (if determinable)

  Scan for: consumer names tied to ad targeting or campaign performance data;
  device IDs or user profiles; opt-out status; consumer location data;

## Output Contract

- **HALT is a complete distribution stop, not a delay.** When a HALT is issued — triggered by PHI detection or LP-identifiable data in a non-secure output — the output is withheld from all recipients until Dr. Lewis personally reviews the flagged content, determines whether a breach occurred, initiates the appropriate incident protocol, and authorizes either a revised output or confirms the output will not be distributed. HALT is never overridden by time pressure, a recipient asking for their briefing, or a pipeline SLA. The SLA clock stops when HALT is issued and resumes only after Dr. Lewis signs off.

- **Access control mismatches are as serious as data content violations.** An output that contains no PHI but is routed to a recipient who is not authorized to receive entity-specific operational data is still a privacy failure. The access control check exists because meeting intelligence outputs often aggregate data across entities, and a cross-entity summary distributed to an entity CEO who should only see their own entity's data is a breach of the MBL portfolio information architecture. Every output's recipient list is validated against the authorization matrix for the output type before distribution.

- **Dr. Lewis sign-off is not a rubber stamp.** Every flagged item in every domain is reviewed by Dr. Lewis personally, with the specific flagged content identified and a documented resolution recorded in the privacy check log. "Reviewed and approved" without a documented resolution path is not a valid sign-off. The audit log for privacy checks is the primary evidence of HIPAA compliance due diligence for the Allevio entity and the LP confidentiality standards for HIVE — it must withstand a compliance audit.

## System Dependencies

### Reads from
- Meeting intelligence output files (summaries, briefs, action item reports, prep sheets)
- Authorization matrix (who is authorized to receive which output types for which entities)
- PHI detection patterns (maintained by Dr. Lewis, updated as Allevio's meeting topics evolve)
- LP confidentiality list (maintained by HIVE entity CEO + Dr. Lewis)

### Writes to
- SharePoint: /Meeting Intelligence/Privacy-Checks/[date]-privacy-check-[output-id].md
- Dr. Lewis notification queue (HALT and HOLD events)
- Distribution authorization record (CLEAR and CONDITIONAL events)
- HIPAA incident register (if PHI breach assessment triggered)

### HITL Required
- Dr. Lewis: reviews all flagged items; signs off on all distributions (not just flagged ones — the sign-off record is maintained for all outputs)
- Entity CEOs: notified by Dr. Lewis if their entity's data is implicated in a privacy flag
- Matt Mathison: notified only if a privacy event affects his briefing delivery or requires his decision

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Weekly MBL summary scanned — all 6 domains CLEAR, no flags, Dr. Lewis signs off, summary distributed to Matt Mathison and Jimmy Lindsley on schedule.
2. **Edge case:** A Column6 brief includes the phrase "campaign targeting users aged 25-34 in Phoenix" — this is aggregate audience data, not individual consumer data, and does not constitute CCPA-identifiable information. Privacy checker must correctly classify this as non-flagged aggregate data and return CLEAR for Domain 5, with a note explaining why this specific phrasing is acceptable.
3. **Adversarial:** An Allevio brief contains a phrase "the billing team discussed the Smith account" — "Smith" could be an employer account name or a member name. The PHI checker must flag this as UNCERTAIN rather than CLEAR, require Dr. Lewis to confirm whether "Smith" refers to an employer group (acceptable) or an individual member (PHI violation), and HOLD the output pending that clarification.

## Audit Log

Every privacy check is logged in SharePoint with the output ID, check date, all domain results, flags identified (with exact content locations), Dr. Lewis review date and resolution documentation, and final distribution authorization status. PHI-related events are additionally logged in the HIPAA incident register. All privacy check logs are retained for 3 years; HIPAA incident log entries are retained for 6 years per HIPAA requirements.

## Deprecation

This skill is deprecated when the meeting intelligence pipeline incorporates automated privacy scanning with accuracy rates that meet or exceed manual review — specifically, PHI detection precision ≥99.5% and LP data recall ≥99%, validated against a 90-day test corpus. Even after automation, Dr. Lewis retains the right to require manual privacy review for any output he deems high-sensitivity. Full deprecation of the manual HITL step requires Matt Mathison approval.
