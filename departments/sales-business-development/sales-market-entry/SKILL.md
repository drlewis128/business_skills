# Sales Market Entry

Plan and execute new market entry for MBL portfolio companies — assessing whether a new geography or segment is worth entering, building the go-to-market strategy, and tracking performance against break-even criteria. Market entry is one of the highest-risk strategic decisions a portfolio company makes; Dr. Lewis applies a structured assessment before any new market is entered, and presents the recommendation to Matt Mathison before commitment of material resources.

## When to Use
- Evaluating whether to expand to a new geography (e.g., Allevio to Tucson; HIVE to new Basin areas)
- Evaluating whether to enter a new buyer segment (e.g., Allevio to large employer groups >500 EE)
- A new market opportunity is presented and the CEO wants a go/no-go recommendation
- Reviewing performance of a market that was entered 6-12 months ago

## Market Entry Framework

```
STAGE 1: MARKET ASSESSMENT (before committing resources)

  Define the market:
    □ Geography: What area? (ZIP codes; county; state; DMA)
    □ Segment: What buyer? (HR Director for >500 EE; linear TV buyers migrating to CTV; HIVE adjacent basin)
    □ Size: How large is the potential customer base in this market?
      Use bottom-up methodology (sales-market-sizing) — count specific addressable targets,
        not total dollars; "Arizona has 2,400 companies with 50-500 employees in Maricopa + Pima" is
        more useful than "the AZ employer health market is $1.5B"
        
  Assess fit with current ICP:
    □ Is the new market the same ICP? (Entry with the proven model — lower risk)
    □ Is it an adjacent ICP? (Similar buyer; new context — medium risk)
    □ Is it a new ICP? (New buyer type; new buying process — high risk)
    Recommendation: Enter same ICP in a new geography before entering a new ICP in a known geography
    
  Assess operational readiness:
    □ Can our service delivery support this market?
      Allevio: Do we have enough network providers in the new area? (Clinical capacity)
      Column6: Is our inventory available in this DMA? (Supply-side readiness)
      HIVE: Do we have the legal and geological capability for this area? (Title; geologic assessment)
    □ What does it cost to serve this market? (Is the PMPM or CPM margin the same? different?)
    □ Do we have relationships in this market? (Cold vs. warm entry)
    
  Assess competitive landscape:
    □ Who is already serving this market?
    □ Why would a customer in this market choose us over the incumbent?
    □ Is the market underpenetrated (room for us) or saturated (need to displace an entrenched competitor)?
    
  Break-even analysis:
    □ What does it cost to enter this market? (Rep hire; sales travel; marketing investment)
    □ What revenue is needed to justify the cost? (Same 3× model as event ROI)
    □ What is the realistic ramp timeline? (Month 1-3: no revenue; Month 4-6: first closings)
    □ At what revenue level does the market become profitable? (Contribution margin breakeven)

STAGE 2: GO/NO-GO DECISION

  GO criteria (all should be present):
    □ Market is large enough to justify the investment (SOM > 3× the entry cost)
    □ Operational readiness is confirmed (delivery capability exists)
    □ Competitive advantage exists (we have a differentiated offer; not just a "me too")
    □ Existing business is healthy (we're not entering a new market to escape a failing core market)
    
  NO-GO criteria (any one is sufficient):
    □ We don't have delivery capability yet (network; inventory; legal/geological access)
    □ The new market dilutes focus from a core market that still has significant whitespace
    □ The ICP is materially different and we don't have time to learn the new buyer
    □ The break-even requires >12 months with a single rep — too long before validation
    □ Matt Mathison has not approved the resource commitment
    
  Conditional entry (test-and-learn approach):
    □ If uncertain: Enter the market with a pilot before committing full resources
    □ Pilot definition: 1 rep (or part-time rep); 90-day timeline; specific goal (5 discovery calls;
        2 proposals; 1 closed deal); if pilot meets goal — proceed; if not — assess and decide
    □ Pilot cost should be bounded: Set a ceiling before the pilot begins

STAGE 3: GO-TO-MARKET PLAN (if GO or Conditional)

  Rep deployment:
    □ Who will sell in this market? (Existing rep with expanded territory; new hire; Dr. Lewis leads initial)
    □ What is the ramp timeline? (See sales-onboarding-plan for new hires)
    □ What are the milestones for the first 90 days? (# of discovery calls; pipeline coverage; first close target)
    
  Prospecting plan:
    □ Use sales-bd-deal-sourcer to build the initial target list for the new market
    □ Source from market-specific channels (local chamber; DMA-specific publications; landowner associations)
    □ Set the initial list size goal: 150-200 scored leads minimum for a viable territory
    
  Operational launch:
    □ Allevio: Confirm network providers in the new geography before first sales call
        (selling a clinical benefit that can't be delivered is catastrophic for trust)
    □ Column6: Confirm inventory availability in the new DMA with the supply team
    □ HIVE: Title and geological assessment completed before approaching landowners
    
  90-day milestones:
    □ Pipeline coverage >1× (early stage; not yet at the 3× target)
    □ First discovery call completed
    □ First proposal submitted
    □ Go/no-go review at 90 days: Is the market working? Continue or exit?

ENTITY-SPECIFIC EXPANSION SCENARIOS:

  ALLEVIO — Geographic expansion:
    Next logical geography: Pima County (Tucson) — same AZ regulatory environment; same clinical
      network can potentially serve it; larger employer market than West Valley
    Operational readiness check: Does Allevio have network providers in Tucson? (This must be
      confirmed before any sales outreach begins in Pima County)
    ICP assessment: Same buyer (HR Director; 50-500 employees) — low risk
    Entry approach: Start with broker referrals in Tucson (find local benefits brokers;
      see sales-partner-channel-manager); then direct outreach to Tucson chamber members
      
  COLUMN6 — DMA expansion or segment expansion:
    DMA expansion: Adding a new market (e.g., Phoenix; Denver) to the inventory
      Operational check: Is supply available in this DMA? (SSP relationships)
    Segment expansion: From agency buying to direct brand buying
      ICP change: Brand CMOs vs. agency media buyers — different buyer; different sales process
      Higher risk: Do not attempt without a pilot and Dr. Lewis leading the first 3 calls
      
  HIVE — Basin expansion:
    New basin consideration: Would HIVE expand beyond the Uinta Basin?
    This is a major strategic decision — different geology; different title structure; different
      operator relationships; requires Matt Mathison approval and board-level discussion
    Within the Uinta Basin: Expanding to adjacent areas is lower risk (same legal and geological
      infrastructure; existing relationships may transfer)
```

## Output Contract
- Confirm operational readiness before the first sales call — Allevio cannot sell in Tucson if its network doesn't cover Tucson; Column6 cannot sell Phoenix DMA inventory if it doesn't have it; HIVE cannot approach landowners in a new basin without the title and geological assessment; Dr. Lewis verifies operational readiness as the first step in market assessment before any sales outreach is initiated; a sales call that can't be backed by operational delivery damages trust and wastes everyone's time
- The core market gets priority over expansion until it is healthy — a company that is at <70% of its core market SOM and <$1M in ARR is not ready to expand geographically; the core market still has more growth available; the sales team's attention in a new geography draws resources away from the core; Dr. Lewis flags this risk explicitly in the market entry assessment and recommends against expansion until the core market is at >80% penetration or pipeline coverage is consistently >3×
- Pilot before commitment — for any uncertain market, the right move is a bounded pilot; Dr. Lewis proposes a specific pilot definition (timeline; cost ceiling; go/no-go milestones) before committing full resources; a $15,000 pilot that fails early and cleanly is a better outcome than a $150,000 full market entry that takes 18 months to validate as a mistake
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis builds the market assessment and recommendation; CEO approves any new market entry decision; Matt Mathison approves resource commitments above $50,000 for a new market (rep hire; significant marketing investment; operational investment); Dr. Lewis does not initiate sales outreach in a new market without CEO approval; operational readiness must be confirmed by the relevant delivery team (clinical; supply; legal) before sales begins; Matt Mathison is briefed on any market entry that represents a material change to the portfolio company's strategic footprint

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio evaluating Pima County expansion → Dr. Lewis assessment: Market size: 680 employer groups in Tucson with 50-500 employees (bottom-up from AZ Dept of Labor); SOM: $11.6M potential (assuming 25% penetration at $85 PMPM); ICP fit: Same buyer (HR Director; same AZ regulatory environment); Operational readiness: 2 phone calls — Allevio clinical team confirms 8 network providers in Pima County (sufficient for 300-500 members); break-even analysis: Entry cost (6-month rep ramp at $65K base + travel): $42K; break-even at 3×: $126K closed ACV in 12 months; realistic given core market conversion rates; Competitive: 3 carriers with Tucson presence; no other MSOs identified; Recommendation: GO — conditional pilot; Dr. Lewis leads the first 5 Tucson discovery calls with the existing rep before adding headcount; if 2 proposals result, proceed to full hire; CEO approves; Matt Mathison briefed; pilot begins
2. **Edge case:** Column6 wants to enter direct brand sales (bypassing agencies) to expand its revenue base — the CEO believes brands pay more than agencies → Dr. Lewis: "This is a new ICP, not a new geography. Brand CMOs buy differently than agency media buyers — longer sales cycles; more stakeholders; budget approval in Q4 for the following year; different proposal format (they don't issue IOs; they do upfront deals). My recommendation: Don't attempt this without Dr. Lewis leading the first 10 brand conversations personally. The risk is that we dilute our agency sales focus while learning a new buying process. Let me propose a pilot: I'll take 5 warm brand introductions (companies that are already buying CTV through their agencies), run the discovery, and we'll see if the direct buying process is viable. If we get 2 signed deals in 6 months, we build a brand sales track. If not, we stick with the agency model." CEO agrees to the pilot; Dr. Lewis leads brand outreach; results measured in 6 months
3. **Adversarial:** CEO receives a tip from a friend that "Utah's Salt Lake City is a huge untapped market for Allevio" and wants to begin outreach immediately → Dr. Lewis: "Salt Lake City is an interesting idea and worth assessing — but we need to confirm several things before any outreach begins: (1) Regulatory environment: Utah has different employer health regulations than Arizona. Allevio's MSO model may need state-specific licensing. I'll have legal check this first. (2) Network: Do we have provider relationships in Utah? Zero network = zero product. (3) Core market: Allevio is at 40% of its Arizona SOM — we have significant whitespace in Arizona before we need to export. My recommendation: Complete the Utah regulatory check (2 weeks); if it's green, do a market sizing; if the SOM is compelling, propose a 90-day pilot. Don't initiate outreach until Step 1 and 2 are done. A call to a Salt Lake City HR Director that ends with 'Actually, we're not licensed in Utah yet' damages credibility." CEO agrees to follow the process; legal check initiated
