# THRIVE Culture Ops Review

The THRIVE culture ops review is the accountability mechanism that prevents culture work from becoming invisible — it holds entity CEOs accountable to their culture OKRs, evaluates the skill program's effectiveness, and gives Matt Mathison the annual view he needs to make culture investment decisions. This skill facilitates both the quarterly (45 min) and annual (90 min) review formats.

## When to Use

- Dr. Lewis is preparing for the quarterly culture ops review with entity CEOs
- Dr. Lewis and Matt Mathison are scheduling the annual THRIVE culture review
- Callie needs the quarterly review format to prepare her Playbook Manager report for the session
- An entity CEO asks for the review agenda in advance so they can prepare their culture accountability section

## THRIVE Culture Ops Review Framework

```
THRIVE CULTURE OPS REVIEW — [QUARTERLY / ANNUAL] | [QUARTER / YEAR]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

QUARTERLY REVIEW — FORMAT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Duration:   45 minutes
Attendees:  Dr. Lewis (facilitator) + all entity CEOs (preferred: together; acceptable: serial)
            Callie (last 10 min for playbook update)
Format:     Videoconference (M365 Teams) or in-person at MBL HoldCo

QUARTERLY REVIEW AGENDA
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
00:00–05:00 — OPENING: STATE OF THE CULTURE (Dr. Lewis)
  □ Portfolio culture trend (improving / stable / declining)
  □ Any active early warning flags or Integrity alerts
  □ Top-line OKR composite grade (0.0–1.0)

05:00–25:00 — ENTITY-BY-ENTITY REVIEW (5 min per entity)
  For each entity (Allevio → HIVE → Column6 → MBL HoldCo):
    □ Overall health score + pillar scores (Dr. Lewis presents)
    □ OKR grade contribution (entity CEO accountability)
    □ Top 2 culture wins (entity CEO shares — specific, observable behaviors)
    □ Top 2 culture gaps (entity CEO shares — honest; not minimized)
    □ Entity CEO accountability question: "What did you personally do this quarter
      to model THRIVE?"

25:00–35:00 — NEXT QUARTER PRIORITY (Dr. Lewis facilitates)
  □ Each entity CEO names their #1 culture priority for next quarter
  □ Agreement on any cross-entity interventions
  □ Early warning entities: specific remediation plan with owner and timeline

35:00–45:00 — CALLIE + FITZ PROGRAM UPDATE (Callie presents)
  □ Skills published this quarter (count vs. target)
  □ Skills in testing (Fitz pipeline)
  □ Any gaps in playbook coverage surfaced by entity CEOs or Dr. Lewis
  □ Next quarter skill development priorities

  Q&A: open; close with Dr. Lewis publishing meeting notes within 48 hours

QUARTERLY REVIEW — START/STOP/CONTINUE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitate a brief Start/Stop/Continue at the end of each quarterly review:
  START:    What should we begin doing in our culture program next quarter?
  STOP:     What is not working and should be discontinued or redesigned?
  CONTINUE: What is working and must be protected and sustained?

  Output: 1-3 items per category; entity CEO + Dr. Lewis input
  Dr. Lewis publishes as part of meeting notes

ANNUAL REVIEW — FORMAT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Duration:   90 minutes
Attendees:  Dr. Lewis (facilitator) + Matt Mathison (required) + entity CEOs (optional; 
            recommended for the first 45 minutes)
Callie:     First 20 min of skills section
Format:     In-person at MBL HoldCo preferred; video acceptable

ANNUAL REVIEW AGENDA
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
00:00–10:00 — YEAR-IN-REVIEW: PORTFOLIO CULTURE SUMMARY (Dr. Lewis)
  □ Annual composite OKR grade
  □ Year-over-year pillar trend (all entities)
  □ Culture-attributed voluntary turnover: cost and trend
  □ New hire 90-day integration pass rate (annual; by entity)
  □ Integrity floor breaches (count; resolution status)
  □ Top entity for the year (strongest culture performance)
  □ Most improved entity for the year

10:00–35:00 — OKR GRADES: FULL YEAR REVIEW (Dr. Lewis presents; entity CEOs defend)
  □ KR1 — Entity health targets (all entities; full year)
  □ KR2 — 90-day integration pass rate (annual)
  □ KR3 — THRIVE-attributed turnover (annual)
  □ KR4 — Playbook coverage (annual; Callie's OKR)
  □ KR5 — Assessment completion rate (annual)
  □ Entity CEO OKR contributions (each CEO's grade)

35:00–55:00 — PLAYBOOK UPDATE (Callie presents — first 20 min; then exits)
  □ Skills added this year (count vs. annual target)
  □ Skills updated in annual review
  □ Skills deprecated
  □ Next year development priorities (Callie's backlog top 5)
  □ Fitz testing program health (clearance rate; revision rate)

55:00–75:00 — NEXT YEAR CULTURE PRIORITIES (Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison)
  □ Top 3 culture priorities for next year (portfolio-wide)
  □ Entity-specific culture commitments (each entity CEO's written pledge)
  □ Budget for culture program next year (Dr. Lewis + Matt alignment)
  □ Culture investment ROI summary: turnover cost avoided; integration rate;
    Integrity floor maintenance; comparison to culture program cost

75:00–90:00 — MATT MATHISON ANNUAL CULTURE ATTESTATION
  □ Matt reviews the annual culture summary document (Dr. Lewis prepares; 2-page max)
  □ Matt provides verbal attestation: "The THRIVE culture program reflects MBL's
    operating values and I endorse its continuation and investment."
  □ Matt signs the annual culture summary (physical or digital)
  □ Document filed by Dr. Lewis as a governance artifact

ENTITY CEO CULTURE ACCOUNTABILITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Each entity CEO is accountable for:
  □ Entity OKR grade (contributes to overall program grade)
  □ Participating in every quarterly review (no proxy; no skip)
  □ Naming specific personal culture behaviors at each quarterly review
  □ Written culture priority for the next quarter (filed with Dr. Lewis)
  □ Annual written culture commitment (filed as governance artifact)

CULTURE INVESTMENT ROI SUMMARY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Prepared by Dr. Lewis for annual review:
  Program cost (Dr. Lewis time + Callie + Fitz + survey + external costs): $________
  Controllable voluntary turnover avoided (estimated): _______ FTEs
  Replacement cost avoided: $________
  90-day integration pass rate value (faster productive contribution): $________
  Integrity floor maintenance (regulatory risk avoided; estimated): $________
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Estimated ROI: [ratio] | Culture program justified: [Y/N with narrative]
```

## Output Contract

- Entity CEO attendance is mandatory at every quarterly review — a proxy or a written update submitted in advance is not a substitute, because the accountability function of the quarterly review depends on each CEO personally defending their culture OKR grade and naming their own behavioral contributions to culture; the format is designed to make culture accountability personal, not reportable.

- The annual Matt Mathison attestation is a governance document, not a ceremonial formality — it creates a documented record that MBL's managing partner has reviewed the culture program and endorsed its continuation, which is material in any M&A due diligence where a buyer is assessing the sustainability of the portfolio's operating culture.

- The culture investment ROI summary is prepared for every annual review because culture program budgets compete with operational investments at the portfolio level, and Dr. Lewis must be prepared to defend the program in value terms, not just in values terms; without an ROI summary, the culture program is vulnerable to budget cuts in any year when portfolio financial performance is under pressure.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Q3 quarterly review. All four entity CEOs attend. Dr. Lewis opens with portfolio trend: Improving (+0.2 from Q2). No active flags. Entity-by-entity: HIVE CEO names two specific behaviors (weekly field accountability standup reinstated; Hustle score up 0.4). Allevio CEO acknowledges Enjoy gap honestly (clinical burnout visible in Q3 data). Next quarter priority: Allevio workload audit. Callie reports 3 skills published; 1 in Fitz testing. Start: recognition delegation to managers. Stop: using the same pulse survey questions in all-hands. Continue: CEO personal culture behavior sharing. Notes published within 24 hours.

2. **Edge case:** One entity CEO cannot attend Q2 review due to a board conflict. Dr. Lewis does not accept a written update as attendance. Session proceeds with three CEOs. Absent CEO is given a 20-minute makeup session with Dr. Lewis within 5 business days. Makeup session is logged as partial attendance; noted in meeting record. Dr. Lewis follows up on the absent CEO's culture priority in writing.

3. **Adversarial:** Annual review: Matt Mathison reviews the culture investment ROI summary and challenges the turnover cost avoidance estimate as too optimistic (program claims $340K avoided; Matt believes the attribution is speculative). Dr. Lewis adjusts the methodology in real-time — uses a conservative 50% attribution rate instead of 100%. Revised estimate: $170K. Matt finds this credible. Attestation is provided. The methodology debate is documented in the meeting notes and the conservative approach is adopted for future annual summaries.

## Audit Log

Quarterly review records (agenda, attendees, entity scorecards, Start/Stop/Continue, meeting notes) are retained for five years. Annual review records (full agenda, OKR grades, Callie + Fitz output, ROI summary, Matt Mathison attestation) are retained as permanent governance artifacts. Entity CEO attendance records are maintained separately for accountability tracking. Any quarterly where an entity CEO was absent is flagged in the annual audit log.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed annually as part of the process it describes — the annual THRIVE culture ops review is the natural forum for assessing whether the review format itself needs revision. It is deprecated when MBL adopts a formal governance cadence that supersedes this review structure, or when the portfolio grows to a size that requires a different facilitation model. The Matt Mathison annual attestation requirement is foundational to MBL's culture governance and must be preserved in any successor format.
