# Thrive Truth Framework

Build and apply the Truth pillar of MBL's THRIVE culture — the standard that problems are surfaced early, status is reported accurately, and disagreement is expressed directly rather than withheld. Truth is the first THRIVE value because without it, all other values are performative: a team that Hustles but reports inaccurate status, or Enjoys their work but avoids difficult feedback, will eventually create a crisis that honesty could have prevented. This skill operationalizes Truth across reporting, meetings, feedback, and escalation.

## When to Use
- Building or reinforcing truth-telling standards for a team or entity
- Coaching a manager whose team has a status-reporting accuracy problem
- Establishing escalation culture at a new entity or after a leadership change
- Training new leaders on MBL's expectation of radical honesty

## Truth Framework

```
WHAT TRUTH MEANS AT MBL:
  Truth = delivering accurate information even when it's uncomfortable
  Truth is NOT: brutal criticism with no empathy
  Truth IS: BLUF-first, complete, accurate, timely, actionable
  
  The MBL truth standard: "What would you tell Matt Mathison?"
  If a status report would change if Matt were reading it — the report isn't truthful.

TRUTH IN STATUS REPORTING:
  Red-Yellow-Green must be accurate:
    🟢 Green: On track; no action needed; will hit target on time within budget
    🟡 Yellow: Risk identified; mitigation in progress; escalation may be needed
    🔴 Red: Off track; needs action now; escalate to entity CEO or Dr. Lewis
    
  Common violations (coaching targets):
    Optimism bias: "We'll figure it out" reported as 🟢 when recovery plan doesn't exist
    Optics management: Report 🟡 when it's 🔴 because you don't want to escalate
    Hiding the ball: Report only the good news; leave out the bad news
    Deadline drift: Keep saying "almost done" without escalating that the deadline is at risk
    
  Standard: Any status change from 🟢 to 🔴 in a single reporting period = automatic escalation.
  You don't go from green to red — the yellow was hidden.

TRUTH IN MEETINGS:
  What truth looks like in meetings:
    "I think that approach won't work because [specific reason]"
    "I disagree with [name]'s assessment. Here's why."
    "I don't have the data to support that right now — I'll get it to you by [day]."
    "I made a mistake on [X]. Here's what I'm doing to fix it."
    
  What truth violations look like in meetings:
    Silence when you disagree ("I'll discuss it after the meeting" = not truth)
    "That sounds great" when you have serious concerns
    Hedging every statement with so many qualifiers that it communicates nothing
    Waiting to be asked rather than volunteering critical information
    
  Meeting truth standard: If you leave a meeting thinking something important wasn't said, say it.
  If you didn't say it in the meeting, send it in writing within 2 hours.

TRUTH IN FEEDBACK:
  Direct feedback model (COIN):
    Context: When [specific situation]
    Observation: I observed [specific behavior — not interpretation]
    Impact: The impact was [specific outcome]
    Next: What I need from you going forward is [specific behavioral change]
    
  Feedback truth violations:
    The compliment sandwich: Positive → real feedback → positive → person hears only the positives
    Vague feedback: "You need to communicate better" (what specifically?)
    Delayed feedback: Waiting for the annual review to raise an issue that happened in Q1
    Feedback triangulation: Telling others about the problem instead of the person

TRUTH IN ESCALATION:
  Escalation is not weakness — it is a truth-telling act
  When to escalate (immediately):
    🔴 status — always
    Resource constraint that will cause a miss — before the miss
    Decision that exceeds your authority — before you make it
    Information that changes the picture for your manager — as soon as you have it
    
ENTITY CONTEXT:
  Allevio: Truth in clinical reporting = patient safety and HIPAA compliance dimension
    Accurate billing and clinical documentation is a legal truth requirement
  HIVE: Truth in production reporting = LP credibility and investor trust
    WTI trigger reporting must be accurate and timely (Matt Mathison needs real numbers)
  Column6: Truth in campaign reporting = client trust and agency relationships
    Impression/click/conversion numbers must be accurate — no rounding to make results look better
  MBL HoldCo: Truth in financial reporting = QuickBooks and Bill.com data is the system of record
    No "adjustment" to make numbers look better to Matt — report what the data shows
```

## Output Contract
- Truth standards must be modeled from the top before they will be adopted below — if an entity CEO hedges in team meetings, softens bad news, or avoids escalating problems to Dr. Lewis, the team will observe and mirror that behavior; culture follows leadership; the entity CEO must visibly demonstrate truth-telling: surface their own mistakes, share negative news proactively, escalate to Dr. Lewis before Dr. Lewis asks; Dr. Lewis models the same standard with Matt Mathison; the truth culture is built from the top down — it cannot be trained into the organization if the top isn't living it; this is the first coaching focus when an entity has a Truth pillar deficit
- Accurate status reporting is a system that must be built, not just demanded — most status reporting problems are not integrity failures; they are structural problems (the system rewards optimism; there is no clear yellow-red definition; the leader reacts badly to bad news); before labeling a status reporting problem as a Truth violation, audit the system: has the entity defined what yellow and red actually mean? Does the leader respond supportively to red flags or does the messenger get punished? Is there a clear escalation path that people feel safe using? Fix the system first, then coach the individual; if the structural issues are resolved and specific individuals still hide the ball, address it as a THRIVE Integrity issue
- HITL required: new entity Truth framework → entity CEO reviews and approves before rollout; status reporting redesign → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis alignment before new standards implemented; significant Truth violation (hiding the ball on a major issue) → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis conversation; pattern of Truth violations → THRIVE audit triggered (thrive-culture-audit); coaching plan for Truth deficit → entity CEO awareness; Allevio clinical reporting truth issues → Dr. Lewis + entity CEO immediately (HIPAA dimension)

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** HIVE operations team has a status reporting accuracy problem — production targets consistently reported as 🟢 until the week of the miss, then sudden 🔴. Dr. Lewis implements Truth framework: defines 🟡 for HIVE as "producing below target for 2+ consecutive weeks" and 🔴 as "more than 15% below monthly target." Trains entity CEO on how to receive yellow flags without punishment response. 60 days later: yellow flags are surfacing 3-4 weeks before misses. Entity CEO adjusts resource allocation proactively. One miss prevented. Truth culture beginning to embed.
2. **Edge case:** A Column6 account manager is reporting campaign performance to a client and adjusting the numbers "slightly" to smooth out weekly volatility ("it averages out over the month"). Dr. Lewis: "This is a Truth violation regardless of whether the monthly numbers are accurate. Clients make decisions based on weekly numbers. Smoothing is misleading. The standard is: report actuals every week, with context if there's weekly volatility. 'This week underperformed due to [specific reason]; monthly pace is still on track' is honest and reassuring. Adjusting numbers without disclosure is not. This is addressed directly with the account manager today."
3. **Adversarial:** Entity CEO pushes back: "I can't have my team in full panic every time something goes yellow — constant escalation kills momentum." Dr. Lewis: "Agreed — the goal isn't panic, it's early visibility. Here's the distinction: yellow doesn't require action from you, it requires awareness. Red requires action. The system only works if you respond to yellow with 'thanks for the heads up, what do you need from me?' rather than 'why is this yellow?' If the team is afraid to go yellow, they'll skip it and go straight from green to red — which is exactly the problem you're trying to avoid. Let's talk about how you respond when you receive yellow status, because that behavior is what makes or breaks the system."
