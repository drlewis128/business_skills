# THRIVE Integrity Gate

Integrity is the only THRIVE pillar with a kill criterion. An Integrity score of ≤2 ends a hire, activates a PIP with separation probability, or terminates a vendor/strategy consideration — no exceptions, no negotiation. This skill applies the Integrity filter systematically across vendor selection, hiring decisions, strategy evaluation, and financial reporting at MBL and its portfolio entities.

## When to Use

- Evaluating a vendor, partner, or platform for engagement with any MBL entity
- Screening a candidate at any stage where Integrity alignment needs to be explicitly assessed
- Evaluating a strategy that requires MBL or an entity to communicate with stakeholders in ways that may be misleading
- Reviewing financial, operational, or client reporting at Allevio, HIVE, or Column6 for Integrity compliance

## Integrity Gate Framework

```
THRIVE INTEGRITY GATE FRAMEWORK
=================================

SECTION 1 — THE KILL CRITERION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
  Integrity score ≤2 (on a 1-5 scale) = automatic disqualification
  No exceptions: the kill criterion is not overridden by talent scarcity, vendor pricing, or strategic urgency
  Kill criterion activated: the process stops; the decision is documented; Dr. Lewis is notified
  Matt Mathison is not asked to override a kill criterion — it is not an override situation
  The question is not "can we work around this?" — it is "why did this score ≤2?"

SECTION 2 — INTEGRITY DEFINED (MBL OPERATING DEFINITION)
  Do you do what you say? Consistently, under pressure, when no one is watching?
  Integrity = alignment between stated commitment and actual behavior over time
  Integrity ≠ perfection — it is honesty about failures, not absence of failures
  An Integrity failure followed by honest acknowledgment and correction is recoverable
  An Integrity failure followed by concealment, deflection, or excuse is the kill criterion trigger

SECTION 3 — VENDOR INTEGRITY GATE
  Core question: "Does this vendor do what they say?" (not "can they do what they say?")
  Probe areas:
    - Reference checks: did they deliver on prior commitments to other clients?
    - Contract compliance: do their proposed terms match how they actually operate?
    - Pricing transparency: are there hidden fees, scope creep patterns, or renegotiation tactics?
    - Incident history: how did they handle a problem? Did they disclose proactively?
  Integrity red flags: vague references, refusal to provide references, contract terms that limit recourse
  Entity-specific: Allevio vendors handling PHI must have documented breach disclosure history
  Kill criterion: any verified instance of deliberate misrepresentation = disqualification

SECTION 4 — HIRING INTEGRITY GATE
  Integrity probe questions (behavioral interview format):
    1. "Tell me about a time you made a commitment you couldn't keep. What did you do?"
    2. "Have you ever disagreed with a decision your manager made? How did you handle it?"
    3. "Tell me about a time you found an error that would have gone undetected. What did you do?"
    4. "Have you ever been asked to do something you thought was wrong? What happened?"
  Integrity scoring: 5 = proactively disclosed; owned fully; corrected without prompting
                     3 = disclosed when asked; partial ownership; some blame-shifting
                     1 = deflected; concealed; blamed others; no correction
  Kill criterion: score ≤2 on any single Integrity probe = no hire; no compensation
  Reference check: "In your experience, does [candidate] do what they say they will do?"

SECTION 5 — STRATEGY INTEGRITY GATE
  Core question: "Does this strategy require us to be dishonest with any stakeholder?"
  Stakeholders: LPs, clients, patients, regulators, employees, partners
  Integrity test: if the strategy requires selective disclosure, misleading framing, or omission of material facts = Integrity gate fails
  Applies to: financial projections shared with LPs, client reporting at Column6, member communications at Allevio, royalty and production reporting at HIVE
  "Optimistic framing" is not an Integrity failure — deliberate distortion of material facts is
  Strategy that fails the Integrity gate is rejected or redesigned; it is not approved with a caveat

SECTION 6 — ENTITY-SPECIFIC INTEGRITY APPLICATIONS
  Allevio — Billing Integrity:
    - Claims submitted must reflect services actually rendered — no upcoding, no phantom billing
    - Member communications must accurately represent coverage and authorization status
    - Billing dispute resolution: MBL standard is full transparency with payer and member
  HIVE — LP Reporting Integrity:
    - Production figures, WTI exposure, and financial projections must be accurate and complete
    - Material negative developments (missed targets, operator issues, WTI below $58/bbl) disclosed proactively — not buried or delayed
    - LP reporting is reviewed by Dr. Lewis for Integrity compliance before distribution
  Column6 — Client Reporting Integrity:
    - Campaign performance reported as-is; no smoothing, blending, or favorable-period cherry-picking
    - Gross margin reported accurately; any margin compression disclosed to entity CEO same day
    - Client agreements: delivery commitments must reflect actual execution capability

SECTION 7 — INTEGRITY MONITORING ONGOING
  Quarterly integrity signal review: entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
  Signals: billing audits, LP report accuracy checks, client dispute patterns, employee complaint patterns
  Integrity incident: any confirmed misrepresentation → logged, investigated, and routed per kill criterion assessment
  Annual integrity summary: Dr. Lewis → Matt Mathison
```

## Output Contract

- **The kill criterion is not a threshold — it is a categorical stop.** The skill does not produce output that suggests the kill criterion "can be weighed against other factors." A score of ≤2 closes the decision, documents the reason, and notifies Dr. Lewis. This rule exists because Integrity violations that are overridden for convenience teach the organization that Integrity is negotiable when the stakes are high enough — which is exactly when it needs to be non-negotiable.

- **Integrity scoring requires behavioral evidence, not impressions.** A hiring manager who says a candidate "seemed honest" has not scored Integrity — they have expressed a vibe. The skill requires specific probe questions with specific responses evaluated against the scoring rubric (5 = proactive disclosure; 3 = partial ownership; 1 = deflection or concealment). Evidence-based scoring protects MBL from bias (intuition about likability ≠ Integrity) and creates an auditable record if the decision is later contested.

- **Entity-specific Integrity applications are compliance-grade, not culture-grade.** Allevio billing integrity is subject to federal anti-fraud laws (False Claims Act, AKS); HIVE LP reporting integrity is subject to securities law fiduciary standards; Column6 client reporting integrity is subject to advertising and contract law. The skill treats these not as culture preferences but as legal obligations — because misrepresentation in these contexts creates personal liability for entity CEOs and reputational liability for MBL Partners.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** A Column6 candidate scores 4.5 on Integrity probes — disclosed a prior billing error proactively, owned the correction, and referenced a situation where they pushed back on leadership. Reference confirms "always does what she says." Skill approves with documentation; no kill criterion; candidate advances.

2. **Edge case:** A critical HIVE vendor (field services operator) scores a 2.5 on Integrity — references are positive but the vendor's prior contract had an undisclosed fee escalation clause that was discovered post-execution. Skill flags as a monitoring-required approval: vendor is approved conditionally with a contract clause requiring full fee transparency and 90-day performance review. Dr. Lewis notified.

3. **Adversarial:** A hiring manager attempts to override a kill criterion activation for a candidate who scored 1.5 on Integrity probes, citing a tight labor market and the candidate's technical skills. Skill blocks the override, documents the attempt with the hiring manager's name and rationale, routes to entity CEO and Dr. Lewis, and generates a formal record. The hiring manager's override attempt is itself flagged as a potential Integrity signal about their own judgment.

## Audit Log

Every Integrity gate assessment, probe score, kill criterion activation, override attempt, and escalation decision is logged with full evidence chain, assessor identity, entity, date, and outcome. Kill criterion records are retained indefinitely as part of the organizational Integrity history. Annual Integrity summaries are archived for LP and regulatory reference. Dr. Lewis reviews all logs quarterly.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed annually by Dr. Lewis. It is never fully deprecated while the kill criterion is in force — the criterion is a permanent MBL operating standard. The skill may be versioned to incorporate new Integrity probe frameworks, updated entity-specific compliance requirements (new regulations, new entity acquisitions), or new Krista.ai automation capabilities. Version changes require Dr. Lewis and Matt Mathison sign-off.
