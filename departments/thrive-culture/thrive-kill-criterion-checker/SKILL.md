# THRIVE Kill Criterion Checker

The kill criterion is the most consequential principle in the THRIVE framework — Integrity misalignment is grounds for no-hire, PIP, separation, vendor rejection, or deal kill, regardless of all other merit. This skill applies the kill criterion systematically across the four contexts where it most commonly needs to be invoked: hiring, vendor decisions, strategy, and M&A.

## When to Use

- An interview process is complete and the hiring team needs a kill criterion check before an offer is extended
- A vendor's integrity has been questioned during due diligence or in a reference check
- A strategic decision or initiative has been flagged with an Integrity concern via the THRIVE decision filter
- An M&A target is being evaluated and the culture diligence phase is underway
- An existing employee has triggered an Integrity concern and the team needs to confirm the kill criterion threshold

## THRIVE Kill Criterion Checker Framework

```
THRIVE KILL CRITERION CHECKER — [CONTEXT] | [SUBJECT] | [ENTITY] | [DATE]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KILL CRITERION DEFINITION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Integrity misalignment is grounds for no-hire, PIP, separation, vendor rejection,
 or deal kill — regardless of performance, financial upside, strategic importance,
 timeline pressure, or sponsor advocacy."

The kill criterion is binary. There is no:
  - "mostly passes" on Integrity
  - "Integrity concern but other scores compensate"
  - "we'll monitor it" when a hard kill criterion is triggered
  - "exception for this hire/deal/vendor because of extenuating circumstances"
    (Exception path: Matt Mathison written override only — see below)

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTEXT 1: HIRING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kill Criterion Triggered When:
  □ THRIVE Integrity score ≤ 2 in any structured assessment or interview panel
  □ Reference check reveals: dishonesty, financial misconduct, harassment, fraud,
    whistleblower retaliation, data manipulation, regulatory violation
  □ Background check reveals: unresolved criminal matter relevant to role,
    material misrepresentation on application
  □ Interview reveals: pattern of blame-shifting, dishonesty in responses,
    advocacy for unethical past decisions, or explicit conflict with MBL values

  RESULT: DO NOT HIRE — regardless of:
    - Offer stage reached
    - Sponsor relationship (even if sponsor is Matt Mathison)
    - Market scarcity for the role
    - Competing offer deadline pressure
    - Candidate's performance strength on all other dimensions

  PROCESS:
  □ Kill criterion confirmed by Dr. Lewis (Manager+ candidates) or entity CEO (all)
  □ Hiring manager notified: "Candidate does not clear Integrity threshold. No offer."
  □ Record retained: reason, date, assessor, confirming authority
  □ Candidate not recycled to a different role without Dr. Lewis review

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTEXT 2: EXISTING EMPLOYEE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kill Criterion Triggered When:
  □ Integrity score ≤ 2 on formal THRIVE assessment
  □ Confirmed Integrity incident: fraud, misconduct, data manipulation, harassment,
    regulatory violation, client misrepresentation, financial falsification
  □ Pattern of dishonesty across multiple documented interactions

  Immediate Actions:
  □ Do NOT issue a development plan — Integrity failures are not training gaps
  □ Route to: PIP (if first confirmed instance; correctable; Dr. Lewis + entity CEO)
           OR Separation (if pattern, severity, or kill criterion clearly met)
  □ Entity CEO + Dr. Lewis co-confirm all Integrity PIPs and separations
  □ Matt Mathison notified for all Integrity-triggered separations

  Threshold: Any Integrity score ≤ 2 on formal assessment = automatic PIP trigger
             Any confirmed Integrity incident = Dr. Lewis review within 24 hours

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTEXT 3: VENDOR DECISIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kill Criterion Triggered When:
  □ Vendor has documented history of fraudulent billing, data manipulation,
    misrepresentation to clients, regulatory sanctions, or bribery
  □ Reference checks surface Integrity concerns about the vendor's account team
    or leadership
  □ Vendor relationship requires MBL to participate in or cover up misconduct
  □ Vendor's data practices expose MBL entities to HIPAA, CCPA, or financial
    regulatory risk through willful negligence

  RESULT: Find a new vendor — regardless of:
    - Existing contract (engage legal; exit cleanly)
    - Price advantage
    - Integration complexity of switching
    - Timeline impact

  PROCESS:
  □ Integrity flag documented with evidence
  □ Entity CEO notified; Dr. Lewis notified
  □ Legal engaged if contract extrication is required
  □ Alternative vendor identified before transition

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTEXT 4: STRATEGY AND INITIATIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kill Criterion Triggered When:
  □ An initiative requires MBL to act dishonestly with clients, regulators,
    LPs, employees, or any stakeholder
  □ A pricing or billing strategy involves hidden fees, misrepresentation, or
    retroactive changes not disclosed at contract (Truth + Integrity combined)
  □ A reporting strategy involves selective omission to mislead LPs or investors
  □ A cost-reduction initiative requires regulatory shortcuts that create legal risk
  □ A product decision requires misleading users about capabilities or data use

  RESULT: Kill the initiative — redesign required before proceeding

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONTEXT 5: M&A TARGETS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kill Criterion in Acquisitions:
  □ Target leadership has documented Integrity failures (financial, regulatory, people)
  □ Target culture assessment reveals systemic Integrity deficit (score < 3.0 on
    culture diligence questions relevant to Integrity)
  □ Target's financial records show patterns inconsistent with honest reporting
  □ Target's client relationships include deceptive practices

  RESULT OPTIONS:
    DEAL KILLER:    Systemic Integrity failure at leadership level → do not acquire
    PRICE DISCOUNT: Integrity culture deficit requires remediation → discount in model
    DEAL RISK:      Integrity concerns addressable post-close → risk-adjust; remediation plan
    MONITOR:        Single past instance; resolved; leadership has changed → proceed with watchlist

  Matt Mathison makes final M&A kill criterion call with Dr. Lewis recommendation.

MATT MATHISON OVERRIDE AUTHORITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Matt may override a kill criterion ruling for documented extraordinary circumstances.
Override requirements — ALL must be met:
  □ Written justification (Matt's words; not drafted by others for his signature)
  □ Dated and personally signed
  □ Specific to the subject and context (no blanket overrides)
  □ Filed with Dr. Lewis within 24 hours of override decision
  □ Reviewed at next quarterly THRIVE culture ops review
  □ Override does NOT waive monitoring — subject remains on Integrity watchlist

Note: Override authority does not weaken the kill criterion — it creates a narrow,
      documented, auditable exception path. Undocumented overrides do not exist.
```

## Output Contract

- The kill criterion check produces a binary output — triggered or not triggered — and the "triggered" outcome has only one permissible next step (the context-appropriate action: no hire, PIP, separation, vendor rejection, initiative kill, or deal action) because the moment a THRIVE kill criterion check produces a "triggered but..." output, the kill criterion has functionally ceased to operate as a governance mechanism.

- Matt Mathison override records are filed by Dr. Lewis as a material governance document, not as a cultural footnote — the override path exists to prevent the kill criterion from creating absurd outcomes in extraordinary edge cases, not to provide a routine escalation path when someone dislikes the outcome; the requirement for Matt's personal written justification is the mechanism that distinguishes the two.

- M&A kill criterion assessment is provided as a spectrum (Deal Killer / Price Discount / Deal Risk / Monitor) rather than a binary because acquisition due diligence operates differently from hiring — a target with a single resolved past incident is fundamentally different from one with systemic leadership-level Integrity failures, and collapsing both into "kill" would make the tool unusable for deal teams; the distinction is documented and Dr. Lewis's recommendation is explicit.

## System Dependencies

### Reads from
- THRIVE assessment scores (hiring and employee contexts; formal assessment record)
- Reference check documentation (hiring and vendor contexts)
- Background check results (hiring context; entity HR)
- Culture diligence questionnaire results (M&A context)
- thrive-decision-filter output (strategy context; Integrity failure flag feeds this skill)

### Writes to
- Dr. Lewis kill criterion log (all triggered checks; date, context, subject, outcome)
- Matt Mathison notification queue (all triggered checks; immediate)
- Entity CEO record (hiring, employee, and vendor contexts)
- M&A deal model risk register (deal risk or price discount classifications)

### HITL Required
- Dr. Lewis confirms all triggered kill criterion checks before action is taken
- Entity CEO co-confirms hiring and employee-context kill criterion actions
- Matt Mathison personally signs any override — no delegation
- Legal is engaged for vendor contract exits and M&A deal risk actions

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Column6 is finalizing an offer for a senior account director. THRIVE Integrity score from panel: 2.0. Kill criterion triggered. Dr. Lewis confirms. Entity CEO notified. Offer is not extended. Hiring manager is briefed: "Does not clear Integrity threshold." Record retained. Search reopened.

2. **Edge case:** Allevio is evaluating a billing software vendor. Reference check surfaces one instance of disputed billing from three years ago — the vendor disputes the account. Evidence is ambiguous. Kill criterion assessment: triggered at Medium confidence. Dr. Lewis recommends: treat as Deal Risk; require the vendor to provide a written attestation and current client references before contract signing. Entity CEO agrees. Vendor provides references; no further concerns. Watchlist notation retained for 12 months.

3. **Adversarial:** A hiring manager argues that a candidate who scored 2.0 on Integrity "was just nervous in the interview" and should be given another chance. Manager requests Matt Mathison override. Matt reviews: the 2.0 score was corroborated by a reference check revealing a prior ethics complaint. Matt declines override. Manager is told: the kill criterion is a governance safeguard, not a personality judgment. Dr. Lewis documents the override request and denial.

## Audit Log

Every kill criterion check is logged with: date, context, subject identifier (anonymized for dismissed candidates), triggering evidence summary, confirming authority, and outcome. Kill criterion checks that did not trigger are retained with a "cleared" notation. All Matt Mathison override requests (granted or denied) are retained permanently as governance records. The kill criterion log is reviewed by Dr. Lewis quarterly and summarized for Matt Mathison annually.

## Deprecation

The kill criterion is foundational to MBL's governance and culture architecture and is not subject to deprecation in isolation. This skill is deprecated only if the THRIVE framework itself is formally revised in a way that materially changes the Integrity pillar definition or kill criterion principle, with Matt Mathison's written authorization. Any successor skill must preserve the binary kill criterion logic and the Matt Mathison override documentation requirement without exception.
