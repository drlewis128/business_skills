# THRIVE Culture OKR Tracker

OKRs give the THRIVE culture program the same measurement discipline that MBL applies to revenue and operations — without quantified objectives, culture work is invisible in a portfolio review and cannot compete for resource allocation. This skill tracks, grades, and reports the THRIVE culture program OKRs quarterly and annually.

## When to Use

- Dr. Lewis is preparing the quarterly OKR grade submission for the culture program
- Callie needs her playbook development OKR baseline confirmed before a new quarter begins
- An entity CEO wants to understand their culture OKR contribution and standing
- Matt Mathison is reviewing the annual culture program performance and needs the OKR summary

## Output Contract

- Integrity is weighted separately from overall entity health in KR1 and is never averaged away — a portfolio average of 4.2 that includes one entity at 3.7 would mask the weakest Integrity score, and the kill criterion principle requires that Integrity failures be surfaced individually, not diluted in composite math.

- OKR grades are computed using the published calculation formula, not Dr. Lewis's qualitative judgment — the formulas exist to make the grading auditable and consistent across quarters, and any deviation from formula-based grading must be documented with justification and approved by Matt Mathison.

- Callie's and Fitz's individual OKRs are tracked separately from program-level KRs because their output (playbook development and skills testing) is a leading indicator of future culture health scores; a lagging composite grade that doesn't surface upstream bottlenecks in content production is not useful for program management.

## System Dependencies

### Reads from
- thrive-pulse-survey-builder quarterly scores (KR1 data)
- thrive-30-60-90-culture integration pass rate tracker (KR2 data; Callie maintains)
- thrive-exit-interview-analyzer departure classification (KR3 data)
- THRIVE skill registry (KR4 data; Callie maintains)
- Assessment completion log (KR5 data; Dr. Lewis program log)

### Writes to
- Quarterly OKR report (filed with Dr. Lewis program records)
- Matt Mathison quarterly culture brief (OKR grades included as section)
- Annual OKR summary (year-end; Matt Mathison attestation)
- Entity CEO OKR contribution records (entity-level accountability)

### HITL Required
- Dr. Lewis reviews all KR data inputs before grades are published
- Any KR grade below 0.5 triggers a written root cause analysis before the report is finalized
- Matt Mathison signs the annual OKR summary

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Q3 grading. KR1: all entities ≥3.6; all Integrity ≥4.1 — grade 1.0. KR2: 22 new hires; 20 Full Integration — 91% — grade 1.0. KR3: 8 voluntary departures; 0 THRIVE-attributed — grade 1.0. KR4: 40/40 skills published — grade 1.0. KR5: 18 scheduled assessments; 17 on time — 94% — grade 1.0. Composite: 1.0. Matt Mathison brief reflects strongest quarter on record.

2. **Edge case:** Callie publishes 2 skills in Q2 against a target of 3. Fitz tested both. Callie's OKR grade: 0.67. Root cause: one skill was deprioritized due to Column6 rollout support. Dr. Lewis documents the trade-off and adjusts Q3 target to 4 (to catch up). No program-level OKR impact because KR4 is assessed annually.

3. **Adversarial:** KR3 spikes in Q4: 4 THRIVE-attributed departures out of 18 total (22% rate). Grade: 0.0. Dr. Lewis pulls exit interview data — all four from Allevio; all citing Respect and Enjoy failures. Root cause: a new manager hired in Q2 with a Coaching Needed verdict who was never put on a formal coaching plan. Entity CEO put on notice. Matt Mathison briefed within 24 hours. KR3 root cause analysis attached to annual OKR summary.

## Audit Log

OKR records are maintained quarterly and retained for five years as program management artifacts. Grade calculations are documented with formula inputs, not just final scores, so that any quarter's grade can be independently verified. Entity CEO contribution grades are shared with the relevant CEO at each quarter-end. Callie and Fitz receive their individual OKR grades from Dr. Lewis within 10 business days of quarter-end.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed annually during the THRIVE culture ops review. OKR targets are reviewed and may be adjusted annually based on prior-year actuals and portfolio growth. The skill is deprecated if MBL adopts a formal OKR platform that supersedes this tracking structure, or if the THRIVE culture program is restructured materially. The five core KRs and the Integrity-floor treatment are considered foundational and must be preserved in any successor OKR framework.
