# THRIVE Manager Effectiveness

Managers are the transmission layer between THRIVE values and team behavior. A manager who scores well on individual execution but poorly on Respect or Truth is actively damaging the culture even while hitting their numbers. This skill provides a structured six-dimension effectiveness assessment for every manager across MBL entities — with a quarterly cadence, entity CEO accountability for the results, and clear escalation when a manager is putting culture at risk.

## When to Use

- An entity CEO needs to conduct quarterly manager effectiveness reviews for their direct reports
- A manager's team is underperforming on culture metrics (low eNPS, high turnover, engagement decline) and the root cause needs to be assessed at the manager level
- Dr. Lewis is reviewing an entity CEO's management of their manager population for the portfolio ops review
- A 360 feedback cycle has completed and the results need to be synthesized into a structured effectiveness summary

## Manager Effectiveness Framework

```
THRIVE MANAGER EFFECTIVENESS FRAMEWORK
========================================

SECTION 1 — WHY MANAGER EFFECTIVENESS DETERMINES CULTURE OUTCOMES
  Team members don't leave companies — they leave managers
  A manager's THRIVE alignment is the single largest predictor of their team's THRIVE alignment
  No culture program, policy, or training can overcome a consistently poor manager
  The entity CEO is accountable for their managers' THRIVE scores — not as blame, but as culture ownership
  The escalation chain: manager behavior → entity CEO responsibility → Dr. Lewis oversight → Matt Mathison awareness

SECTION 2 — DIMENSION 1: TRUTH (Do they surface problems?)
  Definition: the manager consistently surfaces bad news, problems, and risks proactively — to their team and upward
  HIGH indicator: problems surface before they are crises; the manager delivers hard feedback; they tell the entity CEO what they need to hear, not what they want to hear
  LOW indicator: entity CEO is surprised by problems from this manager's team; manager smooths over bad news; team doesn't give honest feedback because the manager doesn't model it
  Assessment question: "When was the last time this manager proactively surfaced a problem before being asked? What happened when a team member brought them bad news?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does your manager give you honest feedback even when it's uncomfortable?"

SECTION 3 — DIMENSION 2: HUSTLE (Do they drive pace?)
  Definition: the manager sets and maintains a pace that matches MBL's execution standard (≥95% delivery rate on their team)
  HIGH indicator: team delivery rate ≥95%; manager self-initiates; they escalate before misses; their 1:1s produce commitments, not conversations
  LOW indicator: team delivery rate <85%; manager accepts excuses without accountability; their team's pace matches their own slow cadence; misses are recounted, not prevented
  Assessment question: "What is this manager's team delivery rate? What is the manager's own delivery rate? Are they the same?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does your manager hold you and themselves to a high standard of follow-through?"

SECTION 4 — DIMENSION 3: RESPECT (Do they treat reports well?)
  Definition: the manager treats every team member with dignity — in feedback, in meetings, in disagreement, and in absence
  HIGH indicator: team 360 Respect scores are high; no HR complaints; team members feel heard; disagreement is safe
  LOW indicator: HR complaints from team members; direct reports feel dismissed or talked over; feedback delivery is punitive or dismissive; different treatment of different-level reports
  Assessment question: "Do this manager's direct reports feel respected by them? Are there any HR complaints on record?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does your manager treat you with respect, even in disagreement or under pressure?"

SECTION 5 — DIMENSION 4: INTEGRITY (Do they do what they say?)
  Definition: the manager's commitments to their team and to the entity CEO are kept — consistently and without prompting
  HIGH indicator: commitments are documented and closed; team members can rely on what the manager says; no gap between stated and actual behavior
  LOW indicator: team members have stopped believing the manager's commitments; the manager makes promises in 1:1s that don't materialize; entity CEO has experienced commitment failures
  Assessment question: "Do team members rely on this manager's commitments? What does the manager's own commitment-keeping rate look like?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does your manager do what they say they will do?"
  Kill criterion: Integrity dimension ≤2 from direct reports = immediate entity CEO + Dr. Lewis review

SECTION 6 — DIMENSION 5: VALUE (Do they produce outcomes?)
  Definition: the manager creates measurable value through their own output, not just through directing others — and ensures their team's work produces quantifiable outcomes
  HIGH indicator: the manager has a personal value contribution statement; their team's output is outcome-oriented; they can articulate the ROI of their team's work
  LOW indicator: the manager's value is defined entirely by their team's activity; they cannot state their own value contribution in dollar terms; team reports activity, not outcomes
  Assessment question: "What specific, quantifiable value has this manager created personally in the last quarter? What is their team's output-to-outcome ratio?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does your manager help you understand how your work creates value for the organization?"

SECTION 7 — DIMENSION 6: ENJOY (Do they build a culture people want to be in?)
  Definition: the manager creates an environment where top performers choose to stay and new talent wants to join
  HIGH indicator: team eNPS is high; voluntary turnover is low; recognition is given regularly; team members express positive energy; people want to work for this manager
  LOW indicator: high voluntary turnover on the team; eNPS is low; team energy is flat or declining; no recognition given; top performers are quietly considering exits
  Assessment question: "Is voluntary turnover from this manager's team higher or lower than the entity average? What is their manager eNPS score?"
  Direct report 360 question: "Does working for your manager make you more likely to stay at this company?"

SECTION 8 — MANAGER EFFECTIVENESS QUARTERLY REVIEW
  Owner: entity CEO reviews all direct report managers quarterly
  Input: self-assessment + entity CEO rating + 360 from direct reports (semi-annual)
  Scoring: 1-5 per dimension; average score + lowest dimension identify
  Minimum standard: all dimensions ≥3; any dimension at 1-2 = active development plan
  Integrity at ≤2: entity CEO + Dr. Lewis review within 5 business days
  High performer (all 5s): recognized by entity CEO + flagged for succession planning
  Entity CEO accountability: entity CEOs who have ≥2 managers at <3 on any dimension = entity CEO coaching conversation with Dr. Lewis

SECTION 9 — ENTITY CEO REVIEW OF ALL MANAGERS
  Entity CEO owns the manager population's THRIVE health — not just individual reviews
  Quarterly: entity CEO presents manager effectiveness distribution to Dr. Lewis
  Red flag: any manager at ≤2 on any dimension; manager population average <3 on any dimension
  Pattern: if the same dimension is low across multiple managers = entity CEO leadership modeling gap (they are setting the floor)
  Action: entity CEO + Dr. Lewis define remediation — targeted coaching, replacement, or entity CEO behavior change
```

## Output Contract

- **The six-dimension assessment is required in full — not abbreviated to the two or three dimensions where the manager struggles.** Managers who score well on Value and Hustle but poorly on Respect are often protected by their output numbers — "they're results-oriented" becomes the excuse for poor people management. The full six-dimension assessment prevents dimension substitution: a manager cannot use a high Value score to offset a low Respect score. Each dimension is a separate and non-negotiable requirement of the THRIVE leadership standard.

- **Team health metrics (eNPS, turnover, delivery rate) are included in the assessment because they are the outcome of the manager's effectiveness, not a separate data point.** A manager who self-assesses at 4 on Enjoy but whose team has a 22% voluntary turnover rate and an eNPS of 8 is either self-deceived or dishonest about their impact. The team health data is the ground truth against which the dimension scores are validated — and when there is a material gap between the manager's self-assessment and their team's health data, the team data prevails.

- **Entity CEO accountability for the manager population is made explicit because entity CEOs frequently focus on their own THRIVE scores while allowing their manager population to drift.** An entity CEO who has two managers with Respect scores below 3 has a culture problem they own — both because those managers were hired or promoted under their watch and because the entity CEO's own modeling likely set the context in which those managers developed. The entity CEO-level accountability clause in the quarterly review forces the connection between entity CEO leadership and manager population health.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Column6 quarterly manager review. Three managers assessed. Manager A: all dimensions 4+, team eNPS 38, delivery rate 96% — flagged for succession planning. Manager B: Enjoy dimension at 2.3 (team eNPS 14, voluntary turnover 22%) — development plan generated for Enjoy, 60-day check-in set, entity CEO coached on conversation. Manager C: all dimensions ≥3, solid trajectory. Entity CEO receives the distribution summary; Dr. Lewis reviews Manager B's Enjoy flag.

2. **Edge case:** A HIVE field manager has been in the role for only 60 days — too short for a meaningful 360 from direct reports. Skill adapts the assessment: entity CEO rating and self-assessment are used; 360 is scheduled for the 90-day mark; team health data is used as a leading indicator in place of 360 scores. The assessment is labeled PROVISIONAL with a 90-day full assessment date set.

3. **Adversarial:** An entity CEO submits all manager scores as 4+ across all dimensions with no development priorities — in a quarter where the entity has 19% voluntary turnover and two HR complaints from the same manager's team. Skill flags the inconsistency between submitted scores and team health data, identifies the manager with HR complaints as a Respect and Integrity concern despite the entity CEO's 4+ ratings, and routes the discrepancy to Dr. Lewis with the full data set for independent review.
