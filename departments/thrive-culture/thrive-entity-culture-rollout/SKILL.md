# THRIVE Entity Culture Rollout

Rolling out THRIVE to a portfolio company is a sequenced change management process, not an announcement event — the sequence matters as much as the content. This skill structures the rollout from entity CEO alignment through the first 90-day monitoring window, with built-in resistance handling and accountability checkpoints for Matt Mathison.

## When to Use

- MBL acquires a new portfolio company and THRIVE needs to be introduced to its leadership and workforce
- An existing portfolio company is undergoing significant leadership change and needs a THRIVE re-establishment
- Dr. Lewis is planning the rollout calendar for a new entity and needs the sequencing framework
- Callie needs to coordinate the skills testing phase of a rollout with Fitz Bowen

## THRIVE Entity Culture Rollout Framework

```
THRIVE ENTITY CULTURE ROLLOUT PLAN — [ENTITY] | ENTITY CEO: [NAME] | START: [DATE]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ROLLOUT SEQUENCE (mandatory order — do not compress or reorder)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 1: Entity CEO Alignment          (Week 1–2)
Phase 2: Leadership Team Workshop      (Week 3–4)
Phase 3: All-Hands THRIVE Launch       (Week 5–6)
Phase 4: Ongoing Systems Integration   (Week 7–12)
Phase 5: 90-Day Post-Rollout Review    (Day 90)

PHASE 1 — ENTITY CEO ALIGNMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitated by: Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison (in person preferred)
Duration: 90-minute working session

Agenda:
  □ THRIVE explained: what it is, why MBL uses it, what it demands from a CEO
  □ CEO's current culture reality: where does this entity sit today?
  □ CEO commitment ceremony: CEO articulates THRIVE in their own words
  □ Kill criterion conversation: CEO confirms understanding + willingness to enforce
  □ Rollout co-ownership: CEO is face of THRIVE, not MBL HoldCo
  □ Agree rollout timeline and CEO's visible role in each phase

CEO Commitment Output (written; CEO signs):
  "I understand the THRIVE values and my accountability to model and enforce them.
   I commit to leading this rollout and holding my leadership team to the same standard."

  Signed: __________________________ Date: __________

PHASE 2 — LEADERSHIP TEAM THRIVE WORKSHOP
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitated by: Dr. Lewis (primary); Callie supports content delivery
Duration: Half-day (4 hours) | All direct reports to entity CEO

Agenda:
  □ THRIVE deep-dive (90 min): each pillar; real examples from this entity's industry
  □ Integrity kill criterion (30 min): non-negotiable; role of each leader
  □ THRIVE assessment preview (30 min): how leaders and their teams will be evaluated
  □ Entity playbook co-creation (60 min): leaders write entity-specific THRIVE examples
  □ Resistance surfacing (30 min): "What about THRIVE feels hardest for THIS entity?"

Playbook Output: Draft entity THRIVE playbook (Callie refines post-workshop)

Resistance Surfacing Protocol:
  Common objections and responses:
  "This isn't how we've always done it." → "This is how MBL entities operate.
   The question is whether this is the culture you want to build."
  "We already have values." → "THRIVE replaces prior values frameworks.
   Prior values that align, survive. Those that don't, don't."
  "Our industry doesn't work this way." → "Name the pillar that doesn't apply here.
   We'll work through it — but the pillars are not optional."

PHASE 3 — ALL-HANDS THRIVE LAUNCH
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Led by: Entity CEO (Dr. Lewis attends; does NOT lead — CEO owns this moment)
Format: All-hands meeting (in-person preferred; video if distributed)
Duration: 60–90 minutes

Agenda:
  □ CEO opens: personal statement of THRIVE commitment (not a reading — genuine)
  □ THRIVE overview: each pillar; what it means for everyone in the room
  □ Entity-specific examples: what THRIVE looks like in our jobs here
  □ Kill criterion: stated plainly; this is not optional; Integrity is the floor
  □ What happens next: assessment cadence, onboarding integration, recognition
  □ Q&A: open; CEO fields questions; Dr. Lewis is available but secondary

Communication Artifacts (Callie prepares):
  □ THRIVE one-pager (entity-branded)
  □ THRIVE pillar cards (desk reference)
  □ FAQ document (common questions + answers)

PHASE 4 — ONGOING SYSTEMS INTEGRATION (Weeks 7–12)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  □ THRIVE integrated into new hire onboarding (thrive-culture-onboarding activated)
  □ THRIVE integrated into performance review cycle
  □ First pulse survey deployed at Week 10 (thrive-pulse-survey-builder)
  □ Manager THRIVE coaching sessions scheduled (all people managers)
  □ Recognition system activated (thrive-recognition-program)

PHASE 5 — 90-DAY POST-ROLLOUT REVIEW
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitated by: Dr. Lewis | Attendees: Entity CEO + Matt Mathison
Format: 60-minute review

Review Agenda:
  □ Pulse survey results (Week 10 data): entity baseline by pillar
  □ Rollout completion: all four phases complete? (Y/N)
  □ Early signals: what behaviors have changed? what hasn't?
  □ Top 2 wins from rollout
  □ Top 2 gaps or concerns
  □ Resistance patterns observed: which leaders or teams are lagging?
  □ Next 90-day priorities

Matt Mathison Output: 1-page BLUF summary (Dr. Lewis writes; CEO approves)
```

## Output Contract

- Entity CEO alignment (Phase 1) must be completed before any other phase begins — launching a leadership workshop or all-hands before the CEO has personally committed to THRIVE creates a situation where employees receive a values message that their CEO has not genuinely endorsed, which is the primary predictor of rollout failure and culture theater.

- The all-hands launch must be led by the entity CEO, not by Dr. Lewis or MBL HoldCo — THRIVE becomes a portfolio mandate delivered from headquarters rather than an owned entity culture when the CEO is not the visible leader of the launch, and this distinction materially affects whether frontline employees internalize the values or treat them as compliance.

- The 90-day post-rollout review with Matt Mathison is required for every entity rollout and must include pulse survey data as a quantitative baseline — without a numeric starting point, subsequent quarterly culture health comparisons have no foundation, and the entire monitoring program loses its early warning capability.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** MBL acquires a new MSO and begins Allevio THRIVE rollout. Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison conduct Phase 1 with the incoming CEO in Week 1. CEO signs commitment. Phase 2 workshop surfaces one resistance point from the COO ("our clinical teams won't engage with values language"). Dr. Lewis adapts framing to patient-safety and HIPAA context. All-hands in Week 5 led by CEO; strong Q&A engagement. Pulse survey at Week 10: T3.8 H3.5 R4.1 I4.0 V3.2 E3.7. 90-day review completed; top gap is Value (output clarity). Next quarter priority: Value pillar training.

2. **Edge case:** HIVE is a small team (12 people); no formal all-hands tradition. Phase 3 adapted to a field crew meeting at the basin and a separate office team session. Same content; different format. CEO presents at both. Completion logged; both sessions count as Phase 3 completion. Pulse survey done via SMS link given limited M365 access in the field.

3. **Adversarial:** Column6 leadership workshop surfaces a VP who explicitly argues that Integrity standards would "limit creative client billing." Dr. Lewis names the statement as a kill criterion concern in the session. Entity CEO is put on notice. VP is not removed immediately but is flagged in the rollout record. Dr. Lewis recommends the entity CEO address the VP directly within 30 days. Matt Mathison is briefed. VP's Day 90 assessment is prioritized for Dr. Lewis direct review.

## Audit Log

All rollout phase completions are timestamped and attributed. Entity CEO commitment signatures are scanned and attached to the entity rollout record maintained by Dr. Lewis. Resistance patterns and how they were resolved are logged as structured notes — not discarded — because they inform future rollout planning for similar entities. The 90-day review summary and Matt Mathison BLUF are retained as program artifacts.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed each time MBL completes a new entity rollout and lessons are incorporated. It is formally reviewed during the annual THRIVE culture ops review. It is deprecated when MBL adopts a successor rollout methodology that supersedes this sequencing, or when the portfolio grows beyond four entities and a scaled rollout infrastructure is required. Callie and Fitz must pilot any successor rollout protocol with a test cohort before the prior version is retired.
