# THRIVE Wellbeing Monitor

Wellbeing is the Enjoy pillar's operational floor. MBL's Hustle standard is high — but Hustle that destroys the people executing it is not sustainable, not strategic, and not aligned with THRIVE. This skill monitors the signals that distinguish productive urgency from unsustainable overload, and equips managers and entity CEOs to intervene before burnout becomes a turnover event.

## When to Use

- A team or individual is showing burnout signals: declining output, energy drop, missed commitments, after-hours communication spikes
- An entity CEO wants a quarterly wellbeing assessment before the engagement survey to act proactively
- Allevio clinical staff are showing signs of compassion fatigue or documentation overload
- HIVE field workers are exhibiting fatigue-related safety signals or attendance patterns

## Wellbeing Monitor Framework

```
THRIVE WELLBEING MONITOR FRAMEWORK
=====================================

SECTION 1 — WELLBEING DEFINED IN THE THRIVE CONTEXT
  Wellbeing = the sustainable capacity to bring Hustle, Truth, Respect, Integrity, Value, and Enjoy to work consistently
  Wellbeing is not about comfort — it is about sustainability of high performance
  The question is not "are people comfortable?" — it is "can they sustain this pace without breaking?"
  MBL does not lower the bar for wellbeing; it ensures the bar is sustainable, not just achievable once

SECTION 2 — WELLBEING INDICATORS (OBSERVABLE SIGNALS)
  Energy:
    Positive: visible engagement, proactive communication, initiative
    Warning: flat affect, slow response, reduced meeting contribution, loss of humor
  Boundaries:
    Healthy: consistent working hours with clear start/end; delegates appropriately; takes PTO
    Warning: 24/7 availability; never delegates; PTO refusal or cancellation pattern
  PTO Usage:
    Target: ≥80% of earned PTO used annually per individual
    Warning: <50% usage = either fear culture or inability to disconnect — both are concerns
    Risk: high PTO accrual + voluntary turnover spike = employees banking PTO before exit
  After-Hours Communication:
    Healthy: occasional; emergency-only after-hours; no expectation of response outside hours
    Warning: regular after-hours messages from manager to team (creates implicit pressure)
    Red: late-night patterns persist >3 weeks; team members respond to after-hours messages within minutes (fear signal)
  Output Trajectory:
    Healthy: stable or improving output over 4-week rolling window
    Warning: output declining despite same hours or more hours (efficiency drop = overload signal)

SECTION 3 — BURNOUT EARLY WARNING SIGNALS
  Level 1 (Watch): 1-2 wellbeing indicators in warning territory; no output impact yet
  Level 2 (Caution): 3+ indicators in warning; output beginning to decline; energy visibly lower
  Level 3 (Alert): Output declining materially; individual reports feeling overwhelmed; 1:1 intervention required
  Level 4 (Crisis): Individual unable to perform; immediate manager + HR involvement; EAP referral; workload relief
  Rule: Level 3+ requires manager-initiated intervention within 48 hours of identification

SECTION 4 — PRODUCTIVE HUSTLE VS. UNSUSTAINABLE OVERLOAD
  PRODUCTIVE HUSTLE: high output; high energy; sustainable pace; individual is energized by the work
  UNSUSTAINABLE OVERLOAD: high output for 4-6 weeks → declining output; declining energy; increasing error rate
  The test: can this pace be maintained for 12 months without degrading output? If no = overload
  Hustle is an asset to be invested, not a resource to be depleted
  Managers who mistake overload for hustle burn out their highest performers first — the people most willing to push

SECTION 5 — ENTITY-SPECIFIC WELLBEING CONTEXT
  Allevio — Clinical Burnout:
    - Clinical and care staff face compassion fatigue, documentation overload, and payer friction stress
    - Burnout signals in clinical roles: increased clinical errors, patient interaction quality decline, documentation delays
    - Intervention: workload assessment + care team coverage review + EAP + clinical peer support
    - HIPAA: wellbeing monitoring does not access clinical records; behavioral observation only
  HIVE — Field Fatigue:
    - Extended field rotations, physical demands, and isolation create fatigue risk
    - Fatigue signals: attendance irregularities, equipment handling changes, communication withdrawal
    - Intervention: rotation schedule review + field safety check-in + supervisor wellbeing conversation
    - Safety link: fatigued field workers = elevated OSHA incident risk; wellbeing is a safety metric
  Column6 — Agency Pace:
    - Campaign deadline pressure, creative cycles, and client demand create agency-style overload
    - Signals: late creative submissions, client communication delays, team conflict spike
    - Intervention: campaign portfolio rationalization + scope management + deadline buffer review
  MBL HoldCo:
    - AI strategy velocity and multi-entity oversight create cognitive overload risk for small team
    - Dr. Lewis wellbeing: Matt Mathison monitors; no automated tracking applies at this level

SECTION 6 — MANAGER CHECK-IN PROTOCOL
  Weekly 1:1 wellbeing check-in question (rotate): "What's your energy like this week?" / "Are you getting time to recharge?" / "Is there anything on your plate we should move?"
  Manager does not interpret silence as wellbeing — ask the question directly and listen
  If warning signals observed: "I've noticed [specific behavior change]. I want to check in — how are you doing, really?"
  Manager obligation: if Level 3+ signals are present, they do not wait for the next 1:1 — they act

SECTION 7 — EAP AWARENESS AND REFERRAL PROTOCOL
  EAP (Employee Assistance Program) resources are communicated at onboarding and quarterly
  EAP referral is offered (not mandated) at Level 3+; mandatory at Level 4 with HR involvement
  Manager script: "We have an EAP that provides confidential support. I'd like to share it with you. You don't have to use it, but I want you to know it's there."
  EAP use is confidential — managers do not receive utilization data; they only know they made the referral
```

## Output Contract

- **Wellbeing signals are monitored at the behavioral level, not the emotional level, because managers are not therapists and clinical diagnosis is not the goal.** The skill tracks observable indicators (output trajectory, PTO usage, after-hours patterns) rather than asking managers to assess emotional states — which they are not qualified to assess and which creates privacy and liability risk. Behavioral observation gives managers actionable, observable data; the conversation that follows opens the door to emotional honesty on the individual's terms.

- **The distinction between productive Hustle and unsustainable overload is explicitly enforced because confusing them destroys MBL's best people.** High performers are most willing to push through overload signals — and most likely to stay silent about it until they break. If managers interpret overload as hustle, they will continue to load the people who are already overloaded while undertasking the people who are not. The 12-month sustainability test is the operational distinction that forces the manager to ask whether the pace is an investment or a depletion.

- **EAP referral is offered, not mandated, because the wellbeing intervention must preserve the individual's autonomy and dignity.** Mandating EAP use conflates a support resource with a disciplinary action, which causes people to hide wellbeing problems rather than address them. The offer creates awareness and removes the barrier; the individual's choice to use it is what makes it therapeutic rather than coercive. The manager's obligation is to make the offer clearly and sincerely — not to control the outcome.

- HRIS: PTO usage rates, accrual balances, absenteeism records, tenure

### Writes to
- Entity CEO wellbeing report: team-level signals with individual flags (entity CEO view)
- Manager coaching agenda: wellbeing check-in scripts and intervention actions
- HR system: Level 4 crisis documentation; EAP referral records
- Dr. Lewis queue: ALERT and CRISIS level events for entity CEO support

### HITL Required
- Entity CEO reviews all ALERT and CRISIS designations before Dr. Lewis is notified
- HR is looped in at Level 4 before any formal intervention is communicated to the individual
- Dr. Lewis reviews entity CEO wellbeing assessments when the entity CEO is the manager of concern

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** An Allevio care coordinator shows Level 2 wellbeing signals — PTO usage at 42% of earned (YTD), output declining 15% over 4 weeks, and two missed documentation deadlines. Manager is coached to conduct a direct wellbeing check-in using the script, offer workload relief on two non-critical items, and log the conversation. EAP is mentioned but not pushed. Two-week check-in scheduled.

2. **Edge case:** HIVE production peaks during a drilling period, and the whole field team is working extended hours for a defined 3-week window. Skill classifies this as a bounded Hustle event (not overload) based on defined start/end date and manager's awareness, but sets a 4-week post-peak wellbeing check to ensure recovery has occurred — flagging that if output hasn't returned to baseline within 2 weeks of the peak ending, the assessment should be revisited.

3. **Adversarial:** A Column6 manager is the primary source of after-hours communication, sending 11 PM Slack messages to direct reports and expecting same-evening responses. Skill identifies the manager's behavior (not the team's) as the wellbeing risk, routes the finding to the entity CEO — noting this is a manager behavior change required, not a team intervention — and documents the pattern for the entity CEO's coaching conversation with the manager.

## Audit Log

All wellbeing assessments, burnout level designations, manager intervention records, EAP referral events, and escalation decisions are logged with entity, date, reviewer, and data sources. Individual-level wellbeing data is access-controlled: entity CEO for their entity; HR for Level 4 events; Dr. Lewis for ALERT+ events. Logs are retained for 24 months. No clinical or PHI data is included in any wellbeing log at any entity.

Deprecation requires Dr. Lewis confirmation that the automated system covers entity-specific contexts (Allevio clinical, HIVE field, Column6 agency) and does not create any HIPAA or employment law compliance risk.
