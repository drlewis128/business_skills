# THRIVE 30-60-90 Culture Integration

The first 90 days are the window in which a new hire decides whether the stated culture and the lived culture are the same thing — and whether they belong here. This skill manages the structured culture integration milestone sequence for all new hires, producing a Day 90 verdict that is actionable, documented, and owned by the entity leadership chain.

## When to Use

- Any new hire at any MBL entity reaches the 30-, 60-, or 90-day mark and needs a structured culture integration check
- Dr. Lewis is conducting a spot-check audit on entity-level 90-day integration pass rates
- An entity CEO requests a summary of new hire culture integration status across their team
- A manager escalates a new hire concern before Day 90 and needs the framework to structure the conversation

## THRIVE 30-60-90 Culture Integration Framework

```
THRIVE 30-60-90 CULTURE INTEGRATION — [ENTITY] | [EMPLOYEE] | [ROLE] | START: [DATE]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DAY 30 MILESTONE — UNDERSTAND
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Confirm the new hire understands THRIVE — not just memorized the acronym.
Facilitator: Direct manager
Format: 15-min structured 1:1

Questions:
  □ In your own words, what does THRIVE mean at [entity]?
  □ Which pillar feels most natural to you so far?
  □ Which pillar is most challenging or unclear?
  □ Have you seen any behaviors that felt off or surprising?
  □ Do you have any questions about the Integrity kill criterion?

Culture Carrier Check:
  □ Have they met their THRIVE buddy? (Y/N)
  □ Has buddy reported any concerns? (Y/N — if Y, manager follows up)

Day 30 Output:
  □ Brief written summary filed with Callie's tracking system
  □ Flag to Dr. Lewis if: Integrity question raised / Respect concern surfaced /
    employee expresses confusion about values expectations after orientation

DAY 60 MILESTONE — DEMONSTRATE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Confirm the new hire is showing THRIVE behaviors, not just describing them.
Facilitator: Direct manager + peer input (informal; 2-3 peers)
Format: Manager observation + peer check-ins; brief written summary

Behavioral Observations (manager scores 1-5):
  TRUTH     — Communicates honestly; flags problems early; doesn't hide issues
  HUSTLE    — Follows through without reminders; owns outcomes; doesn't make excuses
  RESPECT   — Positive interactions; respectful of peers, clients, stakeholders
  INTEGRITY — No compliance flags; handles sensitive info appropriately
  VALUE     — Output is contributing meaningfully; not just busy
  ENJOY     — Engaged; collaborative; not a drain on team energy

Thresholds:
  Any pillar score ≤ 2.0 → coaching conversation required before Day 70
  Integrity score ≤ 2.0  → immediate escalation to Dr. Lewis; do not wait

Peer Input (informal):
  Ask 2-3 peers: "Is [name] someone you'd go to with a problem? Is there anything
  about how they work that feels off to you?" — qualitative; no formal scoring.

Day 60 Output:
  □ Pillar scores documented (manager)
  □ Peer input summary (1-2 sentences)
  □ Any coaching actions recorded (what, by when)
  □ Threshold flags transmitted to Dr. Lewis

DAY 90 MILESTONE — ASSESS + VERDICT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Goal: Render a documented, actionable culture integration verdict.
Facilitator: Manager (primary) + entity CEO review for any verdict below Full Integration
Format: Self-assessment (employee) + Manager assessment; verdicts compared

SELF-ASSESSMENT (Employee completes before manager conversation):
  Rate yourself 1-5 on each THRIVE pillar in your role:
  T ___ H ___ R ___ I ___ V ___ E ___
  Where do you feel strong? Where do you feel you have room to grow?
  Is there anything about the culture here that has been hard to adapt to?

MANAGER ASSESSMENT:
  Rate the employee 1-5 on each THRIVE pillar based on observed behaviors:
  T ___ H ___ R ___ I ___ V ___ E ___
  Average: _____ (≥3.5 = integration threshold; I must be ≥3.5 independently)

VERDICT OPTIONS:
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ FULL INTEGRATION     │ All pillars ≥ 3.5; I ≥ 3.5; no open flags  │
  │                      │ Employee is cleared; record closed           │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ COACHING NEEDED      │ 1-2 pillars between 2.5–3.4; I ≥ 3.5       │
  │                      │ 60-day coaching plan activated               │
  │                      │ Reassessment at Day 150                     │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ VALUES MISALIGNMENT  │ Any pillar < 2.5; OR Integrity < 3.5        │
  │                      │ Route to PIP (thrive-pip-builder)           │
  │                      │ Entity CEO + Dr. Lewis co-sign decision      │
  │                      │ Matt Mathison notified                       │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

ENTITY CEO REVIEW:
  Required for: Any verdict below Full Integration
  CEO confirms: verdict, coaching plan (if applicable), or separation path
  Signature: __________________________ Date: __________

DR. LEWIS OVERSIGHT:
  Reviews all Manager+ verdicts directly
  Reviews 20% random sample of all other verdicts quarterly
  Any Values Misalignment verdict across any entity → Dr. Lewis informed same day
```

## Output Contract

- The Day 90 verdict must be rendered as one of three explicit options — no partial language like "mostly integrated" or "making progress" is acceptable in the formal record because ambiguous verdicts cannot be tracked at portfolio level and often allow culture drift to continue unaddressed for another quarter.

- Self-assessment scores are compared to manager scores before the verdict conversation, and material discrepancies (2+ point gap on any pillar) are discussed explicitly in the Day 90 meeting — employees who score themselves significantly higher or lower than their manager on Integrity are a specific risk pattern that Dr. Lewis tracks at the portfolio level.

- Entity CEO signature is required for any verdict below Full Integration and must be captured before the record is filed as complete — a coaching plan or separation decision without CEO co-ownership frequently stalls, and the signature requirement creates accountability at the entity leadership level, not just the manager level.

## System Dependencies

### Writes to

- Callie's 90-day integration pass rate tracker (entity-level; quarterly roll-up)

### HITL Required
- Manager conducts all three milestone conversations directly — no delegation to HR
- Entity CEO co-signs all non-Full Integration verdicts before record is filed
- Dr. Lewis reviews all Manager+ Day 90 verdicts; spot-checks 20% of all others

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** New sales coordinator at Column6 reaches Day 90. Self-assessment: T4 H4 R5 I4 V3 E4. Manager assessment: T4 H3 R4 I4 V3 E4. Averages align; minor Hustle gap noted but above threshold. Verdict: Full Integration. Record closed. No CEO review required. Logged in Callie's pass rate tracker.

2. **Edge case:** New care coordinator at Allevio reaches Day 90. Manager assessment: I2.0 (one PHI handling incident at Day 55; corrected). Self-assessment: I4. Two-point gap triggers explicit discussion. PHI incident reviewed — employee demonstrated corrective behavior post-incident. Verdict upgraded from initial concern to Coaching Needed with HIPAA compliance refresh required. Entity CEO reviews and signs. Dr. Lewis notified.

3. **Adversarial:** Manager at HIVE submits Day 90 with all scores listed as 4.0 across the board for a new hire who received two peer Respect complaints in Month 2. Dr. Lewis spot-check selects this record. Inconsistency flagged — manager's scores do not reflect documented peer concerns. Manager asked to revise with peer input incorporated. Revised assessment: Respect 2.5. Verdict changed to Coaching Needed. Entity CEO review triggered.

## Audit Log

All 30-60-90 records are created at hire, updated at each milestone, and retained for the duration of employment plus three years. Verdict classifications are stored as structured data (not free text) to enable portfolio-level aggregation. Day 90 entity CEO signatures are scanned or digitally confirmed and attached to the record. Dr. Lewis quarterly spot-check selections and findings are logged separately as program quality records.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed annually during the THRIVE culture ops review. It is deprecated when MBL adopts a successor integration framework that formally replaces the 30-60-90 structure, or when verdict thresholds are revised by Dr. Lewis based on portfolio data. The three-verdict classification system (Full Integration / Coaching Needed / Values Misalignment) is a core data standard and must be preserved in any successor skill to maintain historical comparability.
