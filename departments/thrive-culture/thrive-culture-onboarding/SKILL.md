# THRIVE Culture Onboarding

Every new hire joins a culture before they join a job — how THRIVE is introduced in Week 1 determines whether it becomes a real operating norm or a wall poster. This skill structures the THRIVE culture integration sequence for all new hires across MBL entities, tailored to the specific operational context of each portfolio company.

## When to Use

- Any new hire joins Allevio, HIVE Partners, Column6, or MBL HoldCo at any level below Manager (use thrive-new-leader-onboarding for Manager and above)
- Callie is updating the THRIVE onboarding curriculum and needs the current protocol as a reference baseline
- An entity is onboarding a cohort and needs a batch THRIVE orientation plan
- Dr. Lewis is auditing onboarding completion rates and needs the checklist format

## THRIVE Culture Onboarding Framework

```
THRIVE CULTURE ONBOARDING — [ENTITY] | [NEW HIRE NAME] | [ROLE] | START: [DATE]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WEEK 1 — THRIVE ORIENTATION SESSION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitator: Callie (primary) or Dr. Lewis (if Callie unavailable)
Duration: 60 minutes | Format: 1:1 or small cohort (≤5)
Content:
  □ What is THRIVE? Why these six values? (origin and intent)
  □ What does each pillar look like at THIS entity specifically?
  □ What does a THRIVE failure look like? (real examples; anonymized)
  □ The Integrity kill criterion — explained plainly, not sugarcoated
  □ How THRIVE is assessed and what the stakes are
  □ Q&A — open; no wrong questions

ENTITY-SPECIFIC THRIVE CONTEXT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Allevio (Healthcare / MSO):
  TRUTH     — Honest clinical and billing reporting; no upcoding
  HUSTLE    — Care coordination doesn't wait; patients first
  RESPECT   — Patient dignity, HIPAA as Respect in practice
  INTEGRITY — PHI handling failure = Integrity failure; HIPAA + kill criterion
  VALUE     — Revenue cycle efficiency; PMPM outcomes
  ENJOY     — Clinical staff burnout is a culture signal, not a personal problem

HIVE Partners (Oil & Gas):
  TRUTH     — Accurate production reporting to LPs; no optimistic spin
  HUSTLE    — Field accountability; WTI $58/bbl trigger demands urgency
  RESPECT   — Landowner relations; operator relationships; field crew dignity
  INTEGRITY — LP data accuracy; no selective reporting
  VALUE     — Returns to LPs; cost discipline at the wellhead
  ENJOY     — Field conditions matter; safety is Enjoy in practice

Column6 (CTV / Media):
  TRUTH     — Campaign performance reported accurately; no vanity metrics
  HUSTLE    — Agency speed; client deadlines are real
  RESPECT   — Client relationships; CCPA compliance as Respect
  INTEGRITY — Gross margin ≥35%; no margin-hiding; honest client billing
  VALUE     — Real media outcomes; not impressions theater
  ENJOY     — Creative energy; client delight as culture signal

MBL HoldCo:
  TRUTH     — Honest reporting up to Matt Mathison; no filtered updates
  HUSTLE    — Portfolio execution velocity; M&A pipeline discipline
  RESPECT   — Portfolio CEOs are partners, not subordinates
  INTEGRITY — Financial reporting (QB, Bill.com); governance standards
  VALUE     — Portfolio value creation; AI ROI; deal economics
  ENJOY     — Team sustainability; pace without burnout

THRIVE PILLAR EXAMPLES — ROLE-SPECIFIC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Manager fills in role-specific THRIVE examples at Day 1 conversation:
  "For your role as [ROLE], Truth looks like: ___________________"
  "For your role, Integrity failure would look like: ___________________"
  "We'd say you're creating Value when: ___________________"

BUDDY PAIRING — THRIVE EXEMPLAR
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  □ Assign 1 THRIVE exemplar buddy (peer level; same entity; different team)
  □ Buddy has 2 touchpoints in first 30 days (informal; not formal review)
  □ Buddy is NOT the manager — removes hierarchy from culture questions
  □ Buddy nominated from THRIVE recognition list or Dr. Lewis roster

MANAGER THRIVE CONVERSATION — WEEK 1
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Format: 30-min 1:1 with direct manager in Week 1
Questions manager asks:
  □ What does THRIVE mean to you so far?
  □ Are there any pillars that feel unclear or uncomfortable?
  □ How do you want me to give you THRIVE feedback as we go?
  □ Is there anything from the orientation you want to talk through?

DAY 30 — THRIVE CHECK-IN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Facilitated by: Manager (all roles); Dr. Lewis spot-checks 10% of new hires
Format: 15-min structured conversation
  □ How are the THRIVE values showing up in your day-to-day?
  □ Have you seen any behaviors that didn't feel aligned?
  □ Any confusion about expectations?
Output: Brief note to HR / Dr. Lewis program log

ONBOARDING COMPLETION TRACKING
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Entity-level completion tracked by Callie:
  □ Orientation session attended (date + facilitator)
  □ Buddy paired (name + first touchpoint date)
  □ Manager conversation completed (date)
  □ Day 30 check-in completed (date + summary on file)
  Target: 100% completion within 35 days of start date
  Flag: Any new hire missing 2+ items at Day 35 → Callie notifies Dr. Lewis
```

## Output Contract

- Onboarding records are entity-specific and must include the entity-context section populated for the correct portfolio company — a generic THRIVE orientation without entity context fails to connect values to the actual work, which is the primary reason new hires report THRIVE feeling abstract or theoretical in the first 90 days.

- Buddy pairing is mandatory and must be documented with a name and first touchpoint date before the record is marked complete — buddies provide a peer-level culture translation layer that managers cannot replicate because the power dynamic changes what questions new hires feel safe asking.

- Day 30 check-in notes are retained in the program log, not just verbally completed — undocumented check-ins cannot be used to detect early warning patterns when Dr. Lewis reviews quarterly completion data, and pattern detection across entities is one of the primary value outputs of this program.

## System Dependencies

### Reads from
- Entity THRIVE playbook (Callie maintains; entity-specific version)
- THRIVE exemplar roster (Dr. Lewis; used for buddy pairing pool)
- Onboarding calendar (entity HR or Krista.ai scheduling integration)

### Writes to
- Callie's onboarding completion tracker (entity-level; updated per new hire)
- Dr. Lewis program log (quarterly roll-up; completion rate by entity)
- HR record (Day 30 check-in summary attached to new hire file)

### HITL Required
- Callie reviews all completion records at Day 35 and flags gaps to Dr. Lewis
- Dr. Lewis spot-checks 10% of Day 30 check-in notes per quarter
- Any new hire who raises an Integrity concern in Day 30 check-in → immediate Dr. Lewis review

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** New billing coordinator joins Allevio on Week 1. Callie facilitates a 45-min THRIVE orientation with two other new hires. Manager delivers HIPAA-as-Respect framing in Week 1 conversation. Buddy (senior care coordinator) checks in at Day 14. Day 30 check-in: new hire articulates Integrity clearly and asks a specific question about PHI handling — manager answers and flags the question as a training gap to Callie.

2. **Edge case:** HIVE hires a field technician in a remote basin location with limited access to scheduled video calls. Callie coordinates an asynchronous THRIVE orientation package (recorded video + PDF playbook + async Q&A channel). Buddy pairing is done with a local field lead. Day 30 check-in happens via phone. Completion record is filed; all milestones met within 35 days.

3. **Adversarial:** Column6 onboards three new hires in the same week; manager skips the manager conversation for two of them citing campaign deadline pressure. Callie's Day 35 audit flags two incomplete records. Dr. Lewis is notified. Manager is asked to complete the conversations by Day 40. One of the skipped new hires later surfaces a peer Respect concern at their 60-day review — the missing conversation is noted as a contributing factor in the incident record.

## Audit Log

All THRIVE culture onboarding records are created at the time of hire, updated at each milestone, and retained for the duration of employment plus two years. Facilitator name and date are captured for every session. Callie's quarterly completion report is archived as a program artifact and shared with Dr. Lewis. Any Integrity-related flag from a new hire check-in is logged separately with escalation timestamp.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed by Callie and Dr. Lewis each January as part of the annual THRIVE playbook update cycle. It is deprecated when MBL adopts a materially different onboarding architecture that supersedes this protocol, or when THRIVE pillar definitions are formally revised. Fitz Bowen must test any successor onboarding skill with a real new hire cohort before the prior version is retired.
