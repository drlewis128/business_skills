# THRIVE Recognition Builder

Recognition is how MBL reinforces the behaviors it wants to see more of. This skill builds and operates a recognition system aligned with THRIVE's Enjoy and Value pillars — ensuring that recognition is tied to specific behaviors and outcomes, delivered at the right frequency and visibility level, and structured so entity CEOs and Dr. Lewis are modeling the culture they expect.

## When to Use

- An entity CEO wants to launch or refresh a recognition program aligned with THRIVE values
- A team is producing strong outcomes without visible acknowledgment, creating retention risk
- A spot bonus or formal recognition event needs to be structured and communicated correctly
- Callie is building THRIVE Spotlight content or Dr. Lewis is coaching an entity CEO on recognition cadence

## Recognition Builder Framework

```
THRIVE RECOGNITION BUILDER FRAMEWORK
=======================================

SECTION 1 — THRIVE RECOGNITION PRINCIPLES
  Recognition reinforces what it rewards — recognize THRIVE behaviors, not just outcomes
  THRIVE behavior + outcome = the ideal recognition event (behavior + result)
  Outcome only: reinforces result-seeking without values alignment
  Behavior only: reinforces effort without demanding impact
  Recognition delayed >2 weeks from the event loses its reinforcement value
  Frequency matters: too rare = performative; too frequent = meaningless; the right cadence = cultural

SECTION 2 — RECOGNITION TIERS (THRIVE FRAMEWORK)
  MICRO RECOGNITION (Weekly):
    - What: specific, verbal or written acknowledgment of a THRIVE behavior observed that week
    - Who gives: any manager or peer
    - Channel: 1:1 conversation, team Slack/Teams channel, brief ops meeting mention
    - Format: "[Name] — I noticed you [specific behavior tied to THRIVE pillar]. That's the kind of [pillar] that makes this team stronger."
    - Cost: $0; time: 30 seconds

  THRIVE SPOTLIGHT (Monthly):
    - What: formal recognition tied to a specific THRIVE pillar; entity-wide visibility
    - Who gives: entity CEO (nominates); Dr. Lewis (MBL level)
    - Selection: entity CEO reviews manager nominations; one per pillar per month (six possible)
    - Channel: all-hands meeting, entity newsletter, Monday.com announcement
    - Format: name + pillar + specific behavior + outcome + impact on team/entity
    - Cost: $0 + optional spot bonus (see Section 3)

  SPOT BONUS (As-Occurs):
    - Tier 1 ($100-$500): Dr. Lewis approval required; entity CEO or manager nominates
    - Tier 2 ($500+): Matt Mathison approval required; entity CEO nominates
    - Criteria: exceptional THRIVE behavior + measurable impact + above-and-beyond standard
    - Timing: within 5 business days of the event (not bundled into payroll cycles)
    - Delivery: personal communication from nominating leader before paycheck notice

  ANNUAL THRIVE AWARD (Yearly):
    - One per entity; entity CEO + Dr. Lewis select
    - Recognizes the individual who most embodied ALL SIX THRIVE pillars over the year
    - Presented by Matt Mathison in person or by video if not in-person feasible
    - Award value: defined by entity; minimum meaningful ($1,000+ or equivalent)

SECTION 3 — WHAT TO RECOGNIZE (THRIVE PILLAR MAPPING)
  Truth:      Surfacing a problem early; delivering hard feedback that improved an outcome; honest reporting when it was uncomfortable
  Hustle:     Delivering ahead of deadline; self-initiating work that wasn't assigned; recovering a miss with speed and ownership
  Respect:    Creating inclusion in a meeting; supporting a peer under pressure; handling a conflict with grace
  Integrity:  Choosing the harder right thing; declining to conceal an error; correcting a mistake proactively
  Value:      Delivering a measurable outcome that exceeds expectation; creating ROI that wasn't planned
  Enjoy:      Building team energy; recruiting a peer; creating a moment that made the team want to keep going

SECTION 4 — PUBLIC VS. PRIVATE RECOGNITION
  Public recognition: appropriate for THRIVE behaviors and outcomes visible to the team; motivates observers
  Private recognition: appropriate for sensitive situations, personal growth, or intimate impact
  Never public: corrective feedback, recognition that embarrasses the recipient (read the person first)
  Always private first for a new recipient: check comfort level before going public
  Entity CEO default: public recognition for Spotlight; private for personal 1:1 acknowledgment

SECTION 5 — ENTITY CEO RECOGNITION CADENCE
  Weekly: ≥1 micro recognition in team communication (email, Slack, Teams)
  Monthly: THRIVE Spotlight selection and delivery
  Quarterly: review recognition data — who has been recognized; who hasn't; gaps signal culture risk
  Annual: THRIVE Award delivery
  Dr. Lewis expectation: entity CEOs who give no recognition for >30 days = flagged in culture review

SECTION 6 — PEER RECOGNITION CHANNELS
  Peer-to-peer recognition is enabled through: Teams/Slack designated channel, Monday.com recognition board, or structured peer appreciation in retrospective meetings
  Peer recognition must be anchored to a THRIVE pillar to be logged and counted
  "Great job" without a THRIVE anchor = social nicety; not recognition data
  Entity CEO acknowledges peer recognition publicly to amplify its signal

SECTION 7 — ENTITY-SPECIFIC RECOGNITION CONTEXT
  Allevio:  Recognize patient care wins, near-miss safety reporting, member retention behaviors
  HIVE:     Recognize production milestones, field safety behaviors, LP communication excellence
  Column6:  Recognize client wins, creative output, gross margin protection behaviors
  MBL HoldCo: Recognize portfolio value creation, AI system delivery, cross-entity collaboration
```

## Output Contract

- **Recognition must be anchored to a specific THRIVE behavior — not just a good outcome — because the behavior is what the organization needs to replicate.** A salesperson who closes a deal through a lucky referral has produced an outcome; a salesperson who closes a deal by surfacing a client's unstated concern through honest discovery has demonstrated Truth + Value. Recognizing only the deal trains everyone to seek deals; recognizing the behavior trains everyone to ask better questions. The THRIVE anchor makes recognition a culture-building tool, not just a reward mechanism.

- **Spot bonuses require rapid delivery (within 5 business days) because delayed financial recognition becomes disconnected from the behavior it rewards.** A bonus received in the next regular payroll cycle with no personal communication is indistinguishable from a salary adjustment — the recipient cannot connect the reward to the specific action that earned it. The 5-day rule with personal delivery from the nominating leader ensures the recognition retains its behavioral signal and its personal impact.

- **Recognition gaps (>30 days without recognition for an individual) are surfaced as culture risk signals.** Teams where recognition is clustered around a few visible performers and absent for the steady, reliable contributors create two-tier cultures: a recognized elite and an invisible majority. The invisible majority is the segment with the highest voluntary turnover risk — because they are doing the work, getting no signal that it is seen, and eventually deciding they are not valued. The 30-day gap flag exists to catch this dynamic before it becomes a resignation.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** An Allevio care coordinator proactively reports a near-miss medication issue that could have harmed a member and resulted in a regulatory incident. Manager nominates for a THRIVE Spotlight (Integrity pillar) and a $250 spot bonus. Dr. Lewis approves. Entity CEO delivers the Spotlight in the monthly all-hands with the specific behavior and outcome named. The care coordinator is informed personally by the manager before the all-hands.

2. **Edge case:** A HIVE field crew member has strong production output but is uncomfortable with public recognition due to crew culture norms ("don't make anyone stand out"). Skill recommends private micro recognition in the 1:1, notes the preference for the entity CEO's awareness, and suggests a team-level acknowledgment (the crew as a whole, not the individual) to honor the cultural context while still reinforcing the behavior.

3. **Adversarial:** An entity CEO has not given any recognition in 47 days. When flagged, they argue that "the team knows I appreciate them" and that formal recognition "feels forced." Skill flags the 47-day gap as a culture risk, pulls voluntary turnover data showing an uptick in the same period, and presents the correlation to Dr. Lewis with a recommendation for a coaching conversation — not a mandated recognition event, but a conversation about what the gap signals to the team.

## Audit Log

All recognition events (micro, spotlight, spot bonus, annual award), gap flags, approval chains, and entity CEO recognition cadence data are logged with entity, date, pillar, recipient role, channel, and approver. Spot bonus amounts and THRIVE basis are retained for HRIS and payroll audit purposes. Dr. Lewis reviews recognition pattern data quarterly to assess culture reinforcement alignment across entities.
