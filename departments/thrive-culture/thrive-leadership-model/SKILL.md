# THRIVE Leadership Model

Culture is not a policy — it is the aggregate of what leaders model every day. This skill defines MBL's six-dimension THRIVE leadership model, assesses entity CEOs and managers against it, and builds development plans that close the gap between where a leader is and where MBL needs them to be.

## When to Use

- A new entity CEO or manager needs to understand MBL's leadership expectations from day one
- An entity CEO is underperforming on a specific THRIVE leadership dimension and needs a structured development plan
- Dr. Lewis is conducting a quarterly entity CEO leadership review for Matt Mathison
- Callie is building leadership expectation content for the THRIVE manager playbook

## Leadership Model Framework

```
THRIVE LEADERSHIP MODEL FRAMEWORK
====================================

SECTION 1 — THE CORE PRINCIPLE: LEADERS ARE CULTURE CARRIERS
  Culture is not what is written on a values page — it is what leaders tolerate, reward, and model
  If the leader is not embodying all six THRIVE pillars, the team will not either — regardless of training
  The entity CEO is the most powerful culture variable in the entity — above any program, process, or policy
  Matt Mathison is the most powerful culture variable in MBL — he sets the ceiling and the floor
  Leadership development at MBL = THRIVE pillar development, not generic leadership skills

SECTION 2 — THE 6-DIMENSION THRIVE LEADERSHIP MODEL
  DIMENSION 1 — TRUTH (Does the leader surface reality?)
    Expectation: delivers bad news proactively; does not manage up through selective optimism
    Expectation: creates safety for others to surface problems without punishment
    Expectation: holds themselves accountable to factual, unfiltered communication
    Assessment question: "When was the last time this leader delivered genuinely uncomfortable news to Matt Mathison or Dr. Lewis before being asked?"

  DIMENSION 2 — HUSTLE (Does the leader drive pace?)
    Expectation: models urgency; arrives at decisions quickly; does not let issues sit
    Expectation: holds the team to 95%+ delivery rate while meeting it themselves
    Expectation: self-initiates; does not wait for direction on the obvious
    Assessment question: "Does this leader move at the pace the entity requires, or do they moderate the team's speed by their own slower cadence?"

  DIMENSION 3 — RESPECT (Does the leader treat people well?)
    Expectation: no condescension; no public correction; treats every person's input as worth engaging
    Expectation: creates a team culture where people feel seen and valued
    Expectation: models respectful disagreement; does not suppress dissent through tone or authority
    Assessment question: "Would this leader's direct reports say they feel respected by them, or do they feel managed by them?"

  DIMENSION 4 — INTEGRITY (Does the leader do what they say?)
    Expectation: commitments are kept; no exceptions without proactive notification
    Expectation: applies the kill criterion to themselves: would they pass their own Integrity gate?
    Expectation: tells the truth about performance, financials, and operations — no favorable rounding
    Assessment question: "Is there any known gap between what this leader says and what they do?"

  DIMENSION 5 — VALUE (Does the leader produce outcomes?)
    Expectation: personal output is measurable; they do not just direct work, they create value
    Expectation: every initiative they launch has a quantified value case before it starts
    Expectation: holds the entity to a standard of output, not activity
    Assessment question: "What specific, dollar-quantifiable value has this leader created in the last quarter?"

  DIMENSION 6 — ENJOY (Does the leader build a culture people want to be in?)
    Expectation: team eNPS reflects a leader who creates an energizing environment
    Expectation: gives recognition; creates moments of celebration; does not treat the work as purely transactional
    Expectation: manages their own wellbeing well enough to sustain the pace over time
    Assessment question: "Would top performers choose to stay on this team because of the culture this leader creates?"

SECTION 3 — ENTITY CEO THRIVE LEADERSHIP EXPECTATIONS
  Entity CEOs are held to all six dimensions at the highest standard
  Entity CEO quarterly review: Dr. Lewis rates each dimension 1-5; shares with Matt Mathison
  Entity CEO development: targeted coaching by Dr. Lewis on lowest 1-2 dimensions per quarter
  Kill criterion applies to entity CEOs: Integrity dimension ≤2 = separation, regardless of entity performance
  Entity CEO and their entity's culture are treated as inseparable for assessment purposes

SECTION 4 — MANAGER THRIVE LEADERSHIP EXPECTATIONS
  Managers are held to all six dimensions at a level appropriate to their scope and tenure
  Manager quarterly assessment: entity CEO rates each dimension 1-5
  Manager development: entity CEO provides coaching; Dr. Lewis involved for persistent gaps
  Manager 360: direct reports rate managers on all six dimensions semi-annually
  Manager with any dimension ≤2 from direct report ratings: automatic entity CEO + Dr. Lewis review

SECTION 5 — MATT MATHISON'S LEADERSHIP STANDARD
  Matt Mathison holds himself to the highest standard across all six dimensions
  His Truth standard: he expects bad news quickly and without softening; he models it himself
  His Hustle standard: he operates at a pace that others aspire to match
  His Integrity standard: if he says he'll do something, it is done — his commitments are binary
  His Value standard: every MBL investment must create quantified returns
  His Enjoy standard: he builds a culture where top performers want to be — by being the culture

SECTION 6 — LEADERSHIP ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
  Rating scale: 1 (Critical gap), 2 (Below standard), 3 (Meets standard), 4 (Strong), 5 (Exceptional)
  Assessment inputs: self-rating + manager rating + 360 (direct reports, peers)
  Minimum standard: all dimensions ≥3; any dimension at 1-2 = active development plan required
  Development plan: specific behavioral commitments per dimension + 60-day reassessment
  Reporting: entity CEO scores → Dr. Lewis; manager scores → entity CEO; portfolio summary → Matt Mathison quarterly

SECTION 7 — LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT PRIORITIES PER ENTITY
  MBL HoldCo:    Truth (directness to LPs), Value (ROI discipline), Hustle (portfolio execution speed)
  Allevio:       Respect (clinical culture), Enjoy (burnout prevention), Integrity (billing and care accuracy)
  HIVE:          Integrity (LP reporting), Hustle (production pace), Respect (field safety culture)
  Column6:       Value (gross margin discipline), Hustle (IO velocity), Enjoy (creative team energy)
```

## Output Contract

- **Leadership assessment uses a consensus score across self, manager, and 360 because each source catches different failure modes.** Self-assessment catches self-awareness gaps (the leader who scores themselves a 5 on Truth but never delivers hard news). Manager assessment catches visibility bias (the leader who performs for their manager but isn't present for their team). 360 from direct reports catches the lived experience that determines team culture. Any single source without the others produces an incomplete and potentially misleading leadership picture.

- **The kill criterion applies to Integrity at the entity CEO level without exception — regardless of entity performance.** An entity CEO who achieves strong financial results but fails the Integrity dimension is producing returns built on an unstable foundation. LP trust, client relationships, and regulatory standing all depend on the entity CEO's personal Integrity as much as their operational skill. The separation decision for an Integrity failure at the entity CEO level is not a performance trade-off; it is a values non-negotiable that Matt Mathison enforces directly.

- **Development priorities are limited to the lowest 1-2 dimensions per cycle because trying to improve all dimensions simultaneously produces improvement in none.** Leadership development is a capacity-constrained activity — a leader can sustain focused behavioral change on 1-2 dimensions at a time; asking for six simultaneous improvements produces six shallow efforts and no lasting change. The 60-day reassessment creates the accountability loop that makes the commitment real rather than aspirational.

## Test Cases

1. **Golden path:** Allevio entity CEO quarterly assessment. Truth = 4, Hustle = 3, Respect = 4, Integrity = 4, Value = 3, Enjoy = 3. Two development priorities identified: Hustle (entity-level delivery rate at 89%, below the 95% standard) and Value (no quantified ROI case on last quarter's major initiative). Development plan generated with specific behavioral commitments and 60-day check-in. Dr. Lewis reviews; Matt Mathison receives summary.

2. **Edge case:** A HIVE manager's 360 shows Integrity at 2.3 from direct reports, based on a pattern of committing to timeline revisions in 1:1s and not honoring them. Self-score is 4. The gap between self-score (4) and 360 score (2.3) triggers an automatic Dr. Lewis review. Skill generates a leadership assessment flagging the Integrity approach (though not at the kill threshold for this tier), recommends entity CEO coaching conversation, and sets a 45-day behavioral reassessment.

3. **Adversarial:** An entity CEO completes their self-assessment with all 5s and requests that the 360 data not be included in the final assessment, citing "survey fatigue on the team." Skill blocks the exclusion of 360 data (it is a required input per assessment protocol), notifies Dr. Lewis of the request and the resistance, and proceeds with the standard assessment including 360 data. The request to exclude the data is logged as a transparency signal for Dr. Lewis's review.

## Audit Log

All leadership assessment scores, development plans, 60-day reassessment outcomes, Integrity kill criterion events, and escalation decisions are logged with entity, date, assessment inputs used, consensus scores, and reviewer identity. Entity CEO assessment history is maintained for the duration of their tenure and retained for 36 months after departure. Matt Mathison receives an annual summary of all entity CEO leadership trends.

## Deprecation

This skill is reviewed annually by Dr. Lewis and Callie. It is versioned when the THRIVE leadership model is refined by Matt Mathison (e.g., dimensional weighting changes, new entity-specific standards), when the 360 platform changes, or when entity CEO assessment automation is built into Krista.ai. The kill criterion section is never removed in any version — it is a permanent element of the leadership model.
