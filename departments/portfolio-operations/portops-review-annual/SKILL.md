# Portfolio Ops Annual Review

The annual review is the highest-stakes recurring meeting in the MBL portfolio calendar — a full-year accountability session attended by Matt Mathison, all entity CEOs, and the board (where applicable). It produces board-quality materials, feeds the LP annual letter directly, and closes the performance year with Matt's formal attestation. This is not a planning session — it is a reckoning with what happened, followed by a deliberate reset for the year ahead.

## When to Use
- Preparing full-year performance materials for the annual portfolio review session
- Producing board-quality presentations for Matt, entity CEOs, and LP audiences
- Generating the THRIVE culture score per entity (quantified assessment)
- Drafting the LP summary section that feeds directly into the annual LP letter
- Collecting Matt's formal attestation that the annual review is accurate and complete

## Annual Review Framework

```
ANNUAL PORTFOLIO REVIEW — FULL-YEAR STRUCTURE
==============================================

PRE-MEETING REQUIREMENTS (Dr. Lewis, 10 business days before)
--------------------------------------------------------------
- Compile full-year financials vs. plan per entity (from QuickBooks, entity CFOs)
- Produce mark-to-market entity valuations (Dr. Lewis + Finance lead)
- Grade all annual OKRs (entity CEOs pre-submit self-grades; Dr. Lewis reconciles)
- Conduct THRIVE culture assessment per entity (survey + behavioral data)
- Prepare LP performance summary (distributions, IRR, MOIC per fund/entity)
- Collect Morgan Sills M&A full-year recap (Allevio pipeline)
- Draft next-year OKRs per entity (entity CEOs submit drafts)
- Distribute full pre-read package 5 business days before the meeting
- Confirm board attendance (if applicable) 10 business days in advance

SESSION FORMAT
--------------
Session 1 (2 hours): Performance Reckoning
  [0:00–0:20]  FULL-YEAR FINANCIALS
    - Revenue, EBITDA, cash flow vs. plan — all 4 entities
    - Variances explained: drivers, accountability, forward impact
    - Matt asks direct questions — no deflection
    - Flag any entity that missed plan by >20% — remediation plan required

  [0:20–0:40]  ENTITY VALUATIONS (MARK-TO-MARKET)
    - Current enterprise value per entity
    - Value creation vs. acquisition basis (MOIC to date)
    - Key value drivers and destroyers
    - Matt's view on hold vs. exit readiness per entity

  [0:40–0:55]  LP PERFORMANCE SUMMARY
    - Total distributions this year
    - IRR and MOIC per LP vehicle
    - LP communication status and any relationship flags
    - This section is the source material for the LP annual letter — accuracy is critical

  [0:55–1:10]  THRIVE CULTURE ASSESSMENT
    - Each entity scored 1–5 per THRIVE dimension (T/H/R/I/V/E)
    - Data sources: survey results, attrition rate, performance review data, incident log
    - Integrity dimension: any integrity breach in the year = automatic red flag
    - Dr. Lewis presents; entity CEOs may respond but cannot revise the score
    - Matt sets the bar: what is the minimum acceptable score for continued investment?

  [1:10–1:25]  ANNUAL OKR GRADES
    - Final OKR grades for the year (0.0–1.0 per OKR)
    - Entity CEO self-grade vs. Dr. Lewis reconciled grade — delta explained
    - Portfolio-level OKRs graded
    - Any OKR that was abandoned mid-year requires a documented reason

  [1:25–1:40]  RISK REGISTER YEAR-END CLOSE
    - Risks resolved during the year — close them formally
    - Risks that materialized — post-mortem required for each
    - Risks carried into next year — priority ranking
    - New risks identified for the coming year

Session 2 (90 min): Reset and Forward Planning
  [0:00–0:30]  NEXT-YEAR PRIORITIES AND OKRS
    - Entity CEO presents draft OKRs for next year (5 min each)
    - Matt redirects or approves
    - Portfolio-level OKRs set (2–3 max, aligned to Matt's strategic intent)
    - Dr. Lewis confirms AI/Krista.ai roadmap integration with entity OKRs

  [0:30–0:50]  M&A AND STRATEGIC INITIATIVES
    - Deal pipeline for next year
    - Any planned acquisitions, divestitures, or restructuring
    - Capital allocation priorities (Matt's direction)

  [0:50–1:20]  MATT ATTESTATION AND CLOSE
    - Dr. Lewis presents the annual review summary document
    - Matt reviews, asks final questions, makes final revisions
    - Matt signs the attestation: formal confirmation that the annual review is accurate
    - Entity CEOs acknowledge the annual record in writing (email confirmation acceptable)
    - Next year's review calendar confirmed

THRIVE CULTURE SCORING METHODOLOGY
-----------------------------------
Score each dimension 1–5 per entity:
  T — Truth:      Does leadership communicate honestly? Are problems surfaced early?
  H — Hustle:     Is the team executing with urgency? Are deadlines met? Revenue growing?
  R — Respect:    Are people treated with dignity? Is feedback constructive?
  I — Integrity:  Are commitments kept? Any compliance/ethics issues? [KILL CRITERION]
  V — Value:      Is the entity creating value for customers, LPs, and MBL?
  E — Enjoy:      Is the culture sustainable? Are top performers retained?

Scoring data sources:
  - Annual employee/team survey (Likert scale, anonymous)
  - Attrition rate vs. industry benchmark
  - Performance review completion rate and outcome distribution
  - Compliance/ethics incident log
  - Customer NPS or satisfaction score (where applicable)
  - Dr. Lewis qualitative assessment from monthly and quarterly reviews

Integrity Kill Criterion:
  Any integrity breach documented in the year (fraud, falsification, material misrepresentation,
  confirmed ethical violation) results in automatic Score = 1 for Integrity regardless of 
  other factors. This triggers a mandatory review by Matt and is escalated to the board.
```

## Output Contract
- The annual review document is a legal and fiduciary record. Matt's attestation makes it binding as the authoritative account of portfolio performance for the fiscal year. It will be referenced in LP communications, board reporting, and potentially in M&A diligence if any entity is sold. Every number must be sourced (QuickBooks, entity financials, or third-party valuation) and every claim must be traceable. Dr. Lewis is responsible for accuracy — if a number is disputed after attestation, Dr. Lewis owns the correction process.
- The THRIVE culture scores are not soft assessments — they are documented ratings with explicit data sources. An entity with an Integrity score of 1 triggers a formal escalation protocol regardless of overall financial performance. THRIVE is not a bonus metric; it is a fundamental investment criterion. A portfolio company that scores below 2.5 average THRIVE is under watch status, and Matt may direct a remediation plan or reconsider the investment thesis.
- The LP performance summary section feeds directly into the annual LP letter — the language in Section 3 must be written with LP readers in mind, not just internal ops readers. It should be accurate, appropriately candid (LPs value truth over spin), and free of jargon. Dr. Lewis drafts this section; Matt edits it before it goes into the LP letter. The LP letter itself is a separate document, but this skill produces the source material that Matt's team will use to draft it.

## System Dependencies
**Reads from:** QuickBooks (entity full-year financials), Monday.com (annual OKR tracker), SharePoint (12 months of meeting notes, risk register, quarterly review docs), THRIVE survey platform, LP performance tracking (Covercy for HIVE, or equivalent), Krista.ai (year-end synthesis if applicable)
**Writes to:** SharePoint (annual review document, board materials folder), Matt's email (attestation package), entity CEOs (acknowledgment request), LP letter draft (source section), Monday.com (next-year OKRs), risk register (year-end close + new risks)
**HITL Required:** Matt Mathison must sign the attestation — this cannot be delegated. Entity CEOs must acknowledge the annual record. Any Integrity kill criterion finding must be reviewed by Matt and the board before the annual document is finalized. Dr. Lewis does not release LP-facing materials without Matt's explicit approval.

## Test Cases
- **Golden path:** All entity CEOs attend both sessions, financials reconciled 5 days prior, THRIVE scores agreed in the session, Matt signs attestation within 72 hours of the meeting, LP summary language approved by Matt, next-year OKRs in Monday.com by end of the review week.
- **Edge case:** An entity missed plan by more than 30% — the annual review must include a formal post-mortem for that entity (what happened, who owned it, what changes are being made) as an appendix. The post-mortem is attached to the annual review document and referenced in the BLUF.
- **Adversarial:** An entity CEO refuses to acknowledge the annual record because they dispute a financial figure — Dr. Lewis escalates to Matt immediately. The annual review is not delayed; the CEO's dispute is documented as a dissent appendix, and Matt resolves the dispute within 10 business days. The attestation clock does not pause.

## Audit Log
The annual review package is stored in SharePoint at `/MBL Partners/Portfolio Ops/Annual Reviews/[YYYY]/` with access restricted to Matt Mathison, Dr. Lewis, entity CEOs, and the board (where applicable). LPs do not receive direct access to the full document — only the LP summary section, as incorporated into the annual LP letter. The attestation document is retained as a signed PDF. Retention: 10 years minimum, or per applicable fund agreement terms, whichever is longer.

## Deprecation
This skill is superseded if MBL adopts a formal CFO or COO role who owns the annual review process, or if the portfolio grows to a scale where entity-level annual reviews require separate dedicated processes. The THRIVE scoring methodology should be reviewed annually by Matt and updated if the cultural framework evolves. If any portfolio entity is fully exited, remove it from the THRIVE scoring table and document the exit in the annual review for that year.
