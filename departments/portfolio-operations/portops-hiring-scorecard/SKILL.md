# Portfolio Operator Hiring Scorecard

This scorecard is used for entity CEO hires and senior ops roles across the MBL portfolio. It assesses five core competencies through structured interview questions and a weighted scoring rubric. THRIVE alignment is non-negotiable — an Integrity flag at any point in the process is an automatic disqualification, regardless of scores in other dimensions. Matt Mathison has final approval authority on all entity CEO hires.

## When to Use
- Evaluating a candidate for an entity CEO role (Allevio, HIVE, Column6) or a senior portfolio ops position
- Structuring the interview process to ensure consistent, bias-resistant evaluation
- Documenting the hiring recommendation for Matt Mathison's final review and approval
- Identifying THRIVE misalignment early before extending an offer

## Hiring Scorecard Framework

```
PORTFOLIO OPERATOR HIRING SCORECARD
=====================================

CANDIDATE INFORMATION
---------------------
  Candidate Name:     ___________________________
  Role:               ___________________________
  Entity:             ___________________________
  Interviewer(s):     ___________________________
  Interview Date:     ___________________________
  Scorecard Owner:    Dr. John Lewis

COMPETENCY WEIGHTING
---------------------
  1. Business Acumen & Financial Literacy     — 25%
  2. Portfolio / Multi-Entity Thinking         — 20%
  3. Systems Orientation & Operational Rigor  — 20%
  4. THRIVE Alignment (Culture Fit)           — 25%
  5. Leadership & Accountability              — 10%

  Note: THRIVE — Integrity dimension is a KILL CRITERION.
  Any integrity flag = automatic disqualification. No exceptions.
  Matt Mathison does not override the integrity kill criterion.

SCORING RUBRIC (Per Competency)
--------------------------------
  5 — Exceptional: Exceeds the role's requirements; clear evidence of mastery
  4 — Strong:      Meets the role's requirements with demonstrated track record
  3 — Adequate:    Meets minimum requirements; may need development in this area
  2 — Weak:        Below requirements; would require significant support
  1 — Disqualifying: Clear gap that cannot be bridged; do not proceed

COMPETENCY 1: BUSINESS ACUMEN & FINANCIAL LITERACY (25%)
----------------------------------------------------------
  Core Question: Can this person read the business and drive value creation?

  Interview Questions:
    Q1. Walk me through how you think about revenue vs. EBITDA margin trade-offs in
        a growth-stage business. Give me a specific example from your experience.
    Q2. Tell me about a time you identified a financial problem before it hit the P&L.
        What were the signals? What did you do?
    Q3. If you joined this entity and found a $500K variance to plan by month 2,
        what would you do in the first 72 hours?
    Q4. How do you build a budget that operations actually owns, not just finance?

  Scoring Indicators:
    5: P&L fluency, cash flow instinct, proactive financial management, unit economics
    4: Strong financial literacy, able to explain variances, comfortable with budgeting
    3: General finance awareness, may rely heavily on CFO/finance lead
    2: Limited financial literacy; struggles to explain variance or margin trade-offs
    1: Cannot engage with basic P&L questions; does not understand EBITDA

  Score: ___   Weighted: ___ (x0.25)   Notes: ___________________________

COMPETENCY 2: PORTFOLIO / MULTI-ENTITY THINKING (20%)
-------------------------------------------------------
  Core Question: Can this person operate within a PE-owned structure and think
                 beyond their own entity?

  Interview Questions:
    Q1. Have you ever worked in a PE-backed or multi-entity holding company structure?
        What did you learn about managing up to a GP or board?
    Q2. How do you balance the needs of your entity against the priorities of the
        parent organization when they conflict?
    Q3. Tell me about a time you had to operate with limited resources and had to
        make trade-offs that affected other parts of the organization.
    Q4. How do you think about sharing talent, systems, or infrastructure across
        business units in a portfolio context?

  Scoring Indicators:
    5: PE fluency, strong GP/board communication skills, cross-entity collaboration
    4: Experience in matrixed or multi-stakeholder environments; good portfolio instinct
    3: Single-entity operator; willing to learn portfolio context
    2: Siloed thinking; struggles to see beyond their own entity
    1: Active resistance to oversight or parent organization involvement

  Score: ___   Weighted: ___ (x0.20)   Notes: ___________________________

COMPETENCY 3: SYSTEMS ORIENTATION & OPERATIONAL RIGOR (20%)
------------------------------------------------------------
  Core Question: Does this person build systems, or just manage chaos?

  Interview Questions:
    Q1. Describe the operating system you use to run your business — how do you
        track priorities, hold people accountable, and make decisions?
    Q2. Tell me about a process you built from scratch. What problem did it solve?
        How do you know it worked?
    Q3. How do you use technology and data in your day-to-day operations?
        Give me a specific example — not a general answer.
    Q4. What breaks down first in a fast-growing business, and how do you prevent it?

  Scoring Indicators:
    5: Strong systems builder; uses data fluently; proactive process design
    4: Good operational rigor; builds repeatable systems; data-informed
    3: Somewhat reactive; manages well but doesn't always build systems proactively
    2: Relies on individual heroics; inconsistent process discipline
    1: No evidence of systems thinking; operates purely on instinct and relationships

  Score: ___   Weighted: ___ (x0.20)   Notes: ___________________________

COMPETENCY 4: THRIVE ALIGNMENT (25%)
--------------------------------------
  Core Question: Does this person live the values we will not compromise?
  CRITICAL: Integrity dimension is a KILL CRITERION. Any flag = disqualify.

  THRIVE Dimensions — Interview Questions:

  TRUTH (T)
    Q: Tell me about a time you delivered bad news to your board or investors.
       What was the news? How did you deliver it? What happened?
    Q: When have you been wrong about something important as a leader?
       How did you handle it?
    Signal: Do they lead with data or with spin? Is bad news early or late?

  HUSTLE (H)
    Q: Tell me about the most demanding period of your career. How did you sustain
       performance? What did you sacrifice, and do you regret it?
    Q: How do you create urgency in a team that has become comfortable?
    Signal: Is their energy level appropriate for a growth-stage portfolio company?

  RESPECT (R)
    Q: Tell me about a time you had a significant conflict with a team member.
       Walk me through it — what happened, what did you do, and what was the outcome?
    Q: How do you give feedback to someone who pushes back hard?
    Signal: Watch for contempt, dismissal, or blame-shifting in their answer.

  INTEGRITY (I) — KILL CRITERION
    Q: Tell me about a time you were asked to do something you believed was wrong.
       What did you do?
    Q: Have you ever had a compliance issue, a legal matter, or an ethics complaint
       in your career? Tell me about it.
    Reference check mandatory: ask every reference directly about integrity, ethics,
    and whether they would trust this person with confidential financial information.
    Background check required before offer.
    RED FLAGS: vague answers, deflection, blaming others, undisclosed legal matters,
    reference inconsistencies, or any pattern of boundary violations.

  VALUE (V)
    Q: How do you define value creation in the context of this role?
       Give me a specific example from your career.
    Q: What is the most value you have ever destroyed in a business, and what did
       you learn from it?
    Signal: Do they think in outcomes and impact, or inputs and effort?

  ENJOY (E)
    Q: What does a sustainable pace look like for you, and how do you protect it
       while still delivering at a high level?
    Q: What do you genuinely love about operating a business?
    Signal: Sustainable enthusiasm vs. burnout risk. Portfolio companies need durable leaders.

  THRIVE Score (average of T/H/R/V/E — note: I is binary kill/pass):
  T: ___ H: ___ R: ___ I: PASS/KILL V: ___ E: ___
  Average (T+H+R+V+E / 5): ___   Weighted: ___ (x0.25)

COMPETENCY 5: LEADERSHIP & ACCOUNTABILITY (10%)
-------------------------------------------------
  Core Question: Does this person attract, retain, and hold people accountable?

  Interview Questions:
    Q1. Tell me about someone you developed into a strong leader. What did you do?
        Where are they now?
    Q2. Tell me about a time you had to let someone go. How did you decide?
        How did you do it?
    Q3. How do you hold yourself accountable? Who holds you accountable?
    Q4. What is your retention rate for your top performers over the last 3 years?

  Scoring Indicators:
    5: Proven talent developer; clear accountability systems; low regrettable attrition
    4: Good people leader; holds team accountable; occasional development gaps
    3: Manages people adequately; limited evidence of developing talent
    2: Retention issues; weak accountability; team performance dependent on candidate
    1: Pattern of talent problems; no accountability mechanisms
```

## Output Contract
- The Integrity kill criterion is absolute and cannot be overridden by weighted score, candidate pedigree, or business urgency. A candidate who triggers the Integrity kill criterion is disqualified immediately, the scorecard is closed, and Dr. Lewis notifies Matt with a brief explanation. The candidate is not told they were disqualified on integrity grounds — only that they are not moving forward. This protects MBL from legal exposure while preserving the kill criterion's force.
- THRIVE alignment is scored against observed behavior and specific examples, not self-reported values. Candidates who say the right things but cannot provide concrete behavioral examples should be scored lower on the relevant THRIVE dimension. Cultural fit language ("they just felt right") is not acceptable as a scoring rationale — every score must be backed by a specific interview moment or reference check finding.
- Matt's final approval for entity CEO hires is not a rubber stamp — it is a substantive review of the scorecard. Dr. Lewis delivers the scorecard to Matt with a 15-minute verbal debrief. Matt may override a "Strong Hire" recommendation (rare) or approve a "Conditional" hire with specific conditions attached. Matt's approval decision and any conditions are documented in the scorecard and retained with the hiring file.

## System Dependencies
**Reads from:** Candidate resume and background check report, reference check notes, M365/Teams (interview scheduling), prior role description and OKR expectations for the role
**Writes to:** SharePoint (hiring file per candidate, stored under entity HR folder), Matt's email (scorecard + recommendation), Monday.com (hiring process task tracker), HR system (if applicable for entity)
**HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis reviews all scorecards before submission to Matt. Matt provides final approval for entity CEO hires in writing. Background check must be complete before offer is extended. Reference checks (minimum 3) must be complete before scorecard is submitted to Matt.

## Test Cases
- **Golden path:** Candidate scores 4.2 overall, passes Integrity, reference checks are clean, Matt approves in 48 hours, offer extended within the agreed timeline.
- **Edge case:** Candidate scores 4.5 overall but Integrity dimension triggers a kill criterion based on a reference check finding (not the interview itself) — candidate is disqualified, scorecard documents the reference finding, Dr. Lewis briefs Matt, process closes.
- **Adversarial:** A hiring manager at an entity pressures Dr. Lewis to change an Integrity score from "kill" to "pass" because the candidate is "the best we've seen" — Dr. Lewis holds the kill criterion, escalates the pressure to Matt, and documents the interaction. The hiring manager's pressure is itself a THRIVE signal worth noting.

## Audit Log
Every completed scorecard is filed in SharePoint at `/MBL Partners/Talent/Hiring Scorecards/[Entity]/[YYYY]/[Role-CandidateName]/`. The file includes: the completed scorecard PDF, reference check notes, background check report, and Matt's approval email. Disqualified candidates have their scorecards retained for 3 years minimum. Hired candidates' scorecards are retained for the duration of their employment plus 5 years.

## Deprecation
This scorecard is superseded if MBL hires a Chief People Officer or Head of Talent who owns the hiring framework. At that point, the scorecard should be handed off with a documented briefing on the Integrity kill criterion — it must survive the transition. The THRIVE dimensions and weighting should be reviewed annually in the annual portfolio review to ensure they still reflect Matt's values framework.
