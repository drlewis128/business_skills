# Portfolio Ops Quarterly Review (90-Minute Format)

The quarterly review is a 90-minute session with Matt Mathison present, all entity CEOs required, and Dr. Lewis facilitating. This is the highest-stakes recurring ops meeting in the MBL calendar — OKRs are graded, financial performance is assessed against plan, and strategic priorities are reset for the next quarter. Entity CEO attendance is mandatory; absence requires a Matt Mathison waiver granted in advance.

## When to Use
- Preparing the 90-minute agenda and facilitating the quarterly review session
- Grading OKRs across all portfolio entities and MBL HoldCo
- Generating the 2-page executive summary for Matt (portfolio scorecard + strategic narrative)
- Updating the OKR tracker, risk register, and next-quarter priorities after the session

## Quarterly Review Framework

```
QUARTERLY PORTFOLIO OPS REVIEW — 90-MINUTE STRUCTURE
=====================================================

PRE-MEETING REQUIREMENTS (Dr. Lewis, 5 business days before)
--------------------------------------------------------------
- Draft OKR grading for each entity (0.0–1.0 per OKR, with rationale)
- Pull full-quarter financials vs. plan per entity (from QuickBooks / entity finance leads)
- Update risk register (close resolved risks, escalate new ones)
- Collect M&A pipeline update from Morgan Sills (Allevio) and deal flow status
- Confirm attendance: all entity CEOs + Matt. If CEO absent, Matt must waive in writing.
- Distribute pre-read package 48 hours before the meeting

PRE-READ PACKAGE CONTENTS
--------------------------
- OKR draft grades per entity (entity CEOs may dispute before the meeting)
- Financial summary: revenue, EBITDA, cash vs. plan (full quarter)
- Risk register current state
- M&A pipeline status
- Agenda with time blocks

MEETING STRUCTURE
-----------------
[0:00–0:05]  FRAMING (Dr. Lewis)
  - Purpose of this session: grade the quarter, align on priorities, reset OKRs
  - BLUF of the quarter: one sentence per entity (portfolio health summary)
  - Norms: THRIVE filter applies — Truth first, no sandbagging

[0:05–0:20]  OKR GRADING (15 min)
  - Dr. Lewis presents draft grades; entity CEOs confirm or dispute
  - Grade scale: 0.0–1.0 (0.7 = on track; <0.5 = at risk; 1.0 = exceptional)
  - Flag any OKR that was abandoned, redefined, or missed without explanation
  - Matt has final say on disputed grades
  - Takeaway: finalized OKR scores for the quarter

[0:20–0:40]  FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE (20 min)
  - Revenue vs. plan per entity (5 min each for 4 entities)
  - EBITDA margin vs. plan
  - Cash position and runway
  - Variances >10% require explanation; variances >20% require a remediation plan
  - Matt may ask direct questions — entity CEOs must answer directly
  - No rounding up. Actual numbers only.

[0:40–1:00]  STRATEGIC PRIORITIES (20 min)
  - Each entity CEO: 4 minutes — what is the single most important strategic priority next quarter?
  - Cross-entity dependencies identified
  - Dr. Lewis flags AI/Krista.ai integration status as it affects portfolio operations
  - Matt provides directional guidance on priorities (not operational instructions)

[1:00–1:15]  M&A UPDATE (15 min)
  - Pipeline status: deals in diligence, LOIs outstanding, deals closed this quarter
  - Morgan Sills update integrated (Allevio M&A pipeline)
  - HIVE: any acquisition or JV activity
  - Matt provides go/no-go signals on any active deals
  - Confidential — not distributed outside this meeting group

[1:15–1:25]  RISK REGISTER REVIEW (10 min)
  - Dr. Lewis walks the updated risk register: high risks only (medium/low reviewed async)
  - New risks added this quarter
  - Risks resolved or downgraded
  - Matt must explicitly acknowledge any risk rated "critical"

[1:25–1:35]  NEXT QUARTER PRIORITIES (10 min)
  - Each entity states top 3 OKRs for next quarter (pre-drafted in pre-read)
  - Matt confirms or redirects
  - Dr. Lewis confirms cross-entity alignment
  - Portfolio-level OKRs set (2–3 max)

[1:35–1:40]  CLOSE
  - Dr. Lewis reads back all decisions and action items
  - Matt provides verbal close: "We are aligned" or flags remaining open items
  - Next quarterly review date confirmed

POST-MEETING DELIVERABLES (Dr. Lewis, within 24 hours)
-------------------------------------------------------
- 2-page executive summary to Matt (template below)
- Updated OKR tracker in Monday.com (all grades + next quarter OKRs)
- Updated risk register in SharePoint
- Next quarter priorities document per entity
- Meeting notes filed in SharePoint
```

## Output Contract
- The 2-page executive summary is the authoritative record of the quarter. Every OKR grade, financial variance, and strategic decision documented here is binding — if a CEO disputes a grade after this document is signed, the dispute must go through a formal correction process, not a quiet edit. This document feeds into LP reporting and board materials, so accuracy is non-negotiable. Matt's attestation signature closes the quarter officially.
- The OKR tracker update in Monday.com must reflect finalized scores, not draft scores. Each OKR entry must link to the quarterly review SharePoint document as its evidence source. Next-quarter OKRs must be entered with clear key results (measurable, time-bound) before the Monday.com update is considered complete — placeholder OKRs are not acceptable.
- The risk register update must use a consistent severity framework (critical/high/medium/low) with defined criteria, not subjective language. Any risk rated "critical" that was not on the register at the start of the quarter must have a root cause note explaining why it was not anticipated. Matt acknowledged all critical risks verbally in the meeting — this is documented in the meeting notes, not just the risk register.

## System Dependencies
**Reads from:** Monday.com (OKR tracker, prior action items), QuickBooks (entity financials), SharePoint (prior quarterly docs, risk register), Krista.ai transcripts, M&A pipeline data from Morgan Sills
**Writes to:** Monday.com (finalized OKRs, next quarter OKRs, action items), SharePoint (quarterly review doc, risk register, meeting notes), Matt's email (2-page exec summary), entity CEOs (next quarter priorities doc)
**HITL Required:** Matt Mathison reviews and signs the 2-page exec summary (attestation). Dr. Lewis does not distribute the M&A section externally. Entity CEOs must confirm their OKR grades are accurate before Matt's attestation is collected.

## Test Cases
- **Golden path:** All entity CEOs attend, pre-read distributed 48 hours prior, OKR grades finalized in the meeting, Matt signs the exec summary within 48 hours, Monday.com updated same day.
- **Edge case:** An entity CEO disputes a financial variance figure during the meeting — Dr. Lewis flags it as "under review," uses the disputed number with a footnote in the exec summary, and sets a 5-business-day deadline to resolve with the entity's finance lead.
- **Adversarial:** An entity CEO requests to regrade a poor OKR score upward without evidence — Matt has final say, and Dr. Lewis documents the request and the outcome in the meeting notes regardless of the decision, maintaining an audit trail.

## Audit Log
Each quarterly review generates a folder in SharePoint at `/MBL Partners/Portfolio Ops/Quarterly Reviews/[YYYY-Q#]/` containing: the 2-page exec summary (PDF, Matt-signed), full meeting notes, OKR grading workbook, updated risk register, and the M&A status memo (access-controlled). The Monday.com OKR tracker maintains a version history. Matt's attestation email or e-signature is retained as a separate file in the same folder. Retention: 7 years minimum.

## Deprecation
Superseded if MBL restructures its portfolio cadence (e.g., moves to biannual reviews) or if Matt delegates the quarterly review to a COO-level role. At that point, the HITL chain and attestation requirements must be re-specified for the new authority structure. The annual review skill (portops-review-annual) subsumes Q4 — do not run the quarterly and annual reviews as separate sessions for Q4.
