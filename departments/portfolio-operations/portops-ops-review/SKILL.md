# Portfolio Ops Director Self-Assessment (Dr. Lewis Quarterly Review)

This is Dr. Lewis's quarterly accountability review — an honest reckoning with what was accomplished, what was missed, and what needs to change. It covers the AI and Krista.ai initiative progress, skill library growth, tools maturity across the portfolio, and Matt Mathison's written feedback on Dr. Lewis's performance. This is not a vanity report. It exists because the portfolio ops role is high-leverage and the person in it must be held to the same standard as the entity CEOs.

## When to Use
- Preparing Dr. Lewis's quarterly self-assessment and submitting it to Matt
- Tracking AI initiative progress (Krista.ai, skill deployments, OpenFang, integrations) against quarterly goals
- Reporting skill library growth: count, coverage by entity, deployment status
- Capturing Matt's written feedback and converting it to next-quarter goals

## Output Contract
- The THRIVE self-assessment must be written before Dr. Lewis sees Matt's feedback — the sequencing is intentional. Self-assessment first ensures the honest view is documented without being shaped by what Matt thinks. If Dr. Lewis's THRIVE scores and Matt's ratings diverge significantly, that gap is itself a signal worth discussing. The Integrity dimension applies to Dr. Lewis as rigorously as to any entity CEO — a self-reported integrity flag triggers a direct conversation with Matt.
- Matt's written feedback section is reproduced verbatim in the final document. Dr. Lewis does not paraphrase, soften, or contextualize Matt's words before including them. If Matt's feedback is vague, Dr. Lewis requests specifics before closing the review — "Keep up the good work" is not an acceptable quarterly feedback response from the managing partner. The next quarter priorities are derived from Matt's feedback, not despite it.
- This review is an accountability mechanism, not a performance management exercise. It is not used to build a case for a raise, justify headcount, or demonstrate busy-ness. Its purpose is to ensure that the portfolio ops director role is creating real value for MBL and the portfolio, and that Matt has a clear, honest view of whether the AI strategy and operations work is on track. If it is not on track, this document says so and explains why.

## System Dependencies
**Reads from:** Monday.com (task completion rate, skill deployment tracking), SharePoint (prior quarterly ops reviews, meeting notes), Krista.ai (integration status and usage data), skill registry (count and coverage data), AI initiative roadmap documents
**Writes to:** SharePoint (ops review document, stored under `/MBL Partners/Portfolio Ops/Dr. Lewis Reviews/[YYYY-Q#]/`), Matt's email (self-assessment for feedback), Monday.com (next quarter priority tasks), skill registry (updated coverage counts)
**HITL Required:** Matt Mathison reviews the self-assessment and provides written feedback within 5 business days. Dr. Lewis does not close the quarterly review without Matt's feedback — an incomplete review (self-assessment only, no Matt feedback) is flagged as incomplete in SharePoint. If Matt does not respond within 5 business days, Dr. Lewis follows up directly.

## Test Cases
- **Golden path:** Self-assessment completed by day 3 post-quarter, Matt's feedback received by day 8, next quarter priorities entered in Monday.com by day 10, review filed in SharePoint with all sections complete.
- **Edge case:** Dr. Lewis identifies an Integrity flag in the self-assessment (e.g., a commitment to Matt that was not kept without prior communication) — the flag is documented explicitly, Dr. Lewis flags it verbally to Matt before Matt reads the written review, and the conversation is documented in the review file.
- **Adversarial:** Matt provides feedback that Dr. Lewis believes is factually inaccurate (e.g., crediting a missed milestone to Dr. Lewis when the blocker was an entity CEO) — Dr. Lewis reproduces Matt's feedback verbatim in the document, adds a clearly labeled "Dr. Lewis Response" section immediately after, and schedules a 30-minute sync to resolve the factual dispute. The resolution is documented in the same file.

## Audit Log
Each quarterly ops review is stored in SharePoint at `/MBL Partners/Portfolio Ops/Dr. Lewis Reviews/[YYYY-Q#]/` containing: the completed self-assessment, Matt's written feedback (verbatim), and the finalized next-quarter priorities document. The review is date-stamped at submission and at Matt's feedback receipt. Annual reviews aggregate four quarters of self-assessment data and Matt's ratings — the trend line is presented in the annual portfolio review as a portfolio ops health metric. Retention: 5 years.

## Deprecation
This skill is superseded if Dr. Lewis transitions out of the portfolio ops director role, or if MBL establishes a formal performance review process for all director-level roles that replaces this self-assessment format. If the AI initiative completes its final phase and transitions to steady-state operations, the AI progress sections should be replaced with a steady-state operations health check. The THRIVE self-assessment section should be retained in any superseding format — it is not optional.
