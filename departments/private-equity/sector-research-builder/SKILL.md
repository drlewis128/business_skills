# Sector Research Builder

Build structured sector research to support PE deal sourcing and investment decisions. MBL invests in sectors where they have operating expertise — healthcare services (Allevio), oil & gas services (HIVE), and CTV/media (Column6). Deep sector knowledge enables better deal sourcing (know which companies to target), better diligence (know what questions to ask), and better value creation (know the operational levers). This skill structures the research into an investment-relevant format.

## When to Use
- New sector being considered for investment — build the thesis
- Annual sector update — refresh knowledge on existing sectors
- Deal screen on a company in a sector not previously covered
- LP asks "what's happening in the healthcare services space?"
- Preparing for a meeting with a management team or broker in a sector

## Sector Research Framework

```
For PE investment purposes, sector research must answer:
  1. Market size and growth: Is this market big enough and growing?
  2. Competitive structure: Is this fragmented (consolidation opportunity) or concentrated?
  3. PE deal activity: Are buyers paying up? What are market multiples?
  4. Value creation patterns: What works? What are the common levers?
  5. Regulatory environment: What's the regulatory risk? What's changing?
  6. MBL relevance: Where does MBL have an edge? What companies to target?
```

## Output Contract
- Investment action always at the top — sector research that ends with "here's a lot of information" is not useful; the research must conclude with "here's what MBL should do"
- Market multiples always included — understanding what buyers are paying is essential for deal screening; vague "high multiples" statements are insufficient
- Fragmentation always quantified — PE buy-and-build strategies require fragmentation; how fragmented? what's the consolidation opportunity?
- Regulatory section always included — regulatory changes are often the most important sector dynamic for PE; missing this is a diligence failure
- MBL-specific sourcing targets always included — sector research should produce actionable sourcing leads, not just general knowledge
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis conducts research and drafts; Matt Mathison reviews sector thesis before sourcing activities; sector research may inform Matt Mathison's LP conversations — confirm before sharing externally

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Public market data, industry reports, LP/advisor conversations, portfolio company management insights
- **Writes to:** Sector research (SharePoint/Strategy/Sector Research/<Sector>); deal sourcing criteria; investment committee briefing
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis researches; Matt Mathison reviews before any sourcing activity; external sharing requires Matt Mathison direction

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Healthcare services sector deep dive → Market: $X billion, 6% CAGR; Fragmented: top 10 players = 25% share; Multiples: 5-8× EBITDA (platform), 4-5× (tuck-in); Value creation: billing optimization (10-15%), technology (AI scheduling), M&A consolidation; Regulatory: HIPAA compliance is table stakes; CMS reimbursement rate changes are key; Sourcing: 3 specific Arizona/Mountain West companies identified for direct outreach; MBL action: focus sourcing on $5-15M EBITDA owner-operated regional healthcare services in AZ/CO
2. **Edge case:** MBL asks for sector research on a sector with very few PE comparables (e.g., a niche specialty chemical market) → use the available data; acknowledge the thin comp set; rely more heavily on market fundamentals (growth drivers, fragmentation) and less on PE deal multiples; draw analogies to comparable sectors; flag the limited data as a diligence risk — thin sector data means more reliance on company-specific diligence
3. **Adversarial:** A broker provides MBL with their own "sector research" that is a marketing document designed to justify a high asking multiple → treat broker research as a starting point, not a conclusion; verify key claims (market size, growth rate, deal multiples) against independent sources; broker-provided research has an inherent bias toward making the deal look attractive; always build independent sector research, especially on multiples

## Audit Log
Sector research retained by date and version. Data sources documented. Matt Mathison review noted. Sourcing actions taken based on sector research logged in deal pipeline.

## Deprecation
Retire when MBL has dedicated research resources (PitchBook, CapIQ, sector analysts, or research staff) providing live sector intelligence for the deal team.
