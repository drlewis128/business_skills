# CS Onboarding Plan

Build the 90-day onboarding plan for new CS team members at MBL portfolio companies — giving every new CS hire a structured path from their first day to full account ownership, with clear milestones, defined learning objectives, and a weekly coaching cadence from Dr. Lewis. The onboarding plan sets the rep's expectations, builds the skills they need for this specific context (Allevio benefits, Column6 CTV, HIVE minerals), and establishes the work habits that will define their performance.

## When to Use
- A new CSM or CS Lead accepts an offer (build before their first day)
- A CS rep is struggling and needs a reset to fundamentals (restart the onboarding sequence)
- A backfill hire is taking over an existing book of accounts (accelerated version)
- Reviewing the onboarding plan at 30/60/90 day milestones

## CS Onboarding Framework

```
PHILOSOPHY:
  The goal of the 90-day onboarding is not to introduce the rep to all accounts —
    it is to make them ready to own accounts independently with Dr. Lewis oversight
  Week 1 is learning; Month 1 is observing; Month 2 is contributing; Month 3 is owning
  Dr. Lewis coaches every new CS rep weekly for the first 90 days, then monthly after that
  
  Warm handoff is non-negotiable: every account transition includes a call with the previous
    owner and the customer; accounts are never transferred without a customer introduction
    
WEEK 1 — ORIENTATION AND FOUNDATIONS:
  Day 1:
    □ IT setup complete (Outlook; Teams; GoHighLevel CRM; SharePoint; entity-specific systems)
    □ Dr. Lewis 1:1 introduction (90 minutes):
        What MBL does and what CS does at MBL
        The role of CS in protecting and growing ARR
        Dr. Lewis's standards: proactive; data-driven; relationship-first
        How we'll work together: weekly coaching cadence; escalation protocol
    □ Portfolio entity overview: Allevio; HIVE Partners; Column6/Siprocal
    □ Read: CLAUDE.md entity vocabulary section
    □ CRM orientation: how accounts are structured; how to log activity
    
  Days 2-3:
    □ Deep dive on the primary entity (the entity they'll serve):
        Allevio: employer group benefits; PMPM pricing; AdvancedMD basics; claim ratio model
        Column6: CTV advertising; IO process; VCR and CPM basics; agency relationships
        HIVE: mineral rights leasing; royalty calculation; Covercy portal; Uinta Basin context
    □ Shadow 1 internal meeting (operations; billing; implementation) to understand delivery
    □ Read: 3 customer contracts (how are our agreements structured?)
    
  Days 4-5:
    □ CS skills overview: cs-health-score-tracker; cs-success-plan-builder; cs-qbr-preparer
    □ Shadow Dr. Lewis on a customer call (observe the relationship dynamic)
    □ GoHighLevel CRM: full account structure walkthrough for 3 accounts
    □ Week 1 debrief with Dr. Lewis (Friday): What did you learn? What questions do you have?
    □ Week 1 assignment: Write a 1-page summary of how Allevio/Column6/HIVE creates value for customers

MONTH 1 — OBSERVE AND ASSIST:
  Week 2-3: Shadow phase
    □ Shadow Dr. Lewis on 2-3 customer calls (different account types — Green; Yellow; new)
    □ Shadow a QBR (observe the structure; the data; the conversation)
    □ Read all active success plans in the portfolio (cs-success-plan-builder)
    □ Build a mock health score for 3 accounts using cs-health-score-tracker (Dr. Lewis validates)
    □ Review 3 onboarding trackers (cs-onboarding-coordinator) — understand the milestone structure
    □ Interview 2 customers (with Dr. Lewis intro): "Tell me about your experience with us so far"
    
  Week 4: Assisted ownership
    □ Manage Day 1 communications for 1 new account (with Dr. Lewis review before sending)
    □ Build a health score update for 5 accounts (Dr. Lewis spot-checks 3)
    □ Prepare the agenda for 1 QBR (with Dr. Lewis review before sending to customer)
    □ Month 1 debrief (Dr. Lewis coaching session): What's clear? What's still confusing?
    □ 30-day milestone assessment (see Milestone Tracker below)

MONTH 2 — CONTRIBUTE AND DEVELOP:
  Week 5-6: First independent actions
    □ Own 5 accounts fully (Green accounts; low complexity)
    □ Conduct 1 QBR independently (Dr. Lewis shadow — observes without participating)
    □ Update health scores for all 5 owned accounts weekly (Dr. Lewis spot-checks)
    □ Handle 1 P3 escalation independently (Dr. Lewis reviews response before it's sent)
    □ Build a success plan for 1 new account (Dr. Lewis reviews)
    
  Week 7-8: Expanding scope
    □ Own 10-12 accounts (adding Yellow accounts with Dr. Lewis oversight)
    □ Participate in a renewal planning conversation (observe Dr. Lewis; then handle the prep)
    □ Conduct 2 more independent QBRs (Dr. Lewis no longer shadows)
    □ Build month 2 churn risk assessment for owned portfolio
    □ Month 2 debrief: Are they ready to own the full portfolio by Day 90?

MONTH 3 — FULL RAMP:
  Week 9-12: Full portfolio ownership
    □ Own the full assigned portfolio (15-25 accounts) with Dr. Lewis oversight
    □ All QBRs conducted independently
    □ Health scores updated weekly; Dr. Lewis reviews changes only (not all updates)
    □ Renewal coordination: fully owns Day -120 to Day -60 process; Dr. Lewis joins Day -45
    □ Expansion: surfaces opportunities to Dr. Lewis for review; Dr. Lewis joins expansion conversations
    □ Day 90 review: formal assessment against cs-hiring-scorecard dimensions
    □ Development plan: What does this rep need to reach the next level?

ENTITY-SPECIFIC LEARNING (Month 1 focus):

  ALLEVIO:
    □ Learn AdvancedMD: how to pull claim ratio data; member count; enrollment status
    □ Understand HIPAA basics: what is PHI? What can we share with the employer? What can't we?
    □ Learn the Allevio open enrollment calendar — which months are high-touch?
    □ Understand the claims process: what happens when a member files a claim?
    □ Shadow a clinical care coordination call (observe — HIPAA briefed first)
    
  COLUMN6:
    □ Learn the Column6 IO process: from signed IO to first impression (5-day timeline)
    □ Understand VCR, CPM, CPA, attribution — pass a written quiz by end of Month 1
    □ Learn the Column6 campaign reporting dashboard: where to find performance data
    □ Understand brand safety: what are blocklists? What happens in a brand safety incident?
    □ Shadow a media buyer conversation
    
  HIVE:
    □ Learn the mineral rights leasing process: LOI → title search → lease execution → royalty
    □ Understand Covercy: how landowners access their royalty statements
    □ Learn the Uinta Basin geography and development activity context
    □ Shadow a landowner call (observe the tone; the questions; the communication style)

BACKFILL ACCELERATION (for hire taking over existing accounts):
  Compress Week 1-2 learning; prioritize customer introductions in the first 2 weeks
  Dr. Lewis joins the first customer call for every account >$100K ACV being transitioned
  Warm handoff call with outgoing CSM: every Tier A account; top 50% of Tier B accounts
  Urgent health score audit: on Day 1, review the health scores for all accounts being transitioned —
    flag any Yellow or Red accounts to Dr. Lewis before the rep takes the first customer call
```

## Output Contract
- Week 1 is not productive work — the first week is pure learning; Dr. Lewis does not assign accounts or customer-facing work in Week 1; a new CS rep thrown into customer calls before they understand the product, pricing, and systems will damage customer relationships; Week 1 ends with the 1-page summary assignment, which confirms the rep processed what they learned
- The shadow phase is non-negotiable — Dr. Lewis requires every new CS rep to shadow at least 3 customer calls before making their first independent customer contact; skipping shadows to "get to productivity faster" creates reps who don't sound like MBL and miss the nuances that training documents can't capture
- Day 90 review drives the development plan — the 90-day review uses the cs-hiring-scorecard dimensions to assess where the rep is strong and where they need development; a rep who scores below 3.5 on any dimension at Day 90 goes on a targeted development plan; a rep below 3.0 on any dimension at Day 90 gets a formal performance discussion with the CEO
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis runs the Day 1 intro and all weekly coaching sessions for the first 90 days; all customer-facing communications in Month 1 reviewed by Dr. Lewis before sending; Day 90 review shared with CEO; any backfill hire taking accounts >$100K ACV — Dr. Lewis joins the first customer call; below-3.0 Day 90 scores trigger a CEO conversation

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** New Allevio CSM starts Monday — IT setup by 10am; Dr. Lewis 90-minute intro; first week: shadow 2 customer calls; read 3 contracts; take AdvancedMD training; Day 30: health scores for 5 accounts all within 1 of Dr. Lewis's scores; Day 60: owns 8 accounts; first independent QBR — Dr. Lewis debriefs: "Opening question perfect; data presentation too report-like — convert it to a conversation"; Day 90: full 22-account portfolio; formal review: Relationships 4.5; Data 4.2; Domain 3.8; Process 4.5; development plan: executive presence for CFO-level QBRs
2. **Edge case:** New CSM asks to own 15 accounts by Day 45 — "I learn by doing" → Dr. Lewis: "I love the drive. I'm moving you to 10 accounts — including 2 Yellow and 1 post-escalation account so you see the full range. If the 10-account management goes smoothly for 3 weeks, we move to the full portfolio by Day 70." Rep manages 10 well; escalation occurs Day 62 (P3); handled correctly; full portfolio Day 75; Day 90 review strong
3. **Adversarial:** Backfill hire: outgoing CSM wants to just email the account list to the new hire without customer introductions — "it'll be disruptive to the customers" → Dr. Lewis: "The disruption of a cold transfer is worse than the disruption of an introduction. Customers who suddenly get a new CSM with no warning feel abandoned. Warm handoffs protect the relationship. I'll join the first calls for every Priority A account. For Priority B: a brief email from the outgoing CSM that says 'meet your new CS contact' is the minimum." All warm handoffs completed; no Yellow accounts from the transition

## Deprecation
Retire when each portfolio company has a dedicated CS enablement program managed by a CS Lead or CS Ops function — with Dr. Lewis reviewing the onboarding program annually and the CEO approving any structural changes.
