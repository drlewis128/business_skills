# Sales Column6 Agency Pitch

Pitch Column6/Siprocal to media agencies and DSP buyers — delivering a concise, evidence-based pitch that moves agencies from "who are you?" to "let's talk about a test IO." The Column6 pitch is not a product demo; it's a conversation about the agency's current CTV supply challenges and a specific, credible offer to be better. Every pitch should close for a test IO — the smallest unit of commitment that proves Column6's value with the agency's own campaign.

## When to Use
- Preparing for a first call or introductory meeting with a new agency
- Running a discovery pitch call with a new media buyer
- Building the Column6 pitch narrative for a new market
- Coaching a Column6 AE on how to open an agency conversation

## Column6 Agency Pitch Framework

```
COLUMN6 PITCH PHILOSOPHY:
  Lead with their problem, not our product
  Quantify: use numbers wherever possible (IVT %; VCR %; CPM comparison)
  Test IO offer: always close the pitch with a concrete next step (test IO at $25K minimum)
  Short: 15-minute discovery call first; product conversations follow after interest is confirmed
  
COLUMN6 CORE PITCH NARRATIVE (30 seconds):
  "Column6 is a premium CTV, Mobile, and Gaming supply partner. 
  What separates us from open exchange: our measured IVT rate is [X]%, our VCR is [X]%, 
  and we guarantee 100% delivery — meaning we make good if we underdeliver, not just best effort. 
  Most agencies we talk to are getting [industry benchmark IVT] from their current supply. 
  If we can demonstrate better quality in a test IO, we want to earn your business."
  
COLUMN6 DIFFERENTIATION PILLARS:
  1. IVT quality: [X]% measured IVT vs. industry open exchange benchmark [Y-Z]%
     Translation: "You're paying for impressions that never delivered. We don't sell you those impressions."
     
  2. Delivery guarantee: 100% delivery commitment with make-good policy
     Translation: "Best effort means you take the risk. We take the risk."
     
  3. Viewability: ≥70% viewability; in-stream VCR ≥85%
     Translation: "Your client's KPIs are our delivery standards — not aspirational."
     
  4. Cross-platform CTV reach: CTV + Mobile + Gaming inventory
     Translation: "Reach your audience across the devices they actually watch on — not just the big screen."
     
  5. White-glove partnership: Column6 AE is an extension of the agency team
     Translation: "You're not buying from a platform. You're partnering with people who care about your campaign."
     
PITCH STRUCTURE BY CALL TYPE:

  Intro call (15 minutes — first contact, agreed to a call):
    Minutes 0-3: Introduction + why we reached out (reference IVT issue or specific signal)
    Minutes 3-8: Discovery question: "How is your current CTV supply performing? IVT? VCR? Delivery?"
    Minutes 8-12: Column6 position: "Here's what we see differently..."
    Minutes 12-15: Test IO offer: "Would a $25K test IO with full performance comparison be worth exploring?"
    Goal: schedule a 30-minute follow-up or get a test IO commitment
    
  Discovery pitch (30 minutes — agency showed interest; deeper conversation):
    Minutes 0-5: Discovery: campaign objectives; current supply performance; pain points
    Minutes 5-15: Column6 narrative: tailor to their specific pain (IVT → quality; delivery → guarantee; VCR → viewability)
    Minutes 15-22: Case study or proof point: comparable agency or campaign type; aggregate performance (no other agency's data)
    Minutes 22-28: Test IO structure: "For a test, we'd suggest [campaign type]; [impressions]; [CPM]; [flight dates]. Let me build you a proposal."
    Minutes 28-30: Next step: proposal by [date]; IO review call [date]
    
  Follow-up pitch (post-test IO, re-pitch for expansion):
    Lead with their campaign results first: "Your test IO delivered [results]"
    Frame the expansion: "Based on what you saw, here's what a larger IO could look like for Q[X]"
    
QUALIFYING QUESTIONS (discovery-first):
  "What percentage of your current CTV buys are on open exchange vs. premium supply?"
  "What IVT rate are you seeing from your current supply?"
  "Has your client ever pushed back on viewability or VCR performance?"
  "Do you have any campaigns this quarter where delivery guarantee matters?"
  [Listen: if they don't know their IVT rate, they've never measured it — that's the opening]
  
PITCH DO's AND DON'Ts:
  DO: ask about their current supply before pitching; use their numbers in your pitch
  DO: offer a specific test IO with a specific price point ($25K minimum)
  DO: reference Column6's measured performance data (not estimates)
  DO: acknowledge what open exchange does well (scale; price) before pivoting to quality
  DO NOT: disparage competitors by name; stay on value comparison
  DO NOT: pitch everything Column6 does in the first call; one clear differentiator is better than five
  DO NOT: close a first call asking for a $500K IO; earn trust with the test
  
POST-PITCH FOLLOW-UP (within 24 hours):
  Email subject: "Column6 | Test IO outline — [Agency]"
  Content:
    3-line summary of what was discussed (their pain + Column6's position)
    Test IO outline: campaign type; estimated impressions; CPM; flight window; investment
    CTA: "Happy to have a proposal ready by [date] — does [next Thursday] work for a 20-minute IO review?"
  Attach: Column6 one-pager (performance benchmarks; differentiation; delivery guarantee)
```

## Output Contract
- The pitch closes for a test IO, not for a relationship — "let's stay in touch" is not a next step; a 15-minute intro call that ends with "sounds interesting, send me some information" is a loss unless the AE schedules a specific follow-up; the test IO offer ($25K minimum; specific campaign type; specific flight dates) is the concrete next step that converts a conversation into a pipeline entry; the test IO is designed to be low-risk for the agency ("you can compare our performance against your current supply") and high-signal for Column6 ("if we deliver on the test, we earn the larger relationship"); every Column6 pitch should close with a test IO offer; if the agency declines even a $25K test, the qualification question is whether they're actually willing to test alternatives to their current supply
- Column6's performance claims must be current measured data — a Column6 AE who says "our IVT rate is 2%" without being able to back it up with measured campaign data destroys credibility the moment the agency asks "can you prove that?"; Dr. Lewis validates all Column6 performance claims against actual campaign data before they're used in pitches; the pitch prep sheet includes the current measured IVT rate; VCR benchmark; and viewability percentage — sourced from Column6's operations team; a claim that is 6 months old without being refreshed against recent campaign data should not be in a pitch without a date qualifier
- HITL required: Column6 performance data in pitch → entity CEO or ops team confirms current measured data before AE uses in pitch; test IO offer at >$50K → entity CEO aware before AE commits; gross margin check → entity CEO before test IO turns into a proposal (margin ≥35%); large agency intro meeting (>$500K potential ACV) → entity CEO may join; post-pitch one-pager → entity CEO reviews and approves current version; competitor comparisons → Dr. Lewis validates claims before AE uses in pitch

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** New agency — 30-person independent media agency in Chicago; $300K/quarter CTV spend. Intro call (15 min): AE leads with "We saw you're active in CTV — what's your current IVT experience?" Media buyer: "Honestly, we don't measure it carefully. We're on Trade Desk open exchange and it's mostly fine." AE: "Most agencies we work with say the same — until they measure it and find they're at 8-12% IVT. That means 1 in 10 impressions never actually delivered. Column6's measured IVT is [X]%. Would you be open to running a $25K test IO so you can see the comparison firsthand with your campaign?" Media buyer: "Yeah, that sounds fair — let's talk about what a test would look like." Next step: AE builds test IO proposal (30-minute follow-up in 3 days). Post-pitch email sent same day. Margin check: ≥35% ✅. Entity CEO aware of test IO offer.
2. **Edge case:** Media buyer says "We're locked into our DSP and can't run IOs directly — everything goes through our DSP" → Dr. Lewis coaching: "That's not a closed door. Options: (1) Column6 can integrate with most DSPs — ask which one they're on and confirm with our ops team whether Column6 inventory is accessible programmatically through that DSP; (2) If they can only buy programmatically, the value proposition shifts from IO-based to supply-quality-based — 'Column6 inventory is available on [their DSP] and you can target it directly; the IVT and viewability standards still apply.' (3) If their DSP doesn't support direct access to Column6: 'I'd like to get our ops team on a call with your ad ops team to explore what a technical path looks like.' This is a discovery outcome, not a rejection — update GHL with the DSP information and flag for ops team."
3. **Adversarial:** AE wants to use another agency's campaign data in the Column6 pitch: "Agency X got [specific results] — can I reference that in my pitch to Agency Y?" → Dr. Lewis: "You can reference Column6's aggregate performance benchmarks — those are not agency-specific. You cannot reference Agency X's specific campaign results without their written authorization to use their data in sales conversations. Agencies trust Column6 with their campaign data because we keep it confidential — if Agency Y learns that Column6 is sharing other agencies' campaign specifics in sales pitches, the trust is gone. Use the aggregate: 'Across our CTV campaigns in [quarter], we averaged [X]% IVT, [Y]% VCR, and [Z]% viewability.' That's compelling and it's compliant with our client confidentiality standards."
