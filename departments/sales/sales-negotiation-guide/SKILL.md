# Sales Negotiation Guide

Guide negotiation tactics and concession management for MBL portfolio company sales teams — providing entity-specific negotiation strategies, concession authority matrices, and principled frameworks for closing deals at terms that work for both sides. Negotiation is not about winning; it is about landing a deal that both parties honor and that supports a long-term relationship. A deal won through pressure tactics is a churned customer 12 months later.

## When to Use
- Entering a pricing or terms discussion with a prospect
- Responding to a discount request or concession ask
- Determining concession authority before making an offer
- Preparing the final concession structure before a close meeting

## Negotiation Framework

```
NEGOTIATION PRINCIPLES:
  Interest-based: understand what's behind the ask ("why do they want a lower price?")
  Value-anchored: defend on value, not just cost; don't race to the concession
  Trade for trade: never give something for nothing; every concession has a reciprocal ask
  Authority ladder: know your authority before you're asked for a concession
  BATNA clarity: know your walk-away point before you start; negotiation without BATNA is guessing
  
CONCESSION AUTHORITY MATRIX (universal across entities):
  <5% price reduction: entity CEO discretion (can approve unilaterally)
  5-15% price reduction: entity CEO + Dr. Lewis approval before offering
  >15% price reduction: entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison approval before offering
  
  Non-price concessions (payment terms; scope; timeline):
    Standard terms modification (net 30 → net 45): entity CEO approves
    Non-standard terms (net 60+; custom reporting; SLA changes): entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
    Structural changes (contract length; exit clause; exclusivity): entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison
    
ALLEVIO NEGOTIATION CONTEXT:
  Price anchor: PMPM rate; 12-month minimum; implementation timeline
  Most common asks:
    Lower PMPM: "Can you come down on the monthly rate?"
    Shorter term: "We want a 6-month pilot instead of 12 months"
    Exit clause: "We want the right to exit without penalty at 6 months"
    Delayed start: "We want to start after open enrollment, not now"
    
  Allevio negotiation responses:
    Lower PMPM: "Our pricing is based on your enrolled employee count — here's what drives the rate.
      If you're committed to a multi-year renewal upfront, I can explore a rate adjustment with our team.
      What would a 2-year commitment look like for you?" [trade: rate for term]
    Shorter term: "A 6-month pilot doesn't give Allevio enough time to show the claims trend impact — 
      the data we've shared shows the trend reduction is strongest in months 9-12. A 6-month evaluation 
      would show utilization but not the cost story. What if we did 12 months with a 90-day performance 
      review at month 3 and month 6 so you have structured check-ins?"
    Exit clause: "We can discuss a 60-day notice window for non-renewal rather than the standard 90 days 
      — that's within what I can offer. A full exit-at-any-time clause is something I'd need to bring 
      to our leadership team, and honestly it changes the risk model in a way that affects pricing."
    
  Allevio PMPM floor: entity CEO sets the floor; no quote below floor without entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
  Contract length minimum: 12 months; no exceptions without entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison
  
COLUMN6 NEGOTIATION CONTEXT:
  Price anchor: CPM rate; IO total value; flight dates
  Most common asks:
    Lower CPM: "Your CPM is higher than open exchange"
    Better payment terms: net 45 or 60 instead of net 30
    Make-good guarantee: "What if you underdeliver?"
    Value-add: "Can you throw in additional impressions?"
    
  Column6 negotiation responses:
    Lower CPM: "The effective CPM comparison is the right frame — let me show you Column6's effective CPM
      after adjusting for IVT rate: at [X]% IVT on open exchange, you're paying CPM for impressions 
      that never delivered. Our effective CPM at our IVT rate is [X] — let me pull the numbers for your 
      current supply vs. Column6 side by side." [defend on value; then offer]
    Payment terms: net 45 is within entity CEO discretion; net 60+ requires entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
    Make-good: standard make-good policy (underdelivery >10% → credit or next IO) is already in the IO
    Value-add impressions: give impressions only when margin allows — confirm margin floor first
    
  Column6 CPM floor: gross margin ≥35% on every IO; no concession below floor without entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
  
HIVE NEGOTIATION CONTEXT:
  HIVE acquisitions: LOI price + payment structure + close timeline
  Most common asks:
    Higher price: "We think the interest is worth more"
    Different payment structure: upfront vs. installment; contingency on production
    Longer diligence period: "We need more time to decide"
    
  HIVE negotiation responses:
    Higher price: "Our acquisition model is based on current production and strip pricing — let me share 
      the model with the seller so they can see the math. If they have information that changes the 
      production estimate, we'd like to see it."
    Payment structure: Matt Mathison decides all HIVE payment structure decisions — not the deal sourcer
    Diligence: longer diligence is fine; LOI price is contingent on verified production; price may be 
      revised if production statement differs from estimate
    
  HIVE LOI negotiation: Dr. Lewis prepares; Matt Mathison decides all pricing and structure
  
TRADING CONCESSIONS:
  Every concession you give should have a reciprocal ask:
    "If I can come down on the PMPM to $X, I'd need a 24-month commitment rather than 12."
    "If I can extend payment terms to net 45, I'd need the IO signed by [date]."
    "If I can add 500K impressions, I need the campaign to start by [date] so we can plan inventory."
  "Yes, and..." beats "no" and beats unconditional "yes"
  
WALK-AWAY FRAMEWORK:
  Know your BATNA: if this deal doesn't close, what happens? (Another prospect in pipeline? Quota impact?)
  Walk-away point: price floor (set by entity CEO) + non-negotiable terms (contract length; margin floor)
  If prospect pushes below walk-away: "I've done everything I can at this price point and these terms. 
    If this doesn't work for your budget, I understand — and I hope we can find a way to work together 
    in the future when timing aligns." [Be comfortable with the silence that follows]
```

## Output Contract
- Concession authority must be confirmed before the rep is in the negotiation room — a rep who says "let me see what I can do" and then comes back with a concession offer that hadn't been approved has committed the entity to terms that the entity CEO and Dr. Lewis didn't authorize; the concession authority matrix is enforced before any negotiation call, not during it; if the prospect springs a surprise concession request, the rep's answer is "let me bring that back to my team — I'll have an answer for you by [tomorrow/end of week]"; that's not weakness; that's not giving away value without a plan; the right concession, offered at the right moment, closes deals; the wrong concession, offered out of pressure, trains the prospect that pushing works and sets up a pattern for the entire relationship
- Every concession must have a reciprocal ask — Column6 reps who cut the CPM without asking for something in return (a longer flight; a larger IO; a faster close; a public case study) have reduced revenue with no corresponding value; Allevio reps who shorten the contract length without asking for a renewal commitment or a higher PMPM have created a less sustainable deal; the principle is not that trades always happen — sometimes the prospect can't give anything; but the act of asking for a reciprocal value anchors the fact that concessions are not free; it preserves the perception of value even when the trade doesn't materialize
- HITL required: <5% concession → entity CEO approves before offering; 5-15% → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis before offering; >15% → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison before offering; non-standard terms (net 60+; contract structure change; SLA) → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis; structural changes → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison; HIVE LOI pricing → Matt Mathison decides; Column6 margin floor concession → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis before IO countersign; any concession offer communicated to prospect → entity CEO is aware before the rep communicates it

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio prospect asks for 5% PMPM reduction ("Your rate is $75 — can you come to $71?"). Rep applies framework: (1) Interests: "Why are they asking? Budget constraint or testing?" — rep asks: "Help me understand — is the rate the primary concern, or is there a specific budget number we need to hit?" Prospect: "Our benefits budget is $85K for the year; at $75 PMPM with 95 enrolled employees we're at $85.5K — just barely over." (2) Concession analysis: 5% is $71.25 PMPM; entity CEO authority. (3) Reciprocal: at this size, offer $73 PMPM (3% reduction) in exchange for 18-month commitment vs. 12 months. (4) Confirm entity CEO approval before call. (5) Rep offer: "At $73 PMPM with an 18-month commitment, your annual investment is $83,106 — that fits your budget, and it gives Allevio time to show you the full cost trend impact." Prospect: "That works." Entity CEO countersigns at new terms.
2. **Edge case:** Column6 prospect pushes CPM below the gross margin floor mid-negotiation ("We need $14 CPM to make this work, not $18") → Rep: "Let me pull up the numbers — at $14 CPM I need to check our margin position before I can make any commitment on that." Rep pauses; confirms margin at $14: 24% — below 35% floor. Rep: "I've looked at the math and $14 CPM isn't workable at this IO size. What I can offer is $16.50 CPM — that's my floor, and I'm confirming that with my team right now so I can be confident. What I'd ask in return: can we get the IO signed by [date] so we can lock in the inventory?" Entity CEO + Dr. Lewis notified: $16.50 CPM approved; margin 36% ✅. Offer made.
3. **Adversarial:** Rep offers a 20% PMPM discount verbally in the negotiation call without checking authority first ("I'm sure we can get to $60 PMPM for you") → Dr. Lewis: "That verbal offer is a problem — 20% is above the entity CEO authority threshold; it required entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison approval before it was communicated. Here's the recovery: (1) Call the prospect today: 'I got ahead of myself yesterday — I need to confirm that pricing with our leadership team before I commit. I'll have a confirmed number for you by [two days].' (2) Bring the request to entity CEO + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison immediately: what's the floor? What's the business case for 20%? (3) If the floor is higher than $60: the rep will need to deliver a different number than what they said — this is recoverable if done quickly and transparently, but it erodes trust. This is why the authority matrix exists — use it before the call, not after."
