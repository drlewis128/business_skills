# Sales Cold Outreach Writer

Write cold outreach emails, LinkedIn messages, and prospecting copy for MBL portfolio company sales teams — producing outreach that earns a reply by being specific, relevant, and short. Cold outreach that doesn't convert is waste: of the rep's time, of the entity's reputation, and of the prospect's goodwill. The rep who sends one targeted, specific email beats the rep who sends 50 generic ones.

## When to Use
- Writing or reviewing a cold outreach email or message for any entity
- Building a multi-touch outreach sequence
- Coaching a rep on outreach quality
- Creating a template library for a new ICP segment

## Cold Outreach Framework

```
OUTREACH PRINCIPLES:
  1. One job per message: get a reply; not to explain the full product
  2. Specific beats generic: "I noticed your benefits renewal is in November" beats "I help companies"
  3. Short beats long: 3-5 sentences max for email; 2-3 for LinkedIn
  4. Lead with their situation; close with a low-friction ask
  5. No attachments on cold touch 1 — attachments go in Touch 3 or after a reply
  6. Personalization > volume: 10 personalized emails outperform 100 generic ones
  
ALLEVIO COLD OUTREACH:

  HIPAA: cold outreach to employers does NOT reference individual employee health conditions
    "Your employees" is fine; "your high-utilization diabetic population" is not
    Copy referencing specific health conditions triggers HIPAA review before sending
    Entity CEO approves all cold outreach templates before the rep sends to prospects
    
  Target: HR Director; CEO; CFO of self-insured employers 75-500 employees in AZ
  
  Trigger events (best times to reach out):
    Benefits renewal window (60-90 days before renewal)
    New HR Director (recently hired — likely reviewing benefits)
    Recent benefits news in the local market (rate increase announcements; insurer exits)
    
  Touch 1 (Email):
    Subject: [Their company] + benefits renewal + [month]
    Example: "Acme Manufacturing — November renewal"
    Body (3 sentences):
      Line 1: "I noticed [Company]'s benefits renewal is in November — reaching out now because most
               HR Directors I work with are already feeling the pressure from claims trends heading
               into renewal."
      Line 2: "We work with self-insured employers in your size range in AZ to reduce healthcare
               cost trend by 30-40% in Year 1 — not through benefit cuts, but through better primary
               care access."
      Line 3: "Worth a 15-minute call to see if this applies to your situation? Happy to work around
               your schedule."
    
  Touch 2 (LinkedIn + Email):
    LinkedIn connect message: "Sent you an email about your benefits renewal — wanted to connect
                               here as well in case this channel is easier."
    Follow-up email (5 days after Touch 1):
      "Following up from last week — I know HR Directors are slammed at this time of year.
       One quick question: is your claims trend a concern heading into November renewal, or
       are you in a good spot? Even a one-word reply helps me know if my timing is right."
       
  Touch 3 (Value add):
    Send a specific piece of value (Allevio case study — anonymous; or industry benchmark)
    "Attaching our anonymized results from a similar-size AZ employer who reduced their claims
     trend from 11% to 4% in Year 1. Thought this might be useful as you're working on renewal.
     Happy to walk through the methodology if it's relevant."
     
  Touch 4 (Phone):
    Brief voicemail: "Hi [Name], this is [Rep] from Allevio. I've emailed a couple of times about
                     your benefits renewal — I'll keep this short. If reducing your healthcare cost
                     trend heading into November is on your radar, I think we can help. My number is
                     [X]. Happy to connect for 15 minutes at your convenience."
                     
  Touch 5 (Break-up):
    "I've reached out a few times without hearing back — which usually means one of two things:
     either the timing is off, or this just isn't relevant to your current situation. Either is
     completely fine. I'll stop reaching out unless you want to reconnect.
     If your benefits renewal does come up as a challenge, I'm happy to have a conversation then.
     All the best — [Rep]"
     
COLUMN6 COLD OUTREACH:

  Target: media buyers; VP media at agencies with active CTV spend ≥$100K/quarter
  
  Trigger events:
    New campaign brief in the market (agency's client announced a campaign)
    Agency announced a new client win (they'll have new media to plan)
    Industry event (follow-up to a conference)
    
  Touch 1 (Email):
    Subject: CTV supply + [Agency name] + delivery rate
    Body (3 sentences):
      "Most media buyers I work with at [Agency size] agencies are dealing with the same problem:
       they're buying CTV on open exchange and questioning whether the impressions are actually
       reaching viewers — IVT and delivery issues."
      "Column6 guarantees 100% delivery and our measured IVT is [X]% — we prove it in the campaign
       report, not just in the pitch."
      "Happy to show you our delivery dashboard on a 15-minute call. Would Thursday work?"
       
  Touch 3+ same structure as Allevio — value add (performance data); phone; break-up
  
HIVE COLD OUTREACH:

  Target: mineral rights owners in Uinta Basin (county recorder research)
  Channel: formal letter (NOT email; NOT LinkedIn — formal correspondence only)
  HIPAA/sensitivity: these are often elderly individuals or heirs; tone must be respectful and non-pressuring
  
  Touch 1 (Formal letter):
    "Dear [Name],
    My name is [Name] with HIVE Partners. We are a mineral rights acquisition firm focused on
    producing royalty interests in the Uinta Basin, Utah.
    Your name appears in public property records as a holder of mineral rights in [County]. We
    are interested in learning whether you would consider selling your interest.
    If you are open to a conversation, please reach out at your convenience: [phone] or [email].
    There is no obligation, and we are happy to answer any questions about our acquisition process.
    Sincerely, [Name], HIVE Partners"
  
  Content rules:
    No pressure; no "limited time"; no comparison to "other offers"
    No language that could be read as threatening or deceptive
    Dr. Lewis reviews all HIVE outreach letter content before sending
    Matt Mathison approves ALL HIVE LOIs; outreach is not an LOI — letters are sourcing only
    
OUTREACH QUALITY CHECKLIST:
  ☐ 3-5 sentences (email); 2-3 sentences (LinkedIn); formal (HIVE letter)
  ☐ First sentence mentions something specific to them (not generic)
  ☐ No product features in Touch 1 — only their situation + one outcome
  ☐ Low-friction ask (15-minute call; not "schedule a 45-minute demo")
  ☐ No attachment in Touch 1
  ☐ HIPAA: no individual health condition references (Allevio)
  ☐ Entity CEO approves template before first use
  ☐ HIVE: Dr. Lewis reviews letter content
  ☐ Personal email from rep (not a marketing tool blast)
```

## Output Contract
- Cold outreach copy is approved by entity CEO before it is sent — not reviewed after a rep has already used it for 200 prospects; the entity's brand reputation is in every cold email the rep sends; a bad cold email from an Allevio rep doesn't just fail to get a reply — it can close the door with that HR Director for years; entity CEO approval on templates means the rep sends with confidence and the entity has control over brand voice; templates that are approved once are approved for that ICP segment and channel; new segment or new channel → new approval
- HIPAA applies to cold outreach even though the prospect hasn't signed anything — an Allevio email that says "employers like yours with high-utilization chronic disease populations" is an assumption about the employer's workforce health that uses language that could be construed as referencing employee health data; the safe language is employer-level: "companies like yours where the healthcare cost trend is putting pressure on the benefits budget"; no reference to specific health conditions, disease states, or clinical outcomes in any cold outreach; if a rep is unsure whether their outreach language is HIPAA-safe, Dr. Lewis reviews before sending
- HITL required: Allevio outreach templates → entity CEO approves before first use; HIPAA language check → Dr. Lewis reviews any outreach that references health conditions or employee health; HIVE outreach letters → Dr. Lewis reviews content before sending; HIVE LOI → separate from outreach; Matt Mathison approves LOI (not Dr. Lewis); mass outreach (email blast >50 recipients at once) → entity CEO approves before sending; outreach mentioning specific competitor by name → entity CEO reviews for accuracy; any outreach that includes a case study attachment → entity CEO confirms the case study is approved for use

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Rep has an Allevio prospect — 180-employee self-insured manufacturing firm in Phoenix; benefits renewal in October. Touch 1 email: Subject: "Acme Industries — October benefits renewal." Body: "I noticed Acme's benefits renewal is coming up in October — reaching out now because most HR Directors I work with are already managing the pressure from claims trends heading into renewal. We work with self-insured manufacturers in the Phoenix area to reduce healthcare cost trend by 30-40% in Year 1 — through better primary care access, not benefit cuts. Worth a 15-minute call to see if this applies to your situation?" Entity CEO reviewed the template. Sent from rep's personal email. Reply from HR Director: "Yes, this is very timely. Let's connect." Stage 1 → meeting scheduled.
2. **Edge case:** Rep wants to write: "Many employers like yours are struggling with high rates of diabetes and chronic disease driving their claims" → Dr. Lewis: "That line needs to change before it goes out. 'High rates of diabetes and chronic disease' is clinical language referencing employee health conditions — even as a general statement, it implies you know something about their employee population's health status. The HIPAA-safe version: 'Many employers like yours are dealing with healthcare costs that are increasing faster than inflation — often driven by conditions that would have been manageable with earlier primary care access.' Same message; no clinical condition references. Always use employer-financial language (cost trend; healthcare spend; benefits budget) instead of health-condition language (diabetes; chronic disease; high-utilization employees). Revised and approved before sending."
3. **Adversarial:** Rep uses a marketing automation platform to blast 300 Allevio employer contacts with the same template at once → Dr. Lewis: "Three problems: (1) Mass outreach at 300 contacts was not approved by entity CEO — this needs entity CEO approval before it goes out at that volume; (2) A 300-recipient blast is not personalized — 'personalization' in a mass tool is field substitution, not actual relevance to their situation; response rates will be low and unsubscribes will be high; (3) Allevio's brand reputation in the AZ employer market is small enough that a spammy mass outreach damages it — an HR Director who gets an impersonal blast doesn't just not reply; they tell their broker about it. Immediate action: pause the sequence; entity CEO notified; review which contacts have received the email; if mass outreach is appropriate (e.g., benefits renewal season blast to a curated list), entity CEO needs to approve the copy, the list, and the timing before it goes out."
