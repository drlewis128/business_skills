# Thought Leadership Planner

Build a thought leadership strategy for an executive — establishing them as a recognized voice in their domain through a disciplined, consistent content and speaking plan. Thought leadership is not writing one article and hoping it goes viral. It is a 12-month discipline of consistent positioning that earns credibility over time. The goal is that when a journalist needs a comment, a conference needs a speaker, or a buyer needs to trust a company, the executive's name comes up without being solicited.

## When to Use
- Matt Mathison wants to build his public profile in the PE/business community
- A portfolio company CEO needs to establish credibility in their market
- Preparing for a capital raise or major partnership where executive profile matters
- Dr. Lewis wants to establish his own AI/systems thought leadership for MBL
- Annual communications planning — thought leadership is a track in the annual plan

## Thought Leadership Positioning

```
The positioning question: What does [Executive] want to be known for 
among [specific audience] in [timeframe]?

This must be specific:
  ❌ Too vague: "A leader in PE"
  ✅ Specific: "The PE investor who figured out how to use AI to accelerate 
               value creation at lower-middle-market portfolio companies"
  
  ❌ Too vague: "Thought leader in healthcare"  
  ✅ Specific: "The operator who knows how to turn independent physician 
               practices into sustainable, patient-first businesses"

The positioning determines what you write, where you publish, and who 
you need to meet. Generic positioning produces generic content.
```

## Content Vehicle Mix

```
Tier 1 — High effort, high impact (1-2 per year):
  - Original research / data study published with media support
  - Long-form article in a tier-1 trade publication (Forbes, HBR, PE Hub)
  - Conference keynote at a major industry event

Tier 2 — Medium effort, medium impact (monthly):
  - LinkedIn long-form post (600-1,200 words) with original perspective
  - Podcast appearance as a guest
  - Panel participation at industry conference

Tier 3 — Low effort, daily signal (2-3 per week):
  - LinkedIn short post (100-200 words) — a perspective on a news item
  - Repost with original commentary
  - Reply to a relevant post by someone in the target community

Ratio: 1 Tier 1 : 4 Tier 2 : 16 Tier 3 per quarter
```

## Output Contract
- Positioning statement is specific and falsifiable — if the positioning could describe any other PE investor/executive, it fails; the positioning must be specific to this person's actual experience and insight
- Three pillars maximum — content without consistent pillars is random; the three pillars define the domain and ensure every piece of content reinforces the positioning
- Tier 3 cadence is non-negotiable — the platform algorithm rewards consistency; two posts a week for 52 weeks beats 10 posts a week for 5 weeks; the plan must include Tier 3 cadence to maintain signal
- Executive reviews all drafts — thought leadership only works if it genuinely reflects the executive's thinking; Dr. Lewis can draft, but the executive must edit substantially enough that it sounds like them
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis drafts content; executive reviews and approves every post before it goes out; Matt Mathison reviews any content that references fund-level strategy, portfolio companies, or LP relationships; legal reviews content related to active deals or regulatory topics

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Industry news, company news, executive's past content and speaking, brand messaging framework
- **Writes to:** Thought leadership plan (SharePoint/Comms/Thought Leadership); LinkedIn content calendar; conference speaking tracker
- **HITL Required:** Executive approves every piece; Matt Mathison reviews fund-level references; legal reviews sensitive topics

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Matt Mathison thought leadership plan → Positioning: "The PE investor who uses AI and operational discipline — not financial engineering — to build durable businesses in the Western US"; 3 pillars: AI-enabled value creation, Owner-operated company transformation, Western US market thesis; Q1: 1 LinkedIn long post on AI in PE (2,400 views, 87 reactions); 1 podcast pitch sent (PE Hub podcast); 2x/week short posts; 1 Becker's expert quote (via media-relations connection); Q2: article in PE Hub accepted; speaking invitation from AZ PE summit; measurable: 4 inbound deal conversations attributed to LinkedIn content in the quarter
2. **Edge case:** Executive produces great content but won't post consistently ("I don't have time") → the plan must be realistic for their actual availability; if they can commit to 1 post per week rather than 2-3, plan for 1; a consistent 1 per week for 52 weeks beats 3 for 4 weeks then nothing; reduce the Tier 3 target to what they will actually do; Dr. Lewis can draft all content but the executive must approve within 24 hours of submission (set this expectation explicitly)
3. **Adversarial:** Executive posts a comment on a competitor's LinkedIn post that comes across as dismissive or negative → this happens in real time and doesn't have a pre-approval workflow; debrief immediately; establish a rule: before engaging with competitor content, pause and consider whether the comment would look good in a screenshot; set up a simple check: "Dr. Lewis, I'm thinking of commenting on X — thoughts?" for anything that could be sensitive
