# Comms OKR Tracker

Track OKRs for the corporate communications function — covering media relations, executive communications, brand, internal communications, and crisis preparedness. Communications OKRs create accountability for a function that is often measured by activity (number of press releases sent) rather than outcomes (press placements that generated business conversations). This skill reorients communications measurement toward business impact.

## When to Use
- Setting communications OKRs for a portfolio company's communications function
- Quarterly review of Dr. Lewis's communications program for MBL Partners
- Matt Mathison asks "what is the communications function actually producing?"
- Annual communications planning — set OKRs before the year begins
- A portfolio company is hiring a communications leader and needs OKRs for that role

## OKR Framework for Communications

```
4 Communications Objective Areas:

1. Media & Reputation: Earned media, brand coverage, reputation health
2. Executive Presence: Thought leadership, speaking, LinkedIn, executive profile
3. Internal Communications: Employee awareness, all-hands quality, CEO comms
4. Crisis Preparedness: Playbook current, simulation complete, monitoring active
```

## Output Contract
- Business impact metrics, not activity metrics — press releases sent is not a KR; press placements in target publications is; LinkedIn posts published is not enough; engagement rate and business conversations attributed are what matter
- Crisis preparedness is always tracked — an organization that treats crisis preparedness as a "when we have time" task will always be unprepared; crisis readiness OKRs make preparation non-optional
- Grade always numerical (0-1.0) — qualitative "good/bad" assessments are not actionable; the grade provides a consistent basis for year-over-year comparison
- Off-track KRs have plans — any KR below 0.5 requires a specific explanation and a corrective action; just reporting the bad grade is not enough
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis maintains OKRs; Matt Mathison reviews quarterly; annual OKRs set with Matt Mathison input; portfolio company communications OKRs require CEO alignment

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Media coverage tracker, LinkedIn analytics, all-hands attendance, newsletter open rates, simulation results
- **Writes to:** OKR tracker (SharePoint/Comms/OKRs); quarterly review; Matt Mathison briefing
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis maintains; Matt Mathison reviews quarterly; annual targets set with Matt Mathison

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** MBL Q3 comms OKR review → O1: 4 placements (target 3), 2 Tier 2 (target 2), 0 negative incidents; grade 0.9; O2: Matt Mathison LinkedIn 3x/week on track, engagement rate 4.2% (target 3%), 1 speaking slot accepted; grade 0.8; O3: 2 portfolio company all-hands on time, Allevio newsletter 78% open rate; grade 0.85; O4: playbook updated Q2, simulation not yet done (Q4 scheduled), media monitoring weekly; grade 0.7; Overall: 0.81; Matt Mathison: "Good quarter on media — prioritize the simulation before year-end"
2. **Edge case:** A KR for "no negative press" is violated by an article that was not caused by the company's communications failure (e.g., a reporter wrote a story without ever contacting the company) → grade the KR fairly based on whether the response was appropriate, not on whether the article was published; the communications function can't control whether negative articles are written; it can control the response speed and quality; adjust the KR definition for next year to "response time to negative coverage" rather than "zero negative coverage"
3. **Adversarial:** Matt Mathison wants to see activity metrics ("how many press releases did we send?") rather than outcome metrics ("how many placements did we get?") → push back respectfully; explain that press releases sent is an activity; press placements (editorial decisions by journalists) are outcomes; reporting activity without outcomes measures effort, not impact; propose tracking both, but labeling clearly which is effort and which is outcome

## Audit Log
OKR grades retained by quarter and year. Target revisions documented. Annual outcomes retained for trend analysis.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies or MBL Partners hire Communications Directors who own OKR management with dedicated reporting tools.
