# Comms Onboarding Plan

Create a 90-day onboarding plan for a new communications hire — covering the learning required, the relationships to build, the early deliverables that establish credibility, and the success criteria that define whether the hire is succeeding. A communications hire who isn't given a clear onboarding structure will default to what they know from their last job — which may not be right for this company.

## When to Use
- A communications manager, director, or VP starts at a portfolio company or MBL Partners
- Dr. Lewis is onboarding a communications hire and needs a structured plan
- Matt Mathison asks "how are we onboarding the new comms person?"

## Output Format

```markdown
# 90-Day Comms Onboarding Plan — <Name>
**Role:** <Title> | **Entity:** <Company>
**Start date:** <date> | **Manager:** Dr. John Lewis
**Success at 90 days means:** [Specific outcomes that prove the hire is succeeding]

---

## Month 1: Learn the Business and the Brand (Days 1-30)

**Mindset:** Listen more than you talk. Understand the business before you change the communications.

### Week 1: Orientation

| Day | Activity | Owner | Outcome |
|-----|---------|-------|---------|
| Day 1 | Welcome, systems access, SharePoint/communications folders | Dr. Lewis | Tool access complete |
| Day 1 | Dr. Lewis overview: communications strategy, current assets, relationships | Dr. Lewis | Communications context established |
| Day 1-2 | Review all existing communications materials: press releases, media kit, newsletters | Self | Current state understood |
| Day 2 | Brand messaging framework review (or brand audit if none exists) | Dr. Lewis | Brand voice understood |
| Day 3 | Matt Mathison intro (30 min) — business strategy, portfolio priorities | Matt Mathison | Strategic context |
| Day 3 | Crisis response playbook review — current state | Dr. Lewis | Crisis readiness understood |
| Day 4 | Media contact list review — who are the active relationships? | Dr. Lewis | Media landscape known |
| Day 4 | Portfolio company overview: Allevio, HIVE, Column6 — business models + PR needs | Dr. Lewis | Portfolio context |
| Day 5 | Review last 12 months of media coverage — what has been written about the company? | Self | Coverage landscape understood |
| Day 5 | AI tools setup: Claude Code, business_skills library | Dr. Lewis | Tools operational |

---

### Week 2-4: Deep Learning

| Topic | Format | Who leads | Outcome |
|-------|--------|----------|---------|
| CEO of each portfolio company — 30-min intro call | Call | Dr. Lewis introduces | Relationship established + portfolio media priorities understood |
| Review all active media relationships (warm/hot) — reach out to re-introduce | Self (with Dr. Lewis guidance) | Dr. Lewis reviews | Active contacts re-engaged |
| First media monitoring weekly review — run the Monday scan | Self | Dr. Lewis reviews | Monitoring process owned |
| Review all-hands meeting materials (last 2 meetings) | Self | Dr. Lewis Q&A | Internal comms style understood |
| LinkedIn review — last 6 months of executive content | Self | | LinkedIn voice and cadence understood |

### Month 1 Deliverable

**Assignment:** Conduct a communications audit — what assets exist, what's missing, what's outdated, what's working, what's not. Present findings to Dr. Lewis.
**Due:** Day 30
**Standard:** The audit is specific (not "our media coverage is limited") with evidence and a prioritized list of quick wins

---

## Month 2: Build and Execute (Days 31-60)

**Mindset:** Start contributing independently. Take on real work. Build relationships. Identify story opportunities.

| Responsibility | Frequency | Standard |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| Weekly media monitoring review | Weekly | Report any items requiring attention |
| Media pitching (first pitch to a target journalist) | Month 2 | Dr. Lewis reviews before send |
| All-hands agenda contribution | Monthly | Draft segment for Dr. Lewis review |
| CEO LinkedIn content draft (for Dr. Lewis review) | 2× per month | Sounds like the CEO |
| Internal newsletter draft (if applicable) | Monthly | Dr. Lewis reviews before distribution |

**Month 2 Deliverable:** Execute first earned media placement — from pitch to coverage.
**Due:** Day 60 (or by end of first 2 months)
**Standard:** One piece of earned coverage that wouldn't have existed without the hire's outreach

---

## Month 3: Own and Improve (Days 61-90)

**Mindset:** Take ownership. Proactively identify what needs attention. Bring ideas before being asked.

| Responsibility | Status target by Day 90 |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Media relations | 3 active journalist relationships built independently |
| Weekly monitoring | Fully autonomous — Dr. Lewis receives summary only when action is needed |
| Internal newsletter | Full ownership of drafting, with Dr. Lewis editorial review |
| Crisis preparedness | Has read and understands crisis playbook; knows their role in it |
| Executive communications | Drafts CEO LinkedIn posts for review; has the CEO's voice guide |
| Reporting | Submits monthly comms KPI dashboard to Dr. Lewis |

**Month 3 Deliverable:** Present comms program recommendations to Dr. Lewis and Matt Mathison — what's working, what to add, what to stop, and the Q[N+1] communications plan.
**Due:** Day 90

---

## 90-Day Success Criteria

By Day 90, the communications hire should be able to:
- [ ] Write a strong media pitch independently (without Dr. Lewis review before send)
- [ ] Name 3 active journalist relationships they built (not inherited)
- [ ] Have gotten at least 1 piece of earned media coverage from their own pitch
- [ ] Run the weekly media monitoring scan without prompting
- [ ] Draft the monthly internal newsletter with one review cycle
- [ ] Know the crisis playbook and their role in it
- [ ] Draft CEO LinkedIn posts that the CEO is willing to post with minimal edits
- [ ] Use Claude Code and the business_skills comms library for standard tasks

---

## Key Relationships to Build in 90 Days

| Person | Role | Why | Target date |
|--------|------|-----|------------|
| Matt Mathison | Managing Partner | Primary communications stakeholder | Day 3 (Dr. Lewis intro) |
| Allevio CEO | Portfolio company | Key spokesperson and relationship | Day 20 |
| HIVE GP | Portfolio company | Understand communications needs | Day 25 |
| Column6 CEO | Portfolio company | CTV market communications | Day 25 |
| 3 target journalists (independently built) | Press | Core of the media program | Day 60 |

---

## 30/60/90 Check-Ins

**Day 30:** Communications audit presentation — what's the state of the program? Gaps identified. Quick wins for Month 2.

**Day 60:** First coverage achieved? Journalist relationships built? Newsletter and LinkedIn cadence established?

**Day 90:** Full program review with Dr. Lewis and Matt Mathison. Against 90-day success criteria. Set first 6-month goals.
```

## Output Contract
- Month 1 philosophy is "learn, don't change" — a communications hire who immediately starts sending press releases before understanding the company's positioning, media relationships, and brand voice will create inconsistency; the first 30 days are for understanding
- First earned media coverage by Day 60 — this is the minimum proof of value for a communications hire; if no coverage has been achieved by Day 60, that is a signal about capability or about the story opportunity available
- AI tools are set up in Day 1 — the communications function at MBL/portfolio companies uses AI tools; this is not optional; Day 1 setup ensures the hire can work in the expected way from the start
- 90-day success criteria are explicit — ambiguous onboarding leads to mismatched expectations; the hire needs to know exactly what "success at 90 days" means
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis builds and runs the onboarding plan; Matt Mathison reviews Day 90 presentation; any media pitch in Month 1 and 2 reviewed by Dr. Lewis before send; no independent pitching until Dr. Lewis confirms readiness

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Communications assets, media contact list, brand messaging framework, crisis playbook, company strategy
- **Writes to:** Onboarding plan (SharePoint/HR/<Name>/Onboarding); 30/60/90 check-in notes
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis leads onboarding; Matt Mathison involved at Day 3 intro and Day 90 review; all early pitches reviewed by Dr. Lewis

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio Communications Manager, Day 1 → Week 1 complete: all systems set up; brand messaging reviewed; Matt Mathison intro done; coverage landscape analyzed; Month 2: first pitch sent to Becker's reporter (Dr. Lewis reviewed); Day 50: placement confirmed; Month 3: independently managing newsletter and LinkedIn; Day 90: presents program review — 3 journalist relationships built, 2 placements, newsletter open rate 71%; 8/8 success criteria met; 6-month goals set
2. **Edge case:** The hire is exceptional at media relations but struggles with internal communications — newsletter drafts are good but not in the right voice for the CEO → this is a Month 2 gap to address; spend extra time on CEO voice capture; have the CEO record a few voice notes that the hire can listen to; this gap is solvable with coaching; escalate to Dr. Lewis only if it persists past Day 60
3. **Adversarial:** The hire is impatient with "learn before you do" and wants to immediately start pitching before understanding the brand → explain the reason: a pitch that goes out before the company's positioning is understood can set media expectations that are wrong and hard to reset; a journalist who gets confused messaging early becomes a journalist who doesn't trust the company's communications; the 30-day learning period protects the company's media relationships

## Audit Log
Onboarding plan retained by hire. 30/60/90 check-in notes retained. 90-day success criteria assessments retained. Matt Mathison Day 90 review documented.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have HR functions managing communications hiring and onboarding as a standard process.
