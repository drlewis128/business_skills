# Ops Culture Ops

Manage culture, engagement, and THRIVE values implementation across MBL Partners portfolio — assessing cultural health, designing programs that reinforce the THRIVE values, measuring employee engagement, and flagging cultural risks that affect retention, execution quality, and the entity's ability to attract talent. Culture is an operational reality, not a soft HR concept: a high-trust, high-performance culture attracts and retains the people who execute the strategy; a low-trust, high-confusion culture loses good people to competitors and keeps the ones who can't find better options. Dr. Lewis maintains the THRIVE filter as the portfolio's cultural anchor — all culture program design, recognition, and values decisions pass through THRIVE; the entity CEO owns the day-to-day culture, but Dr. Lewis provides the portfolio-level framework and monitors the signals.

## When to Use
- Quarterly culture and engagement review
- A significant culture event (key person departure; leadership behavior concern; team conflict)
- Designing a recognition or values reinforcement program
- Post-acquisition culture integration
- Employee engagement scores are declining

## Culture Operations Framework

```
THRIVE VALUES FRAMEWORK (MBL Partners cultural foundation):

  T — TRUTH:
    Communicate honestly; don't sugarcoat; deliver bad news directly
    In culture: employees trust leaders who tell the truth; distrust builds when leaders spin
    Behavioral standard: "Would someone looking at this from outside say it was honest?"
    Violation signal: information being withheld from employees that affects them
    
  H — HUSTLE:
    Urgency; execution speed; competitive drive
    In culture: reward people who execute fast; don't tolerate complacency
    Behavioral standard: "Is the pace of execution competitive with the best in this industry?"
    Violation signal: work that can be done in 3 days taking 10; no one is holding the standard
    
  R — RESPECT:
    Treat people with dignity regardless of role or title
    In culture: respect is non-negotiable; it's not contingent on performance
    Behavioral standard: "Would a senior leader behave the same way if being observed by Matt?"
    Violation signal: dismissive language; public humiliation; bullying behavior
    
  I — INTEGRITY:
    Do what you say; honest even when it's costly
    In culture: integrity is the kill criterion — violations result in termination
    Behavioral standard: "Does this action match what we publicly say we stand for?"
    Violation signal: fraud; misrepresentation; promised-then-abandoned commitments
    
  V — VALUE:
    Create measurable value for the organization and its stakeholders
    In culture: celebrate work that creates real value; don't celebrate activity that doesn't move the needle
    Behavioral standard: "What did this accomplish that wasn't accomplished before?"
    Violation signal: theater of activity (busy work; meetings about meetings; no output)
    
  E — ENJOY:
    Build something people genuinely want to work on
    In culture: sustainable high performance requires people to find meaning in the work
    Behavioral standard: "Can I point to something in this person's work that they find meaningful?"
    Violation signal: consistently negative energy; team unable to celebrate wins; no sense of progress

CULTURE ASSESSMENT:

  SIGNALS TO TRACK:
    Employee engagement score (quarterly; eNPS or full survey)
    Voluntary turnover rate (rolling 12 months; target varies by entity; <15% is healthy)
    Time-to-fill (open roles): if it's taking longer, the employer brand may be declining
    Glassdoor / LinkedIn signals: what are people saying publicly?
    Leadership 360 feedback: how is the entity CEO being experienced by the team?
    
  ENGAGEMENT SURVEY:
    Quarterly pulse survey (5-10 questions; takes 3 minutes)
    Annual deep survey (full engagement + leadership + culture)
    Key questions: I understand what's expected of me; I trust my manager; I'd recommend this company to a friend
    eNPS = % Promoters - % Detractors (same formula as customer NPS)
    
  THRIVE VIOLATION PROTOCOL:
    Minor violation (I, R, T): entity CEO coaches the employee; documented
    Significant violation (H, V, E): performance improvement conversation; timeline to correct
    Integrity violation: immediate escalation to entity CEO + Dr. Lewis
    If the entity CEO is the violator: Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison immediately

CULTURE PROGRAMS:

  RECOGNITION PROGRAM:
    Goal: catch people acting in alignment with THRIVE values and recognize them specifically
    Format: weekly entity-level recognition in team meetings; quarterly portfolio-level recognition
    "THRIVE Award": nominated by peers; winners recognized by entity CEO or Matt Mathison
    Specificity requirement: the recognition must name the specific THRIVE value and the specific behavior
    ("Thank you for your Hustle on the employer onboarding" beats "great work everyone")
    
  PERFORMANCE CULTURE:
    Goals: clear; measurable; set by entity CEO with Dr. Lewis's OKR support
    Feedback: direct; specific; timely
    Accountability: performance gaps addressed, not tolerated
    THRIVE filter: performance management that lacks Respect is not acceptable even when addressing poor performance
    
  POST-ACQUISITION CULTURE INTEGRATION:
    First 90 days: do not force the MBL/THRIVE culture onto the acquired entity aggressively
    First 90 days: observe; listen; identify what's working in the existing culture
    Days 90-180: introduce THRIVE values with context (not mandate)
    Days 180+: evaluate alignment; coach on gaps; build THRIVE-aligned culture organically
    Culture integration failure mode: "We have a new set of values" announced without behavior change in leadership
```

## Output Contract
- THRIVE is applied with specificity, not as a poster on the wall — the THRIVE values are only useful if they are connected to specific behaviors and specific recognition or correction events; "we value truth" means nothing if leaders hedge and spin; "we value truth — and this week [Employee] told our client exactly what wasn't working in the campaign instead of covering it, which is exactly what THRIVE means to us" means something; Dr. Lewis designs culture programs that make THRIVE behavioral and specific, not aspirational and vague
- Integrity violations are never managed downward — an Integrity violation (fraud; misrepresentation; dishonest dealing) is the kill criterion in the THRIVE filter; it does not receive a coaching conversation or a performance improvement plan for a first offense; the Integrity standard applies to every person in the portfolio regardless of role or tenure; an entity CEO who commits an Integrity violation goes to Matt Mathison immediately; a senior employee who commits an Integrity violation goes to the entity CEO + Dr. Lewis immediately; the response must be visible and consequential, or the culture message is that Integrity only applies to some people
- Culture signals from engagement data are surfaced without spin — an eNPS that dropped from 42 to 28 in one quarter is a significant negative signal; Dr. Lewis reports it with the honest context (what happened in that quarter that might explain it?), the root cause hypothesis (what's driving the drop?), and the recommended action (what should the entity CEO do?); there is no massaging of engagement data to make it look better than it is; Matt Mathison trusts Dr. Lewis's culture reporting because it's honest, not because it's positive
- HITL required: Integrity violations by any employee → entity CEO immediate; Integrity violations by entity CEO → Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison immediate; eNPS drop >20 points in one quarter → entity CEO root cause + Dr. Lewis review; significant voluntary turnover spike (>5% in 90 days) → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis root cause; culture conflicts between two senior leaders → entity CEO + Dr. Lewis mediation; post-acquisition culture integration plan → Dr. Lewis design + Matt Mathison awareness; THRIVE award nominations → entity CEO selects winners; Matt Mathison can also nominate

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Q3 culture review. Allevio eNPS: 38 (up from 31 in Q2; strong improving trend). Voluntary turnover TTM: 9.2% (healthy). THRIVE signal: clinical team nominated three colleagues for "Truth" awards — all three surfaced a billing error that could have affected members; all three were recognized at the team meeting. Dr. Lewis to entity CEO: "Strong Q3 culture signals at Allevio. The increase in Truth nominations is particularly meaningful — it shows employees believe it's safe to surface problems. Recommend continuing the recognition cadence and explicitly connecting it to the Truth value in your next all-hands."
2. **Edge case:** An entity CEO discovers that a manager is publicly embarrassing an employee during team meetings (repeatedly criticizing the employee in front of peers) → Dr. Lewis: "This is a Respect violation — it's not a performance management method, it's public humiliation. The manager's intent (improving performance) doesn't change the behavior's impact (creating psychological unsafety for the employee and degrading the team culture for everyone watching). The entity CEO's action: private, direct conversation with the manager today: 'What you're doing in team meetings with [employee] is not how we give feedback at [Entity]. It stops today. If you have performance concerns about [employee], you bring them to me and we address them through a proper process.' If the behavior continues after this conversation: escalation to a formal performance action. Dr. Lewis note: if the entity CEO is not comfortable having this conversation, I can help them prepare the specific words. The conversation must happen within 24 hours."
3. **Adversarial:** An entity CEO pushes back on the engagement survey, saying "surveys create false expectations that we'll change things and then we look bad when we don't" → Dr. Lewis: "The concern is legitimate — surveys without action are worse than no surveys. But the solution isn't to skip the diagnostic; it's to be deliberate about what you act on. My recommendation: run the survey with a commitment to share the results honestly and act on the top 2-3 issues. When you share results, say: 'Here are the top themes we heard. Here's what we're changing. Here's what we can't change right now and why.' This is the Truth value in practice — employees respect honest responses to survey feedback far more than either no survey or no action. The alternative — leaders operating without engagement data — means you're making culture decisions blind. Would you be willing to do a pilot with 5 questions and see what comes back?"
