# Ops Onboarding Plan

Build and execute 90-day onboarding plans for operations hires at MBL portfolio companies — structuring the new hire's first three months to accelerate context-building, establish clear expectations, and set up early wins. Operations onboarding at portfolio scale is more than access provisioning and policy acknowledgments: it's building the mental model of how the business works, who the key relationships are, where the real work happens, and what "done" looks like in the MBL operations context. Done well, a 90-day onboarding plan gets an operations hire contributing independently by Day 91.

## When to Use
- A new operations hire is starting (any level — coordinator through Director)
- An existing team member is moving into an operations role from another function
- Dr. Lewis is setting expectations for an ops role transition
- The CEO asks "how will we ramp up [new hire]?"

## Ops Onboarding Framework

```
Three-phase structure:
  Phase 1 — Learn (Days 1-30): Context first, contribution second
    Primary goal: Understand the business, the systems, the processes, the people
    Contribution: Support-level tasks; shadow, don't lead
    Mindset: Be a sponge; ask questions; don't fix things yet
    
  Phase 2 — Build (Days 31-60): Understand the problems; start contributing
    Primary goal: Identify the 2-3 highest-impact areas; own defined tasks
    Contribution: Increasing ownership; still checking with Dr. Lewis before significant actions
    Mindset: Build credibility through execution; earn trust before driving change
    
  Phase 3 — Contribute (Days 61-90): Own your scope; deliver measurable value
    Primary goal: Execute against defined OKRs; independent on BAU work
    Contribution: Full accountability for assigned processes; proactive identification of improvements
    Mindset: Be the expert in your assigned area; escalate when uncertain, but don't wait

Onboarding elements:
  System access: All required system access provisioned before Day 1 (Dr. Lewis responsible)
  Documentation: SOPs, process maps, vendor list, org chart provided Week 1
  Introductions: Structured introductions to all key stakeholders — internal and key vendors/partners
  Dr. Lewis check-ins: Day 1 (orientation), Day 5 (first week), Day 30, Day 60, Day 90
  CEO introduction: Week 1 — set the context for the role from the CEO's perspective
  Peer introduction: Introduce to team leads in Finance, HR, and relevant departments
  
Portfolio-specific considerations:
  Allevio hires: HIPAA Privacy training required before any PHI access; OIG LEIE check on start date (if patient-facing); AZ E-Verify within 3 business days
  HIVE hires: Field safety orientation if any field exposure; HIVE-specific production systems (Covercy)
  All MBL hires: Introduce THRIVE values and framework in Week 1; explain MBL systems map (Monday.com, QuickBooks, Bill.com, Microsoft 365, Krista.ai)
```

## Output Contract
- System access is provisioned before Day 1 — there is no acceptable version of a new hire arriving to no computer, no email, no system access; Dr. Lewis is accountable for the pre-start checklist; the compliance requirements (HIPAA training, OIG check, I-9, E-Verify) have regulatory deadlines — these are tracked and met
- Phase 1 is learn-first, not fix-first — new hires who arrive with strong opinions and try to change things in Week 1 damage relationships and often miss context that explains why things are the way they are; the plan explicitly reserves Phase 1 for observation; "don't fix things yet" is a direct instruction; the Day 5 check-in is Dr. Lewis's opportunity to reinforce this if the new hire is already in change-agent mode
- The Day 30, 60, and 90 check-ins are standing appointments — not optional; Dr. Lewis puts them on the calendar on Day 1; the agendas are the same each time (structured, not improvised); these check-ins give the new hire regular structured feedback and give Dr. Lewis early visibility into integration challenges before they become problems
- Full accountability by Day 91 is the target — the plan is designed so that by Day 91, the new hire is executing BAU independently and contributing to OKRs; if that's not happening, the Day 90 check-in surfaces it and triggers either a performance improvement path or a role redesign conversation; the onboarding plan creates the evidence base for that conversation
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis conducts all check-ins; CEO introduction scheduled in Week 1; compliance items (HIPAA, OIG, I-9, E-Verify) are Dr. Lewis's responsibility to verify completion; Matt Mathison is introduced to Director-level hires in Week 1; all system access provisioned by Dr. Lewis before Day 1

## System Dependencies

- **Writes to:** Onboarding plan (SharePoint/Ops/<Company>/People/Onboarding/[Name]/); compliance tracking (HIPAA training date, OIG check date, E-Verify case #); Monday.com tasks for pre-start checklist; system access requests
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis owns all check-ins; compliance verification by Dr. Lewis; CEO introduction by CEO; system access provisioned by Dr. Lewis before Day 1; Matt Mathison introduction for Director+

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio new billing coordinator starting Monday → Pre-start: M365 account created; AdvancedMD access requested (clinical team has to provision); Monday.com access added; building fob prepared; I-9 Section 2 on Day 3; HIPAA training scheduled Day 4 online; OIG LEIE check run Day 1 (cleared); E-Verify opened Day 2; Documentation package sent Friday before start; Day 1: orientation with Dr. Lewis (2 hours — THRIVE, systems map, role context); CEO introduction at 11 AM; afternoon: IT setup; Day 5: check-in — new hire asks good questions about denial management (a sign of engagement); Day 30: fully oriented, can navigate all systems, met all key contacts; Day 60: independently preparing weekly denial report; drafted first SOP (claim submission checklist); Day 90: full BAU independent; contributing to denial-rate OKR Key Result
2. **Edge case:** New hire is struggling at Day 30 — still needs guidance on basic system tasks, hasn't engaged with key stakeholders yet → Address directly in the Day 30 check-in; be specific about what's expected by now vs. what's been observed; assess: is this a motivation/engagement issue or a capability issue? Provide direct guidance on the remaining 30 days; if no meaningful improvement by Day 60, initiate the performance conversation through HR; the onboarding plan creates the documented baseline for that conversation
3. **Adversarial:** "We're too busy to do 90-day onboarding — the new hire needs to hit the ground running" → The onboarding plan IS hitting the ground running — it's structured to accelerate time-to-productivity, not to slow it down; an unstructured new hire who spends 3 weeks figuring out what they're supposed to do costs more than a structured 90-day plan; respond: "The plan takes 4 hours of Dr. Lewis time spread over 90 days (check-ins + initial orientation). Without it, the new hire typically takes 4-6 months to reach full productivity. This cuts that in half."

## Audit Log
Onboarding plans retained by employee and company. Check-in notes retained. Compliance completion dates retained permanently (HIPAA training dates, OIG check results, E-Verify case numbers, I-9 dates). Day 90 performance assessment retained.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have HR leaders who own onboarding programs — designing the plan, conducting Day 5 and Day 30 check-ins, and delivering the compliance components — with Dr. Lewis conducting only the Day 60 and Day 90 executive check-ins.
