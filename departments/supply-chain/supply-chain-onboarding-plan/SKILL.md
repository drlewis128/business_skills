# Supply Chain Onboarding Plan

Build a 30/60/90-day onboarding plan for a new supply chain or procurement hire. The first 90 days either set a new hire up for lasting success or create confusion, frustration, and early attrition. A structured plan eliminates guesswork, accelerates time-to-productivity, and sets clear expectations. For MBL, supply chain hires need entity-specific context (HIPAA for Allevio, oil and gas for HIVE) in addition to standard procurement and operations knowledge.

## Onboarding Philosophy

```
Month 1 (Days 1-30): Listen and learn
  No major changes; no new systems without Dr. Lewis approval
  Goal: Understand the business, the suppliers, and the current processes
  Output: Current state assessment (what's working, what needs improvement)

Month 2 (Days 31-60): Contribute with supervision
  Begin managing defined supplier relationships and categories
  First independent actions — small procurement decisions within authority
  Output: First quick wins; key relationships established

Month 3 (Days 61-90): Own and lead
  Full ownership of defined categories and supplier relationships
  OKR targets set for Q+1
  Output: 90-day review; transition to independent operation
```

```markdown
## Month 1 (Days 1-30): Learn the business

### Week 1-2: Context and orientation

| Activity | What to learn | Owner | Due |
|---------|-------------|-------|-----|
| Read MBL CLAUDE.md and entity context documents | MBL structure, THRIVE values, portfolio | New hire | Day 3 |

### Week 3-4: Deeper dive

| Activity | What to learn | Owner | Due |
|---------|-------------|-------|-----|
| Review supply chain KPI dashboard | What do we measure? What's performing? | New hire | Day 15 |
| Interview key internal customers (ops leads) | What do they need? What frustrates them? | New hire | Day 20 |
| Interview 2-3 key suppliers | Relationship history; their view of us | New hire | Day 20 |

**Month 1 success criteria:**
- Can describe MBL's supply chain structure and key suppliers from memory
- Has read all Tier A supplier contracts
- Has completed required compliance training (HIPAA / OSHA)
- Has submitted current state assessment

## 90-Day Review Agenda

```
90-Day Review: New hire + Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison (optional)
Duration: 60 minutes

1. What the new hire accomplished (10 min): Milestones hit; wins; contributions
2. Current state assessment findings (15 min): What they observed; supply chain opportunities
3. What's going well (10 min): Strengths demonstrated
4. What needs improvement (10 min): Specific gaps with coaching plan
5. Q+1 OKRs and category ownership plan (15 min): What they own going forward
6. Questions and next steps (10 min)
```

## THRIVE Expectations Set at Onboarding

| Value | Behavioral expectation for supply chain |
|-------|----------------------------------------|
| Truth | When a supplier issue surfaces, bring it to Dr. Lewis immediately — no delay, no spin |
| Hustle | Supply chain problems don't wait for the next meeting; address urgent issues same-day |
| Respect | Build real supplier relationships — not just transactional; your relationship is our asset |
| Integrity | Never accept gifts >$50 from suppliers; disclose any personal relationships with vendors |
| Value | Every procurement decision should have a clear business case; "we always do it this way" is not a reason |
| Enjoy | Supply chain is a team sport; bring your best thinking and collaborate |
```

## Output Contract
- Month 1 always "learn before acting" — no major process changes or supplier decisions in Month 1 without Dr. Lewis sign-off
- HIPAA/OSHA training always in Month 1 for Allevio/HIVE roles — compliance training is not optional and cannot wait
- 90-day review always scheduled before the hire starts — the milestone gates need to be clear from Day 1
- THRIVE expectations always set explicitly at onboarding — values alignment is behavioral, not aspirational
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis designs and owns onboarding plan; Matt Mathison introduced in Week 1; new hire actions outside purchase authority matrix require Dr. Lewis approval in first 90 days; 90-day review includes Dr. Lewis + Matt Mathison for manager-level hires

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Supplier registry, procurement policy, current KPI dashboard, entity compliance requirements
- **Writes to:** Onboarding plan (SharePoint/People/Onboarding); 90-day review notes
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis owns onboarding; Matt Mathison Week 1 intro and 90-day review; all new hire decisions outside authority matrix require Dr. Lewis; compliance training completion verified

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Onboard procurement analyst for Allevio → Day 1 checklist complete; HIPAA training by Day 15; current state assessment by Day 28; Month 2: first RFQ (medical consumables <$8K) led independently — award to backup supplier saves 7% vs. Cardinal Health; Month 3: 90-day review with Dr. Lewis and Allevio CEO; OKRs set; full Tier C + B ownership transferred; Dr. Lewis confidence: high
2. **Edge case:** New hire comes from a very different industry (retail procurement, not healthcare) → Month 1 gets more time on HIPAA context and Allevio clinical operations; pair them with Allevio clinical lead for a "day in the life" shadowing; first independent actions stay in non-clinical categories; HIPAA training extended with hands-on BAA review; timeline adjusts: Tier B ownership at Day 75, not Day 65
3. **Adversarial:** New hire starts making independent supplier decisions in Week 2 without approval → this is a Month 1 boundary violation; reinforce the "learn before acting" rule immediately; the issue isn't initiative — it's unauthorized commitments; set up a daily 15-minute check-in for the first 30 days to channel their initiative; good initiative + right guardrails = what we want

## Audit Log
Onboarding plan retained. Milestone completions documented. HIPAA/OSHA training completion recorded. 90-day review notes retained. Purchase decisions in first 90 days reviewed by Dr. Lewis.

## Deprecation
Retire when MBL has an HR platform with standardized onboarding workflows, automated compliance training assignment, and milestone tracking.
