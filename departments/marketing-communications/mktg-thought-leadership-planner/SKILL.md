# Marketing Thought Leadership Planner

Plan, develop, and publish thought leadership content for MBL Partners principals and portfolio entity executives — building market credibility through expert perspective in private equity, healthcare operations, energy, CTV programmatic, and AI. Thought leadership is a trust multiplier; a well-placed article from Matt Mathison on PE deal value creation generates LP confidence, deal flow, and talent attraction that no paid advertising can replicate.

## When to Use
- Matt Mathison or Dr. Lewis needs to establish credibility in a new market
- An entity CEO needs visibility in their sector (Allevio: healthcare; HIVE: energy; Column6: CTV)
- A conference submission deadline is approaching and speaking topics need development
- Inbound deal flow or LP interest needs content support

## Thought Leadership Framework

```
AUTHOR PROFILES AND DOMAINS:

  MATT MATHISON (Managing Partner, MBL Partners):
    Primary authority: private equity operations; THRIVE values-based business building; lower-middle market deal philosophy
    Secondary authority: portfolio company transformation; AI strategy in PE
    Audience: business sellers; co-investors; LPs; PE industry
    Tone: Matt-specific (Dr. Lewis drafts; Matt reviews and adjusts to his voice; Matt publishes)
    Publishing venues: LinkedIn (primary); NVCA/ACG conference panels; MBL investor materials
    Cadence: 2-3 LinkedIn posts/month; 1 bylined article/quarter (target); 1-2 speaking slots/year
    
  DR. JOHN LEWIS (Principal Systems Engineer & Director of AI Strategy):
    Primary authority: AI agent infrastructure; Rust-based agent runtimes; Claude API integration; enterprise AI orchestration
    Secondary authority: PE operations AI; portfolio company AI strategy
    Audience: technical AI engineers; AI startup ecosystem; PE operations professionals; AI practitioner community
    Tone: Technical authority; direct; practitioner credibility (not theorist)
    Publishing venues: LinkedIn (primary); AI/ML community publications; technical conference submissions; GitHub
    Cadence: 3-4 LinkedIn posts/month; 1 technical article/quarter; GitHub activity (OpenFang)
    
  ENTITY CEOS:
    Allevio CEO: healthcare employer benefits; MSO model; care coordination outcomes (aggregate, HIPAA-compliant)
      Venues: MGMA; SHRM Arizona; employer benefits publications; healthcare HR conferences
    HIVE CEO: Uinta Basin operations; responsible energy development; royalty economics
      Venues: SPE; Utah oil and gas industry events; landowner rights publications
    Column6 CEO: programmatic advertising accountability; CTV measurement; agency economics
      Venues: IAB events; AdExchanger; Digiday; programmatic media publications

THOUGHT LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT PROCESS:
  1. Topic Identification:
     From: industry trends (what is the market debating?); company differentiation (our contrarian view?);
     audience pain (what do our buyers struggle to understand?)
     NOT from: self-promotion; press release repackaging; features we want to announce
     
  2. Point of View Development:
     Contrarian or complementary? (contrarian POV generates more engagement but must be defensible)
     What is the specific, quotable claim? ("Most CTV platforms optimize for delivery, not viewability — that's the wrong metric.")
     What evidence supports it? (data; case studies; client testimony — never PHI for Allevio)
     
  3. Format Selection:
     LinkedIn post (500-1500 words; hooks in first 2 lines; no links in first comment)
     Bylined article (800-1500 words for publications; longer for deep dives)
     Conference abstract (250-500 words; focused on practitioner takeaway)
     Webinar (45 min; 30 min content + 15 Q&A; always include data/case)
     
  4. Drafting (Dr. Lewis drafts for Matt; each author drafts own LinkedIn; entity CEO drafts with marketing support):
     Lead with the claim (BLUF — not a slow build to the point)
     Evidence in the middle
     Call to action at the end (next step for the reader)
     
  5. Review and Approval:
     Dr. Lewis and Matt review any MBL Partners content before publication
     Entity CEOs review and approve entity-specific content
     HIPAA review (Dr. Lewis) for any Allevio healthcare content before external publication
     
CONTENT BANK:
  Maintain a rolling bank of 6-10 potential thought leadership topics per author
  Topic bank reviewed monthly; added to as industry conversations evolve
  Topics that generate 3+ conference submissions are elevated to bylined article targets
```

## Output Contract
- The point of view must be specific and defensible — generic thought leadership ("AI is transforming business") is noise; "Most lower-middle market PE firms are using AI for deal sourcing but missing 90% of the value by not using it in portfolio company operations — here's what the gap looks like" is a specific, defensible, expert claim; Dr. Lewis requires each thought leadership piece to identify the specific contrarian or novel claim before drafting begins; for Matt Mathison, the THRIVE values frame often provides the contrarian angle (PE that has an Integrity kill criterion is genuinely different from standard PE practice)
- Matt's content is drafted by Dr. Lewis and reviewed by Matt — Matt Mathison does not produce first drafts of his own content; Dr. Lewis drafts based on conversation, briefing notes, or Matt's verbal direction; Matt reviews and adjusts to his own voice; Matt publishes under his name; this is standard PE thought leadership practice; the final voice must sound like Matt, not like a corporate communications team; Dr. Lewis schedules a 20-minute monthly brief with Matt to collect direction for the next month's content topics
- HIPAA applies to all Allevio healthcare content published by the Allevio CEO — even when the Allevio CEO writes their own LinkedIn post about healthcare outcomes, if it references Allevio's members or employer client results, Dr. Lewis reviews before publication; an enthusiastic CEO who tweets "our members improved their health outcomes by 30% this year!" is a HIPAA risk without Dr. Lewis reviewing the data behind the claim; no exceptions
- HITL required: Matt Mathison LinkedIn content → Dr. Lewis drafts; Matt reviews and approves before publishing; Allevio CEO healthcare content → Dr. Lewis HIPAA review before external publication; MBL Partners content → Matt Mathison final approval; conference submissions → entity CEO approves before submission; bylined articles → entity CEO (or Matt for MBL) approves before submission to publication

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Matt Mathison Q3 thought leadership plan. Three LinkedIn posts: (1) "Why THRIVE kills deals I should take — and why that's the point" [Integrity kill criterion explainer]; (2) "The question every PE firm should ask before the LOI" [values alignment in diligence]; (3) "What 3 years of portfolio operations has taught me about AI" [Dr. Lewis inputs; Matt voice]. One speaking submission: ACG Mountain West — "Operations-Driven PE: Building with THRIVE Values in Lower-Middle Market." Dr. Lewis drafts all LinkedIn content; Matt reviews in 20-minute monthly brief; Matt approves and schedules posts.
2. **Edge case:** Column6 CEO submits a conference abstract that references a specific advertiser campaign performance data without the advertiser's permission → Dr. Lewis: "Pull this before submission. Client campaign data without written permission is a contract violation and a relationship risk. I'll rewrite the abstract to use: (a) anonymized aggregate VCR benchmarks vs. industry averages; (b) the methodology (not the client result); or (c) a reference to the IAB study Column6 participated in. The abstract can still be strong without client data — the POV is the insight, not the client name."
3. **Adversarial:** Dr. Lewis publishes a LinkedIn post about AI agent architecture that subtly markets OpenFang → Dr. Lewis self-check: "OpenFang is my open-source project; it should be referenced with that context — not used to sell MBL services. When I write about Rust-based agent runtimes for enterprise AI, I can reference OpenFang as a proof point of my technical work. I'm careful not to blur the line between my personal technical brand and MBL Partners commercial interests. OpenFang is shared openly; MBL AI work is proprietary. My LinkedIn content separates these clearly."
