# Allevio Employer Marketer

Execute the full employer-facing marketing program for Allevio — building awareness among Arizona employer HR and benefits leaders, generating employer demo requests, supporting sales in converting prospects, and retaining existing employer clients. Allevio's primary buyer is the employer HR or Benefits VP; Allevio's economic buyer is the employer CFO. Marketing must speak to both and generate the employer GRR growth that sustains the entity.

## When to Use
- Building or refreshing the Allevio employer marketing plan
- Planning specific Allevio employer campaigns, content, or events
- Reviewing Allevio marketing performance and pipeline
- Developing Allevio sales enablement materials

## Allevio Employer Marketing Framework

```
ALLEVIO TARGET AUDIENCE:

  PRIMARY BUYER — HR Director / VP Benefits:
    Pain: employees not using benefits; claim costs rising; broker managing but not accountable
    Decision driver: care coordination model that reduces friction and cost
    Message: "Allevio connects your employees to the right care — care coordinators who navigate the system for them"
    Content preference: practical guides; benchmarks; peer comparison; case studies (aggregate; no PHI)
    
  ECONOMIC BUYER — CFO:
    Pain: benefits cost as % of payroll increasing year over year
    Decision driver: measurable ROI; claim ratio improvement; cost avoidance
    Message: "Allevio reduces your claim ratio. Average employer sees [X]% improvement in year 1."
    Content preference: financial models; ROI calculators; payback period analysis
    
  REFERRAL PARTNER — Benefits Broker:
    Pain: employer clients asking for better utilization; need differentiated solutions
    Decision driver: Allevio as a value-add recommendation that increases broker credibility with employer clients
    Message: "Allevio makes your employer clients stickier — and gives you something to recommend that actually works"
    Content: broker enablement kit; co-marketing materials; referral commission structure

HIPAA COMPLIANCE (ALL ALLEVIO MARKETING — MANDATORY):
  No PHI in any employer-facing marketing material — ever
  Aggregate employer data only (claim ratio improvement; utilization rates from employer cohort)
  Individual employee health data: never used in any marketing context
  Member testimonials: require explicit written consent and Dr. Lewis + legal review before use
  Data sources for marketing claims: employer cohort aggregate data from AdvancedMD; MGMA benchmarks; employer survey results
  Dr. Lewis reviews and approves ALL Allevio external marketing content before publication
  
CHANNEL PRIORITIES (Allevio employer):

  4. Employer webinars (quarterly; see mktg-webinar-manager)
  5. Broker relationship program (referral channel; see mktg-partner-co-marketer)
  6. Content SEO (employer search queries; see mktg-seo-manager)

ALLEVIO MARKETING METRICS:

  All materials: Dr. Lewis HIPAA review before finalization
```

## Output Contract
- All Allevio employer marketing content is reviewed by Dr. Lewis for HIPAA compliance before any publication, send, or launch — this is not a suggestion; it is the standing operating procedure for every piece of Allevio marketing content including LinkedIn posts, email subject lines, landing page copy, sales deck slides, and event collateral; the review is Dr. Lewis's personal responsibility; it cannot be delegated; Dr. Lewis has built a 15-minute HIPAA content review process that does not create significant delays; the two-week production timeline for Allevio content includes this review as a milestone, not an afterthought
- Allevio marketing claims are always aggregate employer-level, never member-level — "our employers see [X]% claim ratio improvement in year one" (sourced from employer cohort aggregate data) is a compliant marketing claim; "our members' preventive care utilization rate improved by [X]%" (sourced from AdvancedMD member data) is a potential HIPAA violation in a marketing context; the distinction is employer-level aggregate vs. member-level data; Dr. Lewis knows this line and enforces it on every piece of copy
- Marketing contributes to Allevio GRR, not just pipeline — the employer GRR (Gross Revenue Retention) target is ≥91%; marketing's role in GRR is not just new employer acquisition but employer retention marketing (showing existing employers the value they're getting; reinforcing the relationship; open enrollment support that makes the employer renewal decision easy); a marketing team that only focuses on new employer acquisition while ignoring the retention marketing program is leaving the most valuable revenue at risk
- HITL required: all Allevio external marketing → Dr. Lewis HIPAA review before publication; Allevio employer marketing plan → Allevio CEO approves; sales enablement materials → Allevio CEO + Dr. Lewis approve; employer case studies (named) → employer written permission + Dr. Lewis HIPAA + Allevio CEO; demo request → automated MQL notification to sales via GoHighLevel; marketing-sourced pipeline → Allevio CEO reviews monthly; GRR impact assessment → Dr. Lewis + Allevio CEO quarterly

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio Q4 employer demand gen campaign. MQL target: 15. Content: "Arizona Employer Benefits Report 2026" (aggregate data from MGMA + Allevio employer cohort; Dr. Lewis HIPAA reviewed). LinkedIn: 3 posts/week for 6 weeks (Dr. Lewis reviewed all). Email: 3-email campaign to employer contact list (GoHighLevel BAA confirmed; Dr. Lewis reviewed). Result: 21 MQLs; 8 demo requests; 3 proposals ($240K pipeline). Marketing-sourced pipeline: 31% of Q4 Allevio new business.
2. **Edge case:** Allevio CEO wants to use a single employer's claim ratio data as a marketing case study without anonymizing → Dr. Lewis: "I need written permission from the employer before we use their data in any external marketing material. Even if the employer is happy with the relationship, publishing their claim ratio data without consent creates a legal and relationship risk. I'll draft a case study permission form and get it signed before we proceed. If they approve, we can use their name and data. If they decline named attribution, I'll create an anonymous version: 'A 200-employee Arizona manufacturing employer saw [X]% claim ratio improvement in year one.' The anonymous version is still compelling."
3. **Adversarial:** Allevio marketing team publishes a LinkedIn post that says "Our care coordination program helped employees with diabetes manage their condition better" → Dr. Lewis: "Take that post down immediately. This implies we're tracking which employees have diabetes — that's PHI. Even if we didn't intend to imply we have individual health data, the language is HIPAA-risk in a marketing context. Revised version: 'Allevio's care coordinators help employees navigate their care — reducing avoidable ER visits and improving benefit utilization for employers.' This makes the same point without any disease-state reference. And this is why every post goes through my review before it goes live."
