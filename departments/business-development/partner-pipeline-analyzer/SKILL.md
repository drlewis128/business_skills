# Partner Pipeline Analyzer

Analyze the quality, volume, and velocity of the pipeline being generated by the partner program — separating the signal from the noise and identifying which partners, partner types, and partnership activities are generating real revenue. Partner pipeline analysis answers: "Is the partner program working, and specifically where is value coming from?" Without this analysis, partner programs are funded on faith rather than evidence.

## When to Use
- Quarterly BD review — Matt Mathison asks how the partner program is performing
- Evaluating whether to invest more in a specific partner or partner type
- Identifying bottlenecks: are partners generating referrals but they're not converting?
- Preparing partner program budget justification
- Identifying which partners should be prioritized, upgraded, or exited

## Pipeline Metrics

```
Partner pipeline is analyzed across 4 dimensions:

1. Volume: How much pipeline is being generated? (total and by partner)
2. Quality: What % of partner referrals meet the ICP? What's the conversion rate?
3. Velocity: How long does it take from referral to close? vs. company-sourced deals?
4. Value: What is the average deal size and LTV of partner-sourced customers?
```

## Output Contract
- TL;DR always leads — pipeline analysis reports that bury the headline in a table of numbers don't get read; the TL;DR must deliver the state of the partner program in 30 seconds
- ROI calculation always included — a partner program that can't demonstrate ROI is a program at risk of being cut; the cost (fees paid + BD time + operating costs) vs. return (closed revenue + pipeline value) must always be visible
- Quality and velocity compared to company-sourced — showing partner pipeline numbers in isolation doesn't answer the key question: are partner deals better or worse than other sources? The comparison is what drives decisions
- Specific bottleneck always identified — the most valuable output of a pipeline analysis is the specific friction point that is slowing deals or reducing quality; generic observations about "pipeline health" are not actionable; a specific bottleneck with an owner and a fix is
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis builds the analysis; Matt Mathison reviews quarterly; any decision to increase/decrease partner program investment requires Matt Mathison approval; partner exits based on pipeline analysis require Matt Mathison approval

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** CRM (partner-attributed deals, stage data, close rates), partner referral log, revenue data, fee payment records
- **Writes to:** Pipeline analysis report (SharePoint/BD/Pipeline Reports); partner OKR tracker
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis builds; Matt Mathison reviews quarterly; investment and exit decisions require Matt Mathison

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio Q2 partner pipeline review → 3 active referral partners; Partner A: 8 referrals, $78K closed, 87% ICP match, 45-day avg close; Partner B: 3 referrals, $18K closed, 75% ICP match, 62-day avg close; Partner C: 5 referrals, $0 closed, 41% ICP match (off-ICP problem) — geography mismatch confirmed; Total: $96K closed from partners; program cost: $18K in referral fees + $5K BD time = $23K; ROI: 4.2×; Recommendation: (1) upgrade Partner A to Gold, (2) resend ICP card to Partner C with AZ-specific focus, (3) investigate Partner B's close rate — 62 days is too slow
2. **Edge case:** Partner-attributed pipeline is high but very little has closed → two common causes: (1) long sales cycles mean the pipeline is real but hasn't converted yet — look at vintage; (2) partner is referring unqualified prospects who stall in pipeline and never close — look at the referral-to-qualified rate; if vintage is <90 days in a 120-day average sales cycle, the pipeline may be healthy; if vintage is >6 months with no movement, it's a quality problem; don't confuse the two
3. **Adversarial:** A partner disputes the pipeline numbers — they claim they referred clients not in our CRM → this is an attribution data quality problem; every referral should be submitted through a documented process (form, email, CRM entry) that creates a timestamp; any referral not submitted through the official process is not attributable; this is why the referral submission process must be clear and the acknowledgment must be immediate — so partners know the referral was received and recorded; build the audit trail first, then dispute resolution is easy

## Audit Log
Pipeline analysis reports retained by period. Partner attribution data retained. Fee payment records maintained. ROI calculations documented. Matt Mathison reviews logged.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have CRM-integrated partner pipeline dashboards that generate these analyses automatically on a scheduled basis.
