# BD Contract Template Builder

Build a business development partnership contract template — a starting-point agreement for a specific partnership type (referral, co-sell, reseller, strategic) that can be used for all future partnerships of that type, reducing legal cost and negotiation time. Having a standard template creates predictability, protects company interests consistently, and signals professionalism to partners. Without a template, every partnership agreement is negotiated from scratch — expensive, time-consuming, and creates inconsistent protections across the partnership portfolio.

**Critical:** This skill produces a template structure and key term recommendations. All final agreements must be reviewed and approved by legal counsel before use. This skill does not produce legally binding agreements.

## When to Use
- Building the first referral agreement for a new partner program
- The company has been using informal "handshake" agreements and needs to formalize
- Scaling a partnership program that needs standardized agreements to process quickly
- A partner requests an agreement and we don't have one ready
- Creating a standard starting point that legal counsel can finalize

## Agreement Types

```
Four standard BD agreement types:

1. Referral agreement: Partner introduces prospects; we close; partner earns referral fee.
   Most common. Simplest structure.

2. Co-sell agreement: Partner actively participates in the sales process alongside us.
   Requires role definitions and attribution clarity.

3. Reseller agreement: Partner purchases and resells our product/service to their customers.
   Requires pricing floors, minimum commitments, and quality standards.

4. Strategic partnership agreement: High-value relationship with mutual obligations.
   Most complex; often includes co-marketing, data sharing, and joint planning requirements.
```

## Output Format

```markdown
# Partnership Agreement Template — <Company Name>
**Agreement type:** Referral / Co-sell / Reseller / Strategic
**Version:** 1.0
**Prepared by:** Dr. John Lewis | **Legal review required before use**
**Governing law:** [State]

---

## REFERRAL AGREEMENT TEMPLATE

---

**REFERRAL AGREEMENT**

This Referral Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") between:

**[COMPANY FULL LEGAL NAME]**, a [State] [entity type] ("Company"), and
**[PARTNER FULL LEGAL NAME]**, a [State] [entity type] ("Partner").

---

### 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Qualified Referral"** means an introduction by Partner to a prospective customer of Company where: (a) Partner has had at least one substantive conversation with the prospective customer regarding the prospective customer's potential need for Company's services; (b) the prospective customer has not previously been identified as a prospect in Company's CRM system at the time of submission; and (c) the referral is submitted through the official referral submission process described in Section 3.1.

**1.2 "Referral Fee"** means the fee payable to Partner upon the occurrence of the conditions described in Section 4.

**1.3 "Contract Value"** means the total annual fees payable by the referred customer under the first 12 months of a signed agreement with Company.

**1.4 "Referred Customer"** means a customer who was introduced to Company by Partner through a Qualified Referral and who subsequently enters into a paid agreement with Company.

---

### 2. PARTNER OBLIGATIONS

**2.1** Partner will identify prospective customers who may benefit from Company's services.

**2.2** Partner will submit Qualified Referrals through the official referral submission process (Section 3.1).

**2.3** Partner will accurately represent Company's services. Partner shall not make representations about Company's services that exceed those described in Company's official materials. If uncertain, Partner will direct prospective customers to Company for specific questions about features, pricing, or capabilities.

**2.4** Partner will complete any reasonable training requested by Company relating to Company's services, target customer profile, and referral process.

---

### 3. COMPANY OBLIGATIONS

**3.1 Referral submission process.** Company will maintain a referral submission method (currently: email to [ADDRESS]). Company will acknowledge receipt of each submission within [2] business days.

**3.2 Prospect follow-up.** Company will contact each Qualified Referral within [2] business days of submission acknowledgment.

**3.3 Status updates.** Company will provide Partner with a monthly status update on all active Qualified Referrals.

**3.4 Fee payment.** Company will pay Referral Fees in accordance with Section 4.

---

### 4. REFERRAL FEES

**4.1 Fee rate.** Company will pay Partner a Referral Fee of **[X%]** of the Contract Value for each Referred Customer.

**4.2 Payment conditions.** Referral Fees are payable only upon: (a) execution of a signed agreement between Company and the Referred Customer; and (b) receipt of the Referred Customer's first payment.

**4.3 Payment timing.** Company will pay Referral Fees within **[30] calendar days** of satisfaction of Section 4.2 conditions.

**4.4 Payment method.** Payment will be made by [ACH / check / other] to the account designated by Partner.

**4.5 No minimum guarantee.** Company does not guarantee any minimum number of customers, revenue, or Referral Fees.

**4.6 Attribution.** If a prospective customer was already in Company's CRM prior to Partner's submission, no Referral Fee is owed for that prospect. In the event of a dispute regarding attribution, Company's CRM records shall be the controlling evidence, subject to Section 9.

---

### 5. EXCLUSIVITY

**5.1** This Agreement is **non-exclusive**. Company may enter into similar referral agreements with other parties. Partner may refer prospective customers to other companies, including competitors of Company, provided Partner does not share any Confidential Information of Company with such competitors.

---

### 6. TERM AND TERMINATION

**6.1 Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for **12 months** unless earlier terminated ("Initial Term"). This Agreement will automatically renew for successive 12-month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **30 days** prior to the end of the then-current term.

**6.2 Termination for convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon **30 days** written notice to the other party.

**6.3 Termination for cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within 10 days of written notice; (b) becomes insolvent or makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (c) engages in conduct that violates applicable law.

**6.4 Effect of termination.** Upon termination: (a) Partner will cease submitting Qualified Referrals; (b) Referral Fees are owed for any Qualified Referral submitted prior to the termination date where the referred customer executes an agreement with Company within **90 days** of the termination date; (c) each party will return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information upon request.

---

### 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

**7.1** Each party agrees to keep confidential all non-public information received from the other party ("Confidential Information") and will use such information only in connection with this Agreement. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for **3 years**.

**7.2** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was known to the receiving party prior to disclosure; (c) is required to be disclosed by applicable law.

---

### 8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

**8.1 Partner represents** that: (a) it has full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) its performance under this Agreement will not violate any applicable law or third-party agreement.

**8.2 Company represents** that: (a) it has full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) it owns or has rights to all materials it provides to Partner.

**8.3 No medical or legal advice.** This Agreement does not authorize either party to provide medical, legal, financial, or regulatory advice on behalf of the other.

---

### 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**9.1** The parties will first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith negotiation. If unresolved within **30 days**, disputes will be submitted to binding arbitration in [City, State] under the rules of [AAA / JAMS]. The prevailing party is entitled to reasonable attorneys' fees.

---

### 10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

**10.1** IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL REFERRAL FEES PAID TO PARTNER IN THE [12] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

---

### 11. GENERAL

**11.1 Governing law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of [State].
**11.2 Entire agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions relating to the subject matter hereof.
**11.3 Amendments.** Amendments must be in writing signed by both parties.
**11.4 Independent contractors.** The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, agency, or partnership relationship.
**11.5 Assignment.** Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent, except in connection with a merger or acquisition of all or substantially all of the assigning party's assets.

---

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties execute this Agreement as of the date first written above.

**[COMPANY NAME]**
By: ___________________________
Name: Dr. John Lewis
Title: Director of AI Strategy, MBL Partners
Date: ___________________________

**[PARTNER NAME]**
By: ___________________________
Name: ___________________________
Title: ___________________________
Date: ___________________________
```

## Output Contract
- Legal review required — always stated; this template is a starting point for legal review, not a final document; any agreement sent to a partner without legal review exposes the company to unintended liability
- Standard protections always included — the template includes: attribution definition (avoiding disputes about who found whom first), in-flight referral protection (post-termination window), no minimum guarantee (avoiding committed liability), liability cap (protecting the company), non-exclusivity (preserving flexibility)
- Definitions section always precise — undefined terms are the most common source of partner disputes; "qualified referral," "contract value," and "referred customer" must be precisely defined in every referral agreement
- Post-termination referral window always explicit — a 90-day post-termination window for referrals submitted before termination is standard and partner-friendly; if it's missing, a partner who terminates loses all in-flight referrals overnight, creating resentment and disputes
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis builds template; legal counsel reviews and approves template before first use; Matt Mathison approves any non-standard fee rates in the template; legal must review every actual agreement before signing even if it uses this template

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio needs a referral agreement template for healthcare consulting partners → Template built using this skill; submitted to healthcare counsel for review; 3 modifications from counsel: (1) added anti-kickback statute compliance language specific to healthcare referrals; (2) added HIPAA data handling clause; (3) modified governing law to Arizona; approved template in use within 2 weeks; 5 partners signed within 30 days using the standard template; zero disputes in first 6 months
2. **Edge case:** A partner asks for changes to the standard template — specifically they want to remove the liability cap → route to legal immediately; liability cap removal is a significant change; legal will evaluate and likely propose a mutual cap increase rather than removal; do not agree to template modifications without legal sign-off; document all proposed modifications in writing before the call with legal
3. **Adversarial:** A partner signs the agreement but later claims that a verbal promise made during the negotiation overrides the written agreement → Section 11.2 (entire agreement clause) addresses this: "This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions"; document this in the response to the partner; if the verbal promise was actually made (and it shouldn't have been — see the negotiation prep skill), work with legal to determine whether to honor it as a commercial matter or stand on the contract
