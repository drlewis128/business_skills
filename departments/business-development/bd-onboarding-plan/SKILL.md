# BD Onboarding Plan

Design a 90-day onboarding plan for a new Business Development or partnerships team member — structured to get them from Day 1 to productive contributor as fast as possible. BD onboarding is different from other function onboarding because the new person's early impression of the company shapes their effectiveness for years: a BD person who doesn't deeply understand the product, the customers, and the partner ecosystem will make relationship-building mistakes that are hard to walk back.

## When to Use
- A new BD or partnerships team member starts
- A BD role is being hired and the onboarding plan needs to be ready before Day 1
- A portfolio company is scaling their BD function and needs a standard onboarding framework
- The current onboarding is ad-hoc and needs to be formalized

## Onboarding Design Principles

```
BD onboarding in three phases:

Phase 1 — Learn (Days 1-30): Listen, observe, read. Don't make changes.
  The new BD person should be absorbing, not acting.
  They need to understand the company, the customers, and the partners
  before they have an opinion about how to improve anything.

Phase 2 — Connect (Days 31-60): Build relationships, make introductions, understand the partner landscape.
  They should meet every current partner, key internal stakeholders, and as many customers as possible.
  Passive learning → Active conversation.

Phase 3 — Contribute (Days 61-90): Begin generating results.
  First partnership prospect identified and approached.
  First partner QBR completed.
  First OKR contribution visible.

Rule: A BD person who starts "fixing things" in Week 1 is making changes
before they understand the system. "Learn, don't change" for 30 days minimum.
```

## 90-Day Review

**Format:** 60-minute 1-on-1 with Dr. Lewis + CEO
**Questions:**
- What are you most proud of in your first 90 days?
- What's been harder than you expected?
- What would you change about how you've been onboarded?
- What do you need from Dr. Lewis and the team to be most effective in the next 90 days?
- What's your #1 priority for Q[N+1]?

**Dr. Lewis and CEO evaluate:**
- Is this the right person for this role?
- What coaching is needed?
- Are they on track to be fully independent by Month 6?

## Output Contract
- "Learn, don't change" principle always enforced for Phase 1 — a BD person who starts suggesting changes to the partner program in Week 1 is making recommendations without context; Phase 1 is explicitly learning-only; the Day 30 "First Observations" document is the first formal channel for suggestions
- Day 1 access always complete — a BD person who doesn't have CRM, email, and SharePoint access on Day 1 starts the relationship with the impression that the company wasn't prepared; all access must be requested before the start date and confirmed complete on Day 1
- 90-day review always scheduled before Day 1 — the 90-day review should be on the calendar from the first day; it signals that there is a structured evaluation process and that the relationship is managed, not just assumed
- First BD contribution by Day 90 — not by Day 30 or 60; a BD person who hasn't had time to learn the business should not be generating (and possibly distorting) pipeline; the first contribution goal is deliberately set at Day 90 to give full Phase 1 and 2 time to contextualize their work
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis designs and oversees onboarding; CEO leads the product deep dive session (Day 2-3) and co-leads the 90-day review; any BD hire whose 90-day review identifies a performance concern requires Matt Mathison awareness

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Existing partner agreements, BD OKR history, ecosystem map, partner health dashboard, customer case studies
- **Writes to:** Onboarding plan (SharePoint/HR/<Company>/Onboarding); Day 30 observations document; 90-day review notes
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis designs; CEO contributes product session and 90-day review; Matt Mathison notified of 90-day concerns

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio hires a BD Manager → Onboarding plan built and shared on hiring offer date; Day 1: all access working; Day 2-3: CEO-led product deep dive with a live demo of the RCM platform; Day 5: reviewed all 6 existing partner agreements; Week 3: attended 3 customer onboarding calls (listener only); Day 30 observations: "Partner agreements are very consistent — good foundation; the biggest gap I see is that 2 active partners haven't had a check-in in 90+ days"; Day 45: led first partner check-in independently; Day 60: first outreach to 3 ecosystem prospects; Day 90: 2 prospects at Stage 3, 1 QBR completed, Q3 OKR draft submitted; 90-day review: fully on track; minor coaching: "slow down slightly — make sure you understand why a prospect is a fit before the discovery call"
2. **Edge case:** New BD person wants to start prospecting in Week 1 (impatient, high energy) → redirect respectfully: "We want to put you in a position to be most effective when you do prospect — that means understanding the product and the partners first. Your instinct to move fast is right; let's make sure the speed is aimed correctly. You'll be ready to prospect by Day 60 with much higher quality context." If they continue to push, it's a coaching moment about discipline and respecting the learning process
3. **Adversarial:** At the 90-day review, the new BD person's first 2 prospecting outreaches have gotten no response → investigate the cause before making a judgment: was the outreach to high-quality targets? Was the message compelling? Was the follow-up done correctly? New BD people often struggle with outreach framing — they use language that sounds corporate rather than personal; review the outreach drafts; coach specifically; don't conclude "this person can't prospect" from a sample size of 2 attempts

## Audit Log
Onboarding plans retained by hire and date. Day 30 observations documented. 90-day review outcomes retained. Performance concerns escalated to Matt Mathison documented.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have an HR/People function that owns structured onboarding for all roles with a standard 90-day framework and manager-guided curriculum.
