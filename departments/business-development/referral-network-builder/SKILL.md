# Referral Network Builder

Build and cultivate a network of referral relationships — people and organizations who informally refer business to the company, not under a formal partner agreement but through trust-based professional relationships. The referral network is broader than the formal partner program: it includes customers who mention the company to peers, advisors who recommend the company without a fee structure, former customers, investors, lawyers, accountants, and other professionals who touch the same customers and refer without an economic incentive.

## When to Use
- Building the company's informal referral relationships beyond the formal partner program
- Identifying who in MBL's professional network is already a referral source
- Cultivating strategic relationships with advisors, lawyers, accountants, and other professionals who refer informally
- A customer or contact says "I'll mention you to a few people" — structure and follow up on that conversation
- Matt Mathison wants to understand the full picture of how business is being referred

## Referral Network vs. Formal Partner Program

```
Referral network (informal) vs. partner program (formal):

Referral network:
  - No formal agreement required
  - No referral fee (reciprocal value instead)
  - Based on trust and relationship — not tracked in the same way
  - Includes: customers, investors, advisors, lawyers, accountants, friends
  - Activated by: relationship investment, visibility, staying top-of-mind
  - Scale: unlimited — many people refer informally

Partner program (formal):
  - Formal agreement required
  - Referral fee paid on conversion
  - Tracked in CRM with deal registration
  - Includes: dedicated referral partners
  - Activated by: economic incentive + enablement
  - Scale: limited by management bandwidth

Both complement each other. The referral network is the broader ecosystem;
the partner program is the formalized, highest-volume subset of it.
```

## Referral Activation Strategies

### Strategy 1: Ask happy customers
**Who:** Customers at 90+ days post-onboarding with positive outcomes
**How:** "One thing that helps us grow is when clients like you mention us to others who might benefit. Is there anyone in your network who faces similar [billing challenges / business problems] that you'd feel comfortable introducing us to?"
**Frequency:** At 90-day check-in; at contract renewal; after a major win
**Expected yield:** 1-2 introductions per 10 satisfied customers asked

### Strategy 2: Invest in professional advisors (CPAs, lawyers, bankers)
**Who:** Professionals who touch the same customers — high trust, advisory relationship
**How:** (a) Offer a formal referral relationship with an incentive; OR (b) offer reciprocal introductions ("I introduce you to my network, you introduce me to yours")
**Cadence:** Quarterly lunch or coffee + share relevant content monthly
**Expected yield:** 3-6 introductions per year from an engaged professional advisor

### Strategy 3: Stay visible at events
**Who:** Industry events, trade association meetings, professional networking events
**How:** Attend + follow up within 48 hours with every meaningful conversation; connect on LinkedIn; send one relevant resource within 1 week
**Expected yield:** 1-3 introductions per quarter from sustained event presence

### Strategy 4: Activate Matt Mathison's network
**Who:** Matt Mathison's personal and professional network (investors, advisors, portfolio contacts)
**How:** Matt Mathison makes warm introductions; Dr. Lewis follows up and develops the relationship
**Expected yield:** 5-10 high-quality introductions per year (Matt Mathison's relationships tend to be strategic and high-trust)
**Process:** Dr. Lewis identifies high-value targets → Matt Mathison reviews + approves introductions → Matt Mathison makes intro → Dr. Lewis handles follow-up

## Output Contract
- Informal referral network always distinguished from formal partner program — conflating the two causes under-investment in the informal network (no fee needed, so it gets less attention) and over-complication of formal partner management; they are separate strategies with different activation mechanisms
- Ask strategy always explicit — the most common reason happy customers don't refer is that no one asked them; "ask happy customers" must include a specific script, a specific trigger moment, and a specific frequency; not just "leverage customer relationships"
- Professional advisor cultivation always includes reciprocity — CPAs, attorneys, and bankers who refer informally are motivated by the relationship, not just the introduction; the cultivation plan must offer something in return (introductions, referrals back, educational content, relevant client invitations); purely transactional cultivation of professional advisors doesn't work
- Matt Mathison network activation always structured — Matt Mathison's network is one of MBL's highest-value BD assets; it must be used deliberately, not ad hoc; Dr. Lewis identifies targets, Matt Mathison approves and makes introductions, Dr. Lewis develops and tracks the relationship; no cold use of Matt Mathison's name without his knowledge
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis builds and manages the referral network; Matt Mathison co-manages his own network contacts — no introduction made using Matt Mathison's name or relationship without his approval; referral network contacts who become formal partners must go through the standard partner agreement process with legal review

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** CRM (customer list, contact history), Matt Mathison's network (by consultation), event calendars, trade association member lists
- **Writes to:** Referral network tracker (SharePoint/BD/Referral Network); CRM (referral network contacts); cultivation calendar
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis manages; Matt Mathison approves use of his name/relationships; formal partner conversions require legal review; any introduction that becomes a formal partner goes through the standard partner onboarding process

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio builds a referral network of 25 informal referral sources → Top 5: 3 happy customers (asked at 90-day mark, all agreed to refer 1-2 people), 1 CPA who serves physician practices in Phoenix (quarterly lunch + reciprocal introductions), 1 AZ Medical Association committee member (event attendance, co-authored piece in association newsletter); Introductions from network: 8 in Q2; 2 converted to customers ($32K combined); 1 is now in formal partner program (signed referral agreement); Matt Mathison activated 2 introductions from his investor network in Q2 both of which are in active conversation; Total referral network pipeline: $55K
2. **Edge case:** A customer refers a prospect but asks to be kept anonymous ("don't tell them it was me who sent you") → honor this; approach the prospect with "I came across your practice through someone in your professional network" — don't lie (don't say "I found you through LinkedIn" if that's not true), but you can decline to name the source; if the anonymous referral is important to the referral source's business relationships (e.g., a CPA who doesn't want their clients to know they recommended a third-party service), respect the request fully
3. **Adversarial:** A contact in the referral network starts asking for compensation for their introductions (even though they're an informal network contact, not a formal partner) → this is a signal that they're ready to become a formal partner; evaluate whether to formalize the relationship with a referral agreement; if yes, process through the standard partner program (qualification, agreement, legal review); if no (they don't meet the partner qualifications), have an honest conversation about why the relationship can't include direct compensation; informal referral sources cannot be paid ad-hoc without creating de facto employment relationships or anti-kickback concerns (especially in healthcare)

## Audit Log
Referral network contacts logged with relationship status and last contact date. Introductions received and source documented. Conversions from informal referral network tracked. Matt Mathison network activations logged. Formal partner conversions from network documented.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have full-time relationship managers who maintain the referral network as a dedicated function with a CRM-integrated cultivation cadence.
