# Partner Recruitment Plan

Build a systematic plan to find, approach, and sign new partners — going beyond waiting for inbound inquiries to proactively building a partner pipeline. Partner programs that only grow through inbound inquiries grow slowly and attract the wrong partners (those who are actively looking for partnerships, not those who are the best fit). A proactive recruitment plan identifies the right partners first, then approaches them with a compelling reason to partner.

## When to Use
- The partner program has fewer partners than needed to hit revenue goals
- Most new partners come from inbound — no systematic outbound prospecting
- Building a partner program from scratch and need to identify first partners
- A new market or geography is being entered and needs partner coverage
- Matt Mathison asks "how are we growing the partner network?"

## Recruitment Framework

```
Partner recruitment has three phases:

1. Identify: Who are the right partners? (Ecosystem map → target list)
   Source from: industry directories, conference exhibitor lists, trade associations,
   LinkedIn, referrals from existing partners, referrals from customers ("who else do you work with?")

2. Qualify: Are they worth pursuing? (Scorecard before outreach)
   Don't spend outreach resources on unqualified prospects.
   Quick pre-qualification can be done in 30 minutes from public information.

3. Engage: Outreach that opens conversations.
   The goal of the first contact is not to sign a partner agreement.
   It's to get a 30-minute exploratory call.
```

## Outreach Sequence

### Step 1: Pre-qualification (before reaching out)
Spend 30 minutes on public information:
- Their website: who they serve, how they describe their business
- LinkedIn: company size, team, who's posting content
- Existing customer/partner references: do any current relationships vouch for them?
- Score ≥60 on the partner qualification scorecard → proceed to outreach
- Score <60 → add to watch list, don't spend outreach time yet

### Step 2: First outreach (email or LinkedIn)
**Subject:** Introduction — [Referrer name] suggested I reach out / Potential partnership for [their customer type]

**Opening:** [Warm lead: "John suggested we connect." | Cold lead: "I came across [Company] through [association/conference] — you're clearly serving the same independent practices we work with at [Company]."]

**Value prop:** "We work with [description]. We're not a competitor — we [specific, non-overlapping service]. We've been exploring partnerships with [their type of company] because we find that [their customers] often need both what we do and what you do."

**Ask:** "Would you have 20 minutes in the next few weeks to explore whether there's a fit? No agenda other than getting to know each other."

**Do not:** Attach a 10-page partner deck. Do not explain the referral economics in the first message. Do not ask them to sign anything.

### Step 3: Follow-up sequence
- Day 1: First outreach (email or LinkedIn)
- Day 5: Follow-up if no response ("Wanted to make sure this didn't fall through the cracks")
- Day 12: Final attempt if no response ("I'll take the no — just wanted to make sure you saw this")
- Day 12+: Move to long-term nurture (quarterly check-in, share relevant content)

### Step 4: Discovery call (20-30 min)
Goal: learn about them, not pitch them.
- "Tell me about your client base — what's the typical profile?"
- "What do your clients most commonly come to you with?"
- "Have you worked with referral partners before? What worked and what didn't?"
- Share: "Here's what we do and who we serve." — 3 minutes max.
- Close with: "Based on what you've shared, I think there could be something here. Would you be willing to do a second call where I walk you through the partnership model specifically?"

## Output Contract
- Ideal partner profile always explicit — an outreach campaign without a precise target profile generates noise; the profile defines who gets an outreach and who doesn't; it must be specific enough to look at any company and say "yes" or "no" in 5 minutes
- Sourcing channels prioritized — the most effective sourcing channels for referral partners are almost always: (1) existing customer referrals, (2) existing partner referrals, (3) trade associations; outbound prospecting is less efficient but necessary when the above channels are exhausted; prioritize accordingly
- Outreach copy always conservative in the first message — the first message's only job is to earn a call; never send partner economics, legal terms, or a long deck in the first message; one message = one ask (a call)
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis designs and executes the recruitment plan; Matt Mathison reviews the ideal partner profile before outreach begins; any outreach to a company where Matt has a personal relationship requires his coordination; partner agreements require legal review and Matt Mathison approval

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Ecosystem map, partner qualification scorecard, CRM, trade association directories, LinkedIn
- **Writes to:** Partner prospect pipeline (CRM); outreach tracker; partnership pipeline report
- **HITL Required:** Dr. Lewis leads recruitment; Matt Mathison reviews ideal partner profile; Matt Mathison coordinates if he has existing relationship with prospect; legal reviews all partner agreements

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio recruits 5 new referral partners in Q3 → Ideal profile: Arizona-based healthcare advisors serving 10-50 physician practices; Sources: (1) Arizona Medical Association member directory (200 relevant members), (2) existing Allevio customers asked at renewal ("who else advises your practice?"); Prospect list: 18 qualified leads; Outreach: 12 first messages sent; 6 discovery calls completed; 3 passed qualification; 2 partnership agreements signed within 60 days; 1 still in process; first referral from new partner within 45 days of signing; outcome: 2 new signed partners, $0 in first 30 days, $45K in pipeline within 90 days
2. **Edge case:** Outreach gets no response after 3 attempts → don't interpret silence as a "no"; 30-40% of non-responses are timing issues (the person is busy, the email got buried); for high-priority targets, try a different channel after 2 weeks (if you emailed, try LinkedIn; if LinkedIn, try email); for top-10 targets, consider asking for a warm introduction from a mutual contact before giving up; after 3 multi-channel attempts with no response, move to quarterly nurture (share a relevant article every 3 months until they're ready)
3. **Adversarial:** A prospect agrees to a discovery call and then uses it as an opportunity to pitch their services to us instead of exploring a mutual partnership → redirect immediately: "I'd love to hear more about what you offer — can we set a separate call for that? Today I wanted to focus on [mutual customers] and whether there's a referral relationship to explore"; if they continue to make the call one-directional, it may signal they're not actually interested in partnership — log the interaction and deprioritize

## Audit Log
Prospect list retained with outreach dates and outcomes. Discovery call notes retained. Partnership offers made and outcomes logged. Signed partner agreements retained with origination source.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have dedicated partner development reps who own outbound partner prospecting as a standing BD function.
