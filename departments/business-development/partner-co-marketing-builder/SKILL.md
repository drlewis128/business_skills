# Partner Co-Marketing Builder

Build a co-marketing campaign with a partner — joint content, co-branded assets, and coordinated marketing activities that reach each other's audiences. Co-marketing is one of the highest-leverage partnership activities: it extends reach into each other's existing networks without requiring either party to build that audience from scratch. The key discipline is ensuring the joint campaign produces measurable leads, not just brand awareness that neither party can track.

## When to Use
- A partner has a relevant audience and wants to create content or campaigns together
- Launching a new product or service and a partner's endorsement would accelerate awareness
- A conference or industry event creates a co-marketing opportunity
- A mutual customer success story would make a compelling co-branded case study
- Partners who are already referring want to deepen the relationship through joint visibility

## Co-Marketing Campaign Types

```
Five co-marketing formats and when to use each:

1. Joint webinar: Best for audience engagement + lead generation.
   Both companies present; both promote to their lists.
   Works when both audiences are in the same market.
   Requires: 6-8 weeks lead time. Expected: 50-200 attendees, 10-25% lead conversion.

2. Co-authored content: Blog post, guide, whitepaper, or article.
   Lower production effort; longer shelf life than events.
   Works when both companies have domain expertise that is genuinely complementary.
   Requires: 3-4 weeks. Expected: 200-2,000 reads; nurture leads over 90 days.

3. Co-branded case study: Mutual client success story with both brands.
   Highest credibility; requires customer consent.
   Works when a shared customer has a compelling outcome from using both solutions.
   Requires: Customer consent + 4-6 weeks. Expected: High-quality sales tool, low volume.

4. Joint email campaign: Both companies email their respective lists promoting a joint offer.
   Fast to execute; immediate pipeline signal.
   Works when offer is relevant to both audiences.
   Requires: List consent review + GDPR/CAN-SPAM compliance + 2-4 weeks.
   Expected: 1-3% lead conversion on combined list.

5. Social media coordination: Coordinated posts, amplification, and mentions.
   Lowest cost; lowest barrier; lowest impact as a standalone.
   Best as a supplement to the above, not a primary campaign.
```

## Output Contract
- Lead routing always defined before launch — co-marketing campaigns where lead routing isn't agreed upfront result in conflicts over who owns which registrant; routing must be documented and both parties must agree before the promotion goes live
- Budget always proportional and approved — "we'll split it 50/50" without calculating proportional benefit creates resentment when one party clearly benefits more; calculate which party benefits more from the campaign and propose proportional investment; any joint budget >$2,500 requires Matt Mathison approval
- Compliance always checked for email campaigns — joint email campaigns must comply with CAN-SPAM (correct unsubscribe, sender identification) and any relevant privacy law (GDPR if EU contacts, HIPAA if healthcare context); never add one party's contacts to the other party's list without explicit consent
- Post-campaign review always scheduled — co-marketing campaigns with no post-campaign review never improve; the 2-week review is what produces the institutional learning that makes the second campaign better than the first
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis coordinates; CEO review of any co-branded materials before launch; Matt Mathison approves budgets >$2,500; legal/compliance review for email campaigns and any regulated-industry co-marketing; healthcare co-marketing (Allevio) must be reviewed for HIPAA compliance and FTC advertising rules before distribution

- **Reads from:** Partner's audience size, both companies' content library, email list data, brand guides

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio + EMR partner co-host webinar "The AZ Independent Practice Tech Stack for 2027" → 6-week production; 180 registrants (both promoted to combined list of ~600 relevant contacts); 145 attended live; 35 post-event recordings; 18 qualified leads for Allevio; 12 qualified leads for EMR partner; 4 interested in both (joint intro call scheduled); 90-day pipeline from webinar: $95K for Allevio; partner reports similar pipeline; post-campaign review: topic resonated, production was easy, recommend quarterly cadence; total cost: $1,200 for webinar platform + promotion (both shared 50/50); ROI: ~80× on joint investment; Matt Mathison approved the $600 investment
2. **Edge case:** A co-branded case study requires a mutual client's written consent and the client doesn't want to be named → explore alternatives: (1) anonymous case study with approval ("A 6-physician AZ practice reduced billing errors by 38% using Allevio + [EMR Partner]"); (2) video testimonial (client decides their own comfort level); (3) reference call (client agrees to speak with prospects but not be published); never publish a case study without written consent — the client relationship is more valuable than any marketing asset
3. **Adversarial:** A partner uses co-branded materials after a campaign ends or after the partnership is terminated → co-branded assets must have a defined shelf life; the partner agreement should specify that co-branded materials may only be used during the term of the partnership; upon termination, partner must remove all co-branded materials within [30] days; this is a brand protection issue; enforce it with a specific written demand and follow up; if the partner doesn't comply, route to legal

## Audit Log
Co-marketing campaign briefs retained. Campaign performance metrics logged. Lead routing records maintained. Co-branded asset usage rights documented. Budget approvals retained. Partner compliance with post-termination material removal logged.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have dedicated partner marketing coordinators who own co-marketing as a standing program function with templated campaign frameworks.
