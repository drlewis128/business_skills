# Partner Onboarding Program

Design the onboarding experience for new partners — the structured process from signed agreement to first referral or revenue. Partner onboarding is where most partnership programs fail: the agreement is signed with great enthusiasm, and then the partner never activates because they weren't given the tools, training, or incentive to actually start. A great onboarding program produces first revenue within 60 days.

## When to Use
- A new partner agreement has been signed and the partner needs to be activated
- Building a scalable partner onboarding process for a growing partner program
- A signed partner has gone quiet and needs re-activation
- Designing a partner launch kit for a new partnership program

## Onboarding Principles

```
Partner onboarding success factors:

1. Time to first value: Can the partner make their first referral within 30 days?
   Everything in onboarding should remove friction from the first referral.

2. Economic reminder: Partners sign agreements, then forget why. 
   Onboarding must re-sell the economics: "Here's exactly how much you'll earn 
   when you refer a customer." Make it concrete and personal.

3. Simple story: Partners cannot sell what they can't explain in 30 seconds.
   The onboarding must produce a 30-second partner pitch.

4. One clear first action: End every onboarding step with one specific next action.
   Not "start referring" — "email your next 3 clients with this template."

5. Early win: The partner needs to succeed quickly. 
   The first referral (even if small) validates the partnership and builds momentum.
```

## Partner Onboarding Kit

Everything a new partner needs, in one package:

### 1. Partner Welcome Letter
> "Welcome to the [Company] partner program. We're excited to work with you. Here's everything you need to make our partnership successful from day one."

### 2. The 30-Second Partner Pitch
*Exactly what to say when introducing [Company] to a prospect:*

> "[Company] helps [specific customer type] [specific problem they solve] — typically [key outcome: reducing costs/improving X by X%]. I refer them because [personal reason — they've delivered for my clients]. If any of your clients are dealing with [specific trigger], I'd be happy to make an introduction."

**Practice note:** The partner must be able to say this without referring to notes.

### 3. Economics Reference Card

> "Here's exactly what you earn when you refer a client:"
> 
> | Referral outcome | Your earning |
> |-----------------|-------------|
> | Signed contract (Year 1 value $X-$Y) | $[Z] referral fee |
> | Signed contract (Year 1 value $Y-$W) | $[A] referral fee |
> | Fee payment timing | [30 / 60 days after contract signed] |
> | How to submit a referral | [Specific form/link/email] |
> | Referral tracking | [How they see the status of their referrals] |

### 4. Target Client Profile (1 page)
*Who is the ideal customer to refer?*

| The best referral is... | The worst referral is... |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| [Specific profile — size, geography, situation] | [Who NOT to refer — saves everyone time] |
| [Specific trigger situation] | [Not-a-fit situations] |

### 5. Referral Process (Step-by-Step)
1. Identify a client who matches the target profile
2. Introduce [Company] to your client: use the 30-second pitch or the email template below
3. If they're interested, submit the referral: [specific link/email/form]
4. We will contact your client within [N] business days
5. We'll keep you updated on the status via [method]
6. When the contract is signed, you receive your referral fee within [N] days

### 6. Email Introduction Template
*Copy and send — customize with [brackets]:*

> Subject: Introduction — [Your Name] → [Contact] at [Company]
> 
> Hi [Client first name],
> 
> I wanted to introduce you to [Contact name] at [Company]. [Company] helps [description] and I thought of you because [specific reason].
> 
> [Contact], [Client name] is [brief intro].
> 
> I'll let you two take it from here — happy to reconnect if you have questions.
> 
> Best,
> [Partner name]

### 7. Partner FAQ
| Question | Answer |
|---------|--------|
| What if my client already knows [Company]? | [Policy — if they're not a current customer, you get credit] |
| What if my referral doesn't close? | [Policy — you earn fee only on signed contracts] |
| Who do I contact if I have questions? | [Dr. Lewis contact + response time commitment] |
| How do I check the status of my referrals? | [Process] |

## Kickoff Call Agenda (Day 5, 30 minutes)

| Topic | Time | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Welcome + why we're excited to work together | 3 min | |
| Economics review — make it personal | 5 min | "If you refer 3 clients this quarter, here's what that looks like for you" |
| The 30-second pitch — practice together | 5 min | Have them say it; give feedback |
| First target identification | 10 min | "Which 3 of your clients might need this today?" |
| Referral process walkthrough | 5 min | |
| Questions | 2 min | |

**Kickoff outcome:** Partner can identify their first 3 referral targets before the call ends.

---

## 30-Day Check-In (Day 30, 15 minutes)

| Question | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Have you made any introductions? If yes — what happened? | Gauge activation |
| What's getting in the way of making referrals? | Identify blockers |
| Is there anything unclear about the referral process? | Remove process friction |
| Do you have a client in mind right now? | Trigger immediate action |

If no referrals in first 30 days: identify the specific blocker and address it directly. The most common reasons: (1) they forgot, (2) they're not sure who to refer, (3) the pitch feels awkward. Each has a specific fix.

## Output Contract
- 30-second pitch always included — if the partner can't explain the product in 30 seconds, they won't use their relationships to refer; the onboarding kit must produce this pitch and the kickoff call must practice it
- Economics reference card always personal and specific — "you earn a fee on closed deals" is not good enough; "you earn $1,500 when a referral signs a contract worth $20K+" is good enough; the partner must be able to visualize their earnings
- First referral target identified on the kickoff call — the kickoff call ends when the partner has named at least 1 specific client they will approach; "I'll think about who to refer" is not an acceptable outcome
- Day 30 check-in is non-negotiable — partners who aren't checked on in the first 30 days go dark; the check-in must be calendared on the day the agreement is signed
- HITL required: Dr. Lewis manages partner onboarding; any partner-facing materials must be approved by the appropriate portfolio company CEO before distribution; referral agreements require legal review; any deviation from standard referral fee terms requires Matt Mathison approval

## System Dependencies
- **Reads from:** Partner agreement (signed), company pitch materials, target customer profile, economics model
- **Writes to:** Partner onboarding kit (SharePoint/BD/Partners/<Company>/Onboarding); kickoff notes; partner CRM
- **HITL Required:** CEO approves partner-facing materials; legal reviews any non-standard onboarding terms; Matt Mathison approves non-standard fee arrangements

## Test Cases
1. **Golden path:** Allevio onboards a physician consulting firm referral partner → Welcome email Day 1; kit sent: 30-second pitch, economics card (20% of Year 1 contract, paid at signature), target profile (independent practice 2-8 physicians, AZ, billing challenges), email template, FAQ; Day 5 kickoff call: practiced pitch (feedback: "too long — cut to 2 sentences"); partner identified 4 clients immediately; Day 10: first email introduction sent to 2 clients; Day 30 check-in: 1 referral in pipeline ($35K contract); Day 45: contract signed; referral fee: $7,000 paid within 30 days; partner activated successfully
2. **Edge case:** Partner completes onboarding but makes no referrals after 60 days → escalate: schedule a 1-on-1 call; find the specific blocker; if it's "I haven't found the right clients," do a joint session where Dr. Lewis reviews their client list and identifies referral candidates together; if it's "I've been too busy," set a specific action: "Can you make one introduction this week?"; if it's "I don't think it's the right fit for my clients," have an honest conversation about whether the partnership should continue
3. **Adversarial:** Partner starts making referrals but introduces the company incorrectly (overpromises capabilities) → catch this early in the first referral; debrief with the partner after the first introduction call; correct the pitch firmly but constructively; if it continues, require that all introductions go through a joint kickoff call with the end customer rather than independent partner introductions until trust is established

## Audit Log
All onboarding kits retained by partner and date. Kickoff call notes retained. 30-day check-in outcomes documented. First referral date logged. Revenue attribution tracked.

## Deprecation
Retire when portfolio companies have dedicated partner success managers who own partner onboarding and activation as a standard function.
